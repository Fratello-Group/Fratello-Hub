# CFIA — Gap Backlog (uncontemplated elements)

_Generated 2026-06-23 from a four-lens expert review (CFIA auditor · floor UX · engineering · manager ops). These are additions to the plan — not yet built. Prioritised within each lens._

> Foundation-affecting items to fold in BEFORE/DURING the form + scheduling build are tagged **[foundation]** in PLAN-OF-ATTACK.md.
**Totals: 26 High · 21 Medium · 4 Low.**

## CFIA / SFCR auditor

- **[High] A first-class CCP critical-limit monitoring + deviation/corrective-action record type, structured from the existing 11.3 CCP Summary Table (per-CCP: critical limit, measured value, in/out-of-limit flag, deviation, corrective action, product disposition). The roasting CCP (≥180°C probe), magnet/screen filtration, and destoning each need their own monitoring log with automatic out-of-limit flagging.**  
  _data-model_ — CCP monitoring records and documented deviations are the single most-scrutinized SFCR/HACCP evidence; the plan only builds Form 6.3a and treats every form as a generic schema, so a missed/out-of-limit CCP reading produces no flag and no linked corrective action — a direct audit failure.
- **[High] A CAPA (corrective/preventive action) register that any record can spawn and that links the trigger (failed CCP, complaint, hold, deviation, internal-audit finding) to root cause, action, owner, due date, and verified close-out — with overdue CAPAs surfacing on the QA/owner dashboards.**  
  _data-model_ — SFCR requires evidence that problems are not just recorded but driven to closure; right now an out-of-limit reading or complaint has nowhere to go and no accountable follow-through, which auditors read as an ineffective preventive-control system.
- **[High] A supplier-control module: approved-supplier list (from 2.2), with per-supplier Letters of Guarantee, allergen statements, and COAs stored as expiry-dated documents that auto-flag when lapsed or missing, plus the incoming-material receiving/inspection log (11.1c) tied to lot numbers.**  
  _data-model_ — The Allergens and Section 2 folders already hold dated supplier letters of guarantee and allergen statements that expire; with no expiry tracking the company can unknowingly receive against a lapsed guarantee — a documented SFCR incoming-input control gap.
- **[High] Lot/traceability linkage and a mock-recall execution workflow: capture green/roast/grind/pack lot numbers (Cropster PR codes per SOP 5.6) on records, and a mock-recall drill record (10.1a / Form E) that runs trace-forward/back and computes time-to-trace and % reconciled.**  
  _audit_ — SFCR Part 5 / s.89 mandates one-up/one-back traceability and Fratello already runs annual mock recalls; without lot capture and a recall workflow the module cannot demonstrate the 4-hour traceability and reconciliation an inspector will test.
- **[High] Equipment calibration & preventive-maintenance scheduling as its own scheduled record class: scale calibration log (7.2a, quarterly), temperature-probe verification (the device used to prove the roast CCP), magnet-integrity check (monthly), with due/overdue tracking and calibration certificates attached.**  
  _scheduling_ — A CCP measurement is only valid if the measuring device is calibrated; the scheduling engine currently knows daily/weekly/monthly/annual cadences but not calibration-due state, so an expired probe calibration silently invalidates every roast CCP record.
- **[High] An internal-audit and management-review scheduler tied to SOP 1.3 / Checklist 1.2a (annual program review, quarterly forms-table review): scheduled internal audits that produce findings, and a recurring management-review record capturing inputs (complaint trends, CAPA status, audit results, deviations) and decisions/sign-off.**  
  _scheduling_ — SFCR expects the PCP to be periodically reviewed for effectiveness and the forms table itself states it is reviewed quarterly under SOP 1.3; with no scheduled internal audit or management review the system has no evidence the program is being verified at the top level.
- **[Medium] Explicit, configurable record-retention and immutable-archive policy per record type (e.g., retain ≥2 years per SFCR; longer for training and CCP records), with no auto-deletion and an exportable, time-bounded audit pack.**  
  _audit_ — Records being immutable is necessary but not sufficient — SFCR requires defined retention periods and the ability to produce records on demand; an undefined retention/export story leaves a question an auditor will ask unanswered.
- **[Medium] A sanitation and pest-control verification record set: pre-op (6.3a, have), facility interior/exterior inspections (6.9/6.10 monthly), sanitation verification, glass-and-brittle-plastic breakage log (6.4), and pest-control reports (6.5) including third-party service reports with trending.**  
  _data-model_ — Sanitation and pest control are prerequisite programs every CFIA inspection covers; only the daily pre-op is built, leaving monthly sanitation/pest/glass-breakage verification with no system home and no overdue visibility.
- **[Medium] A complaint-and-hold workflow with trending: customer complaint intake (9.3a) feeding an immutable complaints log (9.3b) with category tagging and trend charts, plus product hold (9.4) and disposition (9.2) records that link held lots to their release/destruction decision.**  
  _data-model_ — SFCR requires complaints to be recorded, investigated, and trended for systemic issues, and held product to have a documented disposition; without trending and hold-to-disposition linkage the company cannot show it detects recurring problems or controls suspect product.
- **[Medium] Training-effectiveness evidence beyond the 90% quiz pass: store the linkage from each quiz item to the SOP/competency it verifies, plus a supervisor on-the-job competency sign-off (Onboard Training Record / 8.3a) so a passed quiz is backed by demonstrated practical competence.**  
  _training_ — Auditors increasingly ask not just whether training occurred but whether it was effective; a quiz score alone is thin evidence, and Fratello already has an onboard training record and acknowledgment checklist that should become the practical-competency half of the proof.
- **[Medium] A documented document-approval (change-control) workflow, not just version stamping: a draft → review → approve transition that records reviewer, approver, change reason/summary, and triggers re-acknowledgement, so superseded versions are retained and the approval event itself is an auditable record.**  
  _audit_ — SFCR document control (SOP 1.4) requires evidence of who approved each change and why; the plan stamps approvedBy/version but has no approval workflow or change-reason capture, so an auditor cannot see the controlled change happen — only its end state.
- **[Low] An allergen-control record layer specific to Fratello's flavoured/syrup inputs: allergen matrix per product/line, changeover-cleaning verification when switching to/from allergen-containing inputs, and label-allergen verification (SOP 3.3) recorded per production run.**  
  _data-model_ — The Allergens folder shows chocolate sauce, syrups, and barista products with declared allergens entering the facility; with no changeover or label-allergen verification record, cross-contact and mislabelling — a top recall cause — go undocumented.

## Production-floor UX (ESL staff, shared devices)

- **[High] Offline / weak-wifi capture: queue submissions locally (IndexedDB/service worker) and sync when connection returns, with a clear pending/synced badge per record**  
  _data-model_ — createRecord() in cfia-core.js calls addDoc directly — a dropped connection on the floor throws an error and the worker either loses the entry or re-enters everything; a daily food-safety log that silently fails to save is an audit gap, not just an annoyance
- **[High] Photo evidence field type in the form engine (camera capture → compressed image → Firebase Storage ref stored on the immutable record)**  
  _data-model_ — fh-render-form.js only supports date/text/select/textarea/status; a 'Fail' on pre-op inspection or a corrective action is far stronger evidence with a photo, and CFIA inspectors expect visual proof of conditions and remediation
- **[High] Multi-language UI (English/Spanish at minimum) — labels, help text, status options, error messages, and SOP summaries driven from the schema, with a persistent per-device language toggle**  
  _UX_ — Many floor staff are ESL/Spanish; today every label, the 'This record is permanent' warning, and validation errors are English-only, so workers either guess or skip, producing wrong/empty records that look completed
- **[High] Shared-station / kiosk mode: a fast 'Who are you?' staff picker (PIN or tap-a-face tile) instead of full Microsoft/Google/email re-login between workers, plus auto-return-to-home and no auto-logout mid-shift**  
  _UX_ — Locked decision says the real person changes themselves via the staff list OR a quick re-login, but only a free-text 'Completed by' box exists — on a shared tablet that means everything is attributed to whoever signed in first unless they retype names, undermining the unforgeable sign-off
- **[High] Bulk 'All Pass' / 'Mark all Not in use' action at the top of any status checklist, with each row still individually tappable to override**  
  _UX_ — Status fields render as one radio row per line item; a 20-line pre-op log tapped one box at a time is slow and pushes staff toward rushing or skipping — a default-all then exception-edit pattern matches how floors actually work and reduces missed rows
- **[High] Accidental-submit and double-submit safeguards: a review/confirm step (or hold-to-submit), idempotency on the create call, and a 'you already logged this form today' duplicate check**  
  _UX_ — Because records are immutable with no edit/delete, a fat-fingered or double-tapped submit creates a permanent wrong record that can only be superseded; on a shaky shared tablet this will happen and clutter the audit trail
- **[Medium] Big tap-target / glove-friendly floor theme: minimum ~48px targets, high-contrast Pass/Fail, large fonts, spacing tuned for wet/gloved hands — exposed as design tokens so it's one switch**  
  _UX_ — Current inputs/radios are desktop-sized; on a wall tablet with gloves and coffee dust, small targets cause mis-taps that, given immutability, become permanent errors
- **[Medium] Shift-start flow / 'today's required forms for this station' launcher — a single screen showing what's due now for this department with one-tap entry, instead of navigating the doc hub to find a form**  
  _scheduling_ — The plan has cadence-driven 'due' on dashboards, but a floor worker starting a shift needs a dead-simple 'here are your 3 logs for today' kiosk landing, not a personal dashboard or document map to hunt through
- **[Medium] Fast SOP finder from inside a form: scan-a-QR-on-the-machine or a one-tap 'show me how' that opens the linked SOP at the relevant step, with pictures/short steps for ESL readers**  
  _UX_ — Forms link to the SOP as a text link, but a worker mid-task who's unsure needs the right procedure in one tap (ideally from a QR sticker on the equipment), not to read a full English SOP page
- **[Medium] Mandatory corrective-action capture that's guided, not free-text: when any row is Fail/Not-cleared, present picklist of common causes + actions + photo, in the worker's language**  
  _audit_ — Validation already blocks submit without a 'corrective' note, but a free English textarea from an ESL worker yields '-' or 'fix' — useless to an inspector; structured, translated prompts produce defensible CAPA records
- **[Low] On-device confirmation feedback for low-literacy use: large green check + audio/haptic 'saved' cue and a printable/visible record number, distinct success vs error states**  
  _UX_ — On a noisy floor a small text success line is easy to miss, so staff re-submit or assume failure; an unmissable confirmation prevents duplicate immutable records and reassures ESL workers the task is done
- **[Low] Time-zone and clock-trust handling for the displayed/entered date plus a visible 'server time' stamp, and pre-fill date defaulting to the actual shift even on a tablet with a wrong clock**  
  _audit_ — Record date is taken from a client date field defaulting to the device's today; a mis-set kiosk clock or a night shift crossing midnight can mis-date an immutable food-safety record, which an auditor will question

## Engineering / data-model

- **[High] Equipment / machine registry as a first-class collection (cfia_equipment: id, name, code, department, type, manualDocCode, status active/retired, serial). Records and manuals reference equipmentId, not a free-text 'area' string.**  
  _data-model_ — Today equipment is only a free-text 'area' select in form 6.3a; without a stable equipment entity you cannot filter records per machine, attach the right manual, retire a machine, or prove per-CCP coverage to an auditor.
- **[High] Firebase Storage bucket + storage.rules + a 'storage' block in firebase.json, with an attachments subcollection on records (path, contentHash, uploadedBy, uploadedAt). Add to the auto-deploy workflow alongside firestore.rules.**  
  _data-model_ — firebase.json registers ONLY Firestore rules and no storage.rules file exists, yet inspection/complaint/recall records need immutable photo + scanned-PDF evidence; an open or absent bucket is both a security hole and an audit gap.
- **[High] Field-level schema validation in the cfia_records create rule (assert required keys, types, allowed enum values, supersedes shape) instead of only stamping who/when.**  
  _audit_ — The current create rule validates only submitted_by_*/submitted_at/created_via, so a malformed or partial record is permanently writable and can never be corrected because update/delete are false — a bad write is forever.
- **[High] Concrete correction/supersede flow: a rule + UI that requires supersedes to point to an existing same-form record, marks the prior record superseded (computed, not mutated), and a records view that shows the correction chain.**  
  _UX_ — The model has a 'supersedes' field and the UI copy promises corrections, but no code creates or links one and no rule enforces a valid target — the central immutability story is currently unbuildable in practice.
- **[High] Manager/QA verification sign-off as its own immutable collection (cfia_signoffs: covers date-range + form/department, tier supervisor|qa, signedBy, signedAt, status verified|flagged, linked recordIds) with matching Firestore rules.**  
  _data-model_ — The three-tier oversight chain is described as audit-critical but has no collection, schema, or rules yet; CFIA wants documented supervisory oversight as evidence, so this must be a first-class record type, not a screen.
- **[High] Append-only system audit log for CFIA events (doc revisions, role/department changes, exports, record submissions, sign-offs) — reuse the existing activity_log collection + netlify/functions/log-activity.js, extended with CFIA event types.**  
  _audit_ — The Hub already has an append-only activity_log but nothing in CFIA writes to it; without logging revisions/exports/role changes you cannot answer an auditor's 'who changed the current version, and who exported what, when'.
- **[High] Explicit timezone anchor (America/Edmonton) for all 'due today' / cadence math, replacing isoToday() which uses the browser's local date (cfia-core.js:114).**  
  _scheduling_ — Scheduling decides daily Pre-Op is due/overdue; a browser-local or UTC date means a 'due today' can flip a day early/late for a night shift or a traveler, producing false overdue flags on audit-critical tasks.
- **[High] Notification/reminder delivery wired to CFIA: extend the existing Resend stack + the scheduled Netlify function (check-escalations.js, cron 0 */6 * * *) to email staff/dept-head/Kyle on overdue records, unverified sign-offs, and re-acknowledge-required after a doc revision.**  
  _scheduling_ — Reminders are assumed but the only delivery mechanism (Resend + a 6-hour cron) is built for time-off and untouched by CFIA, so 'due/overdue' would live only on a dashboard nobody is pushed to open.
- **[Medium] Server-paginated / indexed records query + composite Firestore indexes (by form_code, department, submitted_at, performed_by), replacing the full-collection getDocs in listRecords (cfia-core.js:93-100).**  
  _data-model_ — listRecords currently downloads the entire cfia_records collection to the client and filters in JS; over years of daily logs this gets slow and costly and makes Audit Mode date/department filtering impractical.
- **[Medium] Offboarding / disable path for CFIA: profile status=disabled keeps records readable but blocks new submissions and sign-offs, and reassigns that person's recurring required-set so their tasks don't silently go unmonitored.**  
  _audit_ — Profiles have an active/status flag but nothing defines what happens to a disabled user's open daily tasks or pending sign-offs; an offboarded supervisor could leave a permanent unverified gap.
- **[Medium] Export-all / backup capability: a scheduled export of cfia_records + signoffs + training completions + storage attachments to durable storage, plus an Audit-Mode 'export this slice' that bundles the matching PDFs/photos.**  
  _audit_ — Immutable records concentrate compliance liability in one Firestore project; with no backup a project-level loss or accidental pre-launch reset destroys legally-required history that cannot be reconstructed.
- **[Medium] Search indexing strategy for the cross-doc search/systems-map and Manuals library (precomputed search index in config or a lightweight client index over the registry), defined before the catalogue grows past the pilot.**  
  _UX_ — The plan promises mini-search on every department page and a searchable Manuals library across 155 docs, but no indexing approach is specified; ad-hoc client scans won't scale and search quality drives daily floor navigation.
- **[Medium] Role/permission edge cases for the verification chain: a person cannot self-verify their own record (staff != signer), multi-department staff, acting/backup dept-head when the head is away, and Kyle-as-submitter conflicts.**  
  _audit_ — Rules today are profile-level (owner/controller/self) with no separation-of-duties; if a supervisor can sign off their own completed record the oversight evidence is worthless to an auditor.
- **[Medium] Stored, versioned snapshot of the form schema and reference-doc version inside each record (not just a version string), so a record renders/prints exactly as it was when submitted even after the schema changes.**  
  _data-model_ — form_version is captured as a string but the actual field set lives only in form-schemas.js; if 6.3a's fields change later, old records lose the labels/structure they were filled against, breaking faithful audit reproduction.
- **[Medium] Defined data model for training completions + acknowledgements (collection vs embedded-in-profile, with score, dateTaken, version, expiresAt) and the computed-compliance read path, before training build starts.**  
  _training_ — The plan says completions are immutable and compliance is computed, but PLAN-OF-ATTACK leaves storage ambiguous (embedded in profile vs collection); embedding immutable, ever-growing completions in a mutable profile doc conflicts with the immutability rule and Firestore doc-size limits.

## Program admin / manager ops

- **[High] Overdue escalation engine: define what happens when a due item is NOT done. Per item type set a grace window, then auto-escalate up the chain (staff miss -> dept head alerted -> Kyle/owner alerted), with the escalation itself logged as an immutable event.**  
  _scheduling_ — The plan computes 'due' and shows 'undone' but nothing forces action; a missed daily Pre-Op or an un-signed verification can sit silently until an auditor finds the gap.
- **[High] Department-head coverage / delegation: a way to name a backup verifier (or auto-route to Kyle/owner) when the dept head is on vacation, sick, or offboarded, tied to the Hub Time Off tool so verification never has a silent owner-of-record gap.**  
  _scheduling_ — The three-tier sign-off has a single point of failure; when the only verifier is away, daily/weekly sign-offs stop and the oversight chain breaks with no fallback.
- **[High] Re-acknowledgement campaign workflow: when a reference doc version bumps, automatically (re)mark everyone tagged as 'acknowledgement required', set a deadline, drive it from each dashboard, and report campaign completion %. Pair with a 'who-approves-the-change' gate before the new version goes live.**  
  _data-model_ — Version drift and stale acknowledgements are the #1 thing CFIA probes; the plan knows who 'must re-acknowledge' but has no mechanism, deadline, or change-approval step to actually run and prove the campaign.
- **[High] Document-change control workflow (draft -> review -> approver sign-off -> publish): a state machine for revising any reference doc, capturing who proposed, who approved (Russ/Kyle), effective date, and reason, before v2 supersedes v1.**  
  _audit_ — Source SOP 1.4 (Document Control Policy) mandates controlled changes; without an in-system approval gate, anyone editing config silently changes a 'controlled' document with no audit trail of authorization.
- **[High] Offboarding / deactivation flow: revoke Hub+module access, reassign their open recurring tasks and any verification duties, and retain all their immutable records intact under a deactivated profile (not deleted).**  
  _data-model_ — Plan covers onboarding only; a departed employee who keeps access is a security/audit risk, while deleting them would orphan or destroy required immutable evidence.
- **[High] Corrective action / CAPA tracker: when a record is flagged, a check fails, or a verification is rejected, open a corrective-action item with owner, due date, and closure evidence, linked to the originating immutable record.**  
  _audit_ — Immutable 'supersede' fixes the data point but nothing tracks the resolution of a real problem (failed sanitation, flagged Pre-Op) to closure, which auditors expect to see end-to-end.
- **[Medium] Contractor / visitor / temp model: lightweight identities + a short GMP/visitor briefing acknowledgement, backed by digitizing Visitor Log 6.8a and Visitor Policy 6.8, separate from full staff onboarding.**  
  _training_ — Source program has a Visitor Policy and Log and GMP applies to anyone on the floor; a staff-only roles model leaves contractors and visitors undocumented for traceability and biosecurity.
- **[Medium] Periodic-review reminder engine off nextReviewDate + the 1.2/1.3 schedule: surface SOPs coming due for review, internal-audit dates, and recurring program-review tasks on the QA/owner dashboard with overdue flags.**  
  _scheduling_ — Source SOPs 1.2 (Program Review) and 1.3 (Internal Audit) mandate scheduled self-verification; the plan stores nextReviewDate but nothing reminds anyone, so reviews lapse unnoticed.
- **[Medium] Owner KPI / program-health report: a periodic rollup (training compliance %, on-time record completion %, verification-coverage %, overdue/escalation counts, open CAPAs, upcoming doc reviews) viewable and exportable.**  
  _UX_ — The owner dashboard is described as 'QA view plus program health' but no concrete metrics are defined; owners need trend-level health and audit-readiness at a glance, not just raw lists.
- **[Medium] Meaningful sign-off capture: require verifiers to attest scope (which records/date range covered, optional sample check, exceptions noted) rather than a one-tap OK, and record verification latency (how long after the work the sign-off happened).**  
  _audit_ — CFIA wants evidence supervisory oversight was real, not rubber-stamped; a bare timestamped OK with no scope or a sign-off weeks late is exactly the weak evidence inspectors challenge.
- **[Medium] Notification/reminder channel + cadence policy: decide how due/overdue/escalation/re-ack reminders actually reach people (email via existing Resend, dashboard badges, digest frequency, quiet hours) and a single admin place to configure it.**  
  _scheduling_ — Every scheduling and campaign feature assumes 'reminders' but no delivery mechanism is specified; without a real channel, dashboards only work for people who happen to log in.
- **[Low] Admin role-change & required-set audit trail: log every change to a person's role/department (which alters their required-set retroactively) and provide an admin view of 'who is required for what and why', so reassignments are traceable.**  
  _data-model_ — Role/department drives all required reading, training, and tasks; silently changing it can quietly drop or add compliance obligations with no record of who changed it or when.
