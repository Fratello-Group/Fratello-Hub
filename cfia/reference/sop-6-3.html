SOP 6.3 — Pre-Operational Inspection Procedure

StatusControlled
Version2
EffectiveApril 1, 2025

Approved byRuss Prefontaine
Daily record for this procedure: Form 6.3a — Pre-Op Inspection Log →. Complete it before production begins for any equipment in use that day.

Purpose

To verify that all food-contact and relevant non-food-contact equipment is in clean and sound condition before production begins. This prevents contamination and ensures all cleaning, sanitation, and maintenance activities have been properly completed, in accordance with CFIA's SFCR Part 4.

Scope

Applies to:

  • All production equipment used during manufacturing
  • Handwashing stations
  • Food-contact tools and utensils
  • Sanitation and inspection records tied to operational readiness

Responsibility

RoleResponsibility
Maintenance Supervisor or DesignateConducts pre-op inspections daily and records findings
Sanitation CrewReviews findings, corrects any issues, and signs off
All Production EmployeesMay not start operations until Pre-Op is completed and cleared

Frequency

Daily, prior to production start, for any equipment in use that day.

Procedure

1. Pre-Operational Inspection

  • Conduct a visual inspection with a flashlight for each piece of equipment in use.
  • Document on the Pre-Operational Inspection Record (mark "Not in use" where applicable).

Inspect for the following:

ItemDetails
Chemical residuesSoapy buildup, sanitizer residue (see Sanitation SOP 6.1)
Product residueCoffee particles, debris, oils or dust
Excess grease or leakingCheck under and around equipment
DamageRust, broken welds, loose parts, or rough food-contact surfaces
Glass / brittle plasticRefer to Glass & Brittle Plastic SOP 6.4 for hazard response
Knife / cutting blade conditionBlades must be intact and accounted for
Handwashing stationsCheck for warm water, soap, paper towels, signage, and garbage bins

2. Daily Cleaner Sign-Off

Before cleaning begins each day, cleaners must review the Pre-Operational Inspection Record from the prior day to:

  • Identify recurring problem areas (continuous improvement)
  • Complete any outstanding cleaning (e.g. non-contact surfaces deferred from the previous shift)

Corrective Actions

DeviationAction required
Dirty food-contact equipmentMust be re-cleaned and/or re-sanitized before production begins; document in Pre-Op Record
Dirty non-food-contact surfaceMay be deferred if no food-safety risk; document in Pre-Op Record and clean post-production
Broken or damaged food-contact equipmentMust be repaired or replaced before production starts. Document and transfer to Maintenance Work Request Record
Knife / blade damageCollect and account for all broken pieces, dispose in sharps bin, check Daily Cleaning Record, and evaluate for product contamination (use Hold Procedure if needed)
Product contamination riskFollow Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product SOP and document on Corrective Action Record
Repeat issuesReview cleaning protocols, observe staff methods, contact chemical supplier for support, and retrain as needed

Verification

  • Daily: Inspection record reviewed by Maintenance Supervisor or designate.
  • Monthly: Random Pre-Op record audit during internal verification review.
  • Annually: Evaluated as part of Fratello's Internal Audit Program and reviewed for continuous improvement.
