Purpose

To ensure that raw green beans are loaded into the roaster and roasted correctly according to customer and QA specifications while also maintaining traceability and inventory of roasted products.

Responsibility

Head Roaster, Quality Assurance and Coffee Roasters

Frequency

Daily (when in operation)

Important Notes

  • The L12 is used for roasting 13lbs batch size of coffee beans and other small batches
  • The L12 is dedicated for the use of Coffee profiling and QA trials
  • Empty the chaff from the chaff collector daily when in use.

Preliminary Roasting Procedure

  • The first employee to arrive at the facility will:
    • Turn on switches which control the lights
    • Turn on dust vacuum which is located on the wall behind the Silos
    • Turn on the compressor located in the compressor room located at the rear of the facility beside the service room. The green button turns the machine on.
  • The order for roasting coffee beans is as follows:
    • Decaffeinated coffee
    • Dark roast coffee
    • Medium roast coffee
    • Light roast coffee

Note: If there is any order for portion pack ground coffee, this will be expedited after the decaffeinated roast has been completed.

Starting and Preheating the Roaster

  • Power on the laptop attached to the L-12 and turn on the Cropster Programme.
  • Check the temperature readings displayed on the screen (bean temp and env. temp).
    • Note: If the temperature readings are displayed, it means it is properly connected. If however it does not show up it means the thermometer is disconnected; then simply adjust the USB connection cable attached to the laptop.
  • On the control panel, turn the 'MAIN POWER' knob clockwise to the 'ON' position.
  • On the control panel, turn the 'BLOWER' knob clockwise to the 'ON' position. This goes hand in hand with the motor rotor of the machine.
  • Open the gas valve beside the roaster.
  • Adjust the gas percentage to the '40' mark.
  • From the control panel, turn the 'BURNER' knob clockwise to the 'ON' position.
  • Close the oven manually.
  • From the control panel, adjust the gas percentage to '80' ensuring that the air adjusting bar is pulled halfway on both sides.
  • This preheats the oven until the pre-heat temperature of 200° - 210°C is attained.
  • When the required roasting temperature is attained, turn the 'BURNER' knob anticlockwise to the 'OFF' position or turn down the gas percentage to the 20 mark. Ensure to maintain a stable temperature (200° - 210°C).

Important Note: You must periodically check the temperature during pre-heating.

Loading Green Beans (from the Silos)

  • Based on the roasting schedule, identify the sacs or silos from which the green beans will be taken.
  • On Cropster, select the Roast Profile required.
  • Using the green bean cart, open the sac and inspect for any left-over green beans. If any is observed, ensure this is emptied out prior to weighing activities.
  • Turn on the scale of the green bean cart and 'TARE' to ensure it reads 0.
    • Note: If the scale is not turned on, check the battery underneath the cart. Make sure the battery switch is positioned on. Then check the 'low battery' light. If the light is on it means the battery needs to be charged.
  • Carefully move and place the cart into position under the Silos from where the green beans will be collected.
  • Once in position, slide back the latch of the silo compartment; this dispenses the green beans into the weighing cart.
    • Note: When weighing, it is important that the precise weight needed for the roast is weighed. If more is dispensed, then the green beans should be manually scooped out into the dedicated blue bins and labeled.
  • Once the green beans needed have been weighed, transfer the beans from the green bean cart into a designated blue bin.
  • Lift the blue bins carefully and manually load the green beans into the roaster hopper.

Loading Green Beans (Manual weighing from green beans sacs)

  • Based on the roasting schedule, identify the sacs or silos from which the green beans will be taken.
  • On Cropster, select the Roast Profile required.
  • For loading green beans from sacs, use designated blue bins, turn on the floor scale at the Green Coffee Area and 'TARE' the blue bin to 0.
  • Find the required coffee sac on the rack.
  • Scoop out and weigh the required quantity into the blue bin.
  • Once green beans needed have been weighed, lift the blue bins carefully and manually load the green beans into the roaster hopper.

Roasting Activities

  • Once the green beans have been loaded, roasting is ready to begin.
  • Double check that the correct roast profile has been selected.
  • Carefully follow the roast profile noting to drop the beans at the actual recommended roasting temperature e.g., for a roast profile temperature of 210°C, beans should be dropped once the temperature is 210°C.
  • Raise the Hopper Lever upwards to load the drum with green beans from the hopper. The roasting cycle begins at this point.
  • Follow the reference graph and try to ensure that the procedures follow and match the reference graph as closely as possible.
  • Periodically, using the tryer, take a small portion from the roast beans and visually inspect the roast beans for color and roasting reaction, also ensuring to smell the product until roasting is completed.
  • Before launching stir arms, check that the cooling tray is clear and free of any debris.
  • Turn on the cooling tray stirring arm by pulling the stir lever upwards.
  • Manually open the oven to transfer the roasted beans into the cooling tray ensuring that the air adjusting bar is completely pulled to the right side.
  • When the roasted beans are offloaded into the cooling tray, it takes at least 5 minutes for them to cool down. Once the roasted beans are cool, the QA inspection is ready to be performed.

Caution: DO NOT TOUCH the roasted beans until 5 minutes after offloading as it will still be very hot.

QA Inspection Activities

  • Power on the laptop attached to the QA and launch the "Colortrack – Laser Color Analyzer" Programme.
  • From the cooling tray, use a dedicated plate to scoop out the roasted beans.
  • Transfer beans into the grinder which is located on the QA inspection rack.
  • Grind a little of the roasted beans to purge left over grinds from the grinder. Discard this first grind and then proceed to grind the remaining roasted beans for inspection.
  • Grind the remaining roasted beans into the plate and level using the dedicated ruler.
  • Gently swipe the bottom of the plate and place it into the Colortrack to read the colour value.
    • Caution: Do not touch the laser equipment by hands or the plate.
  • Set the timer knob by turning clockwise to '45 seconds' and then press to start analyzing.
  • Once analyzed, enter the 'Average' information into 'Roast Value' on Cropster.

Transfer and Weighing

  • Tare designated blue bin on the scale to 0.
  • Manually pull open the gate to transfer the roasted beans into the dedicated blue bins.
  • Ensure the cooling tray is emptied, close the gate, and the air adjusting bar is pulled halfway on both sides.
  • Transfer the blue bin to the scale and weigh the products.
  • Manually transfer beans into coffee cart.
  • Once products have been weighed, enter the weight on Cropster.
  • Enter product details on the coffee tag label.
  • Put the coffee tag on the cart.
    • Note: The roasted coffee's lot number is Cropster's ID-tag number.
  • Place bins in the Work In Progress (WIP) Storage area.

Record Completion and Build Assembly Activities

On the laptop:

  • Open Odoo.
  • Insert username and password provided.
  • Go to the Manufacturing page.
  • Click 'NEW' at the top right to create the new sheet.
  • Type in the Build Code or Product Name in the 'Product' column.
  • Type in the quantity in the 'Quantity' column; this auto-populates the information. Please ensure that you record the actual quantity in the 'To Consume' column.
  • Click the 'Save Manually' button and confirm the page.
  • Type in the quantity in the 'Quantity' column again and type in the 'Cropster ID-tag (PR-code)' in the 'Lot/Serial Number' column. This 'Lot Number' is the Roasted Bean's lot number.
  • Click the icon after the 'Lot/Serial Numbers' on the sheet. Ensure the lot number is below the 'Pick From' column, as well as the quantity. If there is an additional line, please delete it and save.
  • Repeat the previous step for each line all the way down.
  • Ensure that the check mark is in the 'Consumed' column.
  • Click the 'Save Manually' button and click 'Produce All' to build.
  • Confirm the page to finish.
  • Complete the 'Daily Roast Tracking Sheet' with the required information i.e., Item Code, roasted weight, PR. Number and Assembly number.
  • Record the 'Build Ref No' from the Build Assembly page into the 'Assembly' column on the Daily Roast Tracking Sheet.
  • Sign the 'Completed' column on the record.

Important Note: Do not sign under the 'Completed' column until all other information has been written into their respective column.

Turning off the Roaster

  • Adjust the gas percentage to the 0 mark.
  • On the control panel, turn the 'BURNER' knob counter clockwise to the 'OFF' position.
  • Close the gas valve.
  • Manually open the oven to allow air flow.
  • Ensure the air adjusting bar is pushed all the way on the right side.
  • Wait until the Cropster temperature display attains 50°C or less.
  • Turn off 'BLOWER' knob by turning counter clockwise.
  • Visually confirm that the drum in the roaster has stopped rolling.
  • Once drum has stopped, put the 'MAIN POWER' knob to the 'OFF' position by turning counter clockwise.
  • The roaster is completely turned off at this point.
  • On the laptop, close the Cropster Programme and shut down the laptop.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

Deviation and corrective actions to be taken if any of the above is not as described: Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
