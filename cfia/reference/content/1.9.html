Purpose

To ensure adequate, food-safe lighting is maintained throughout all processing and storage areas in accordance with CFIA requirements, to prevent contamination risks and support accurate inspection of products and equipment.

Scope

This procedure applies to all lighting fixtures located in:

  • Production zones (roasting, grinding, packaging)
  • Green coffee receiving areas
  • Cleaning chemical storage and sanitation zones
  • Warehousing and loading docks
  • Staff handwashing stations, lockers, and washrooms

Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
QA Coordinator Conducts monthly lux inspections; records and verifies lighting adequacy
Maintenance Supervisor Replaces burnt-out bulbs, installs new fixtures, verifies bulb type and shielding
Operations Manager Ensures adequate lighting is installed in new areas and verifies lux zones during layout changes

Procedure

4.1 Monthly Light Inspection

  • Inspect all lights for functionality and proper shielding.
  • Confirm that light fixtures in food handling and packaging zones are shatterproof or protected with sleeves/covers.
  • Document results using the Light Lux Monitoring Log (attached).

4.2 Lux Meter Monitoring

Use a calibrated lux meter to measure light intensity at:

Zone Type Minimum Lux Requirement
Critical Inspection Areas (e.g., roasted coffee, packaging stations) 540 lux
General Production & Storage Areas 220 lux
Walkways, Utility Zones 110 lux

Take readings once per month in each designated zone. Record in log with:

  • Date
  • Location
  • Measured Lux Value
  • Pass/Fail Status
  • Corrective Action (if applicable)

Corrective Actions

If any light source is:

  • Below minimum lux: Tag for replacement; install temporary lighting if in critical area
  • Unshielded or broken: Immediate removal and sanitation of surrounding area per SOP 6.4 (Glass Breakage)
  • Burnt-out in food zones: Replace within 24 hours; hold product if contamination is suspected

All deviations must be logged on the Light Lux Monitoring Log and corrective actions documented.
