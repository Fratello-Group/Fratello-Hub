Purpose

To ensure all green and roasted coffee products meet Fratello's internal quality standards, align with SCA cupping protocols, and comply with Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) regulations. This SOP outlines steps from green intake through cupping and documentation to verify consistency, traceability, and product quality.

Scope

This SOP applies to:

  • Green coffee intake and grading
  • Sample and profile roasting
  • Cupping and quality validation
  • Post-roast evaluation
  • Documentation and traceability using Cropster

Responsibilities

  • QA Manager: Implements and monitors QA protocols, reviews results
  • Head Roaster: Oversees roast consistency and profile adherence
  • Roasters: Execute roast batches, record results, and report deviations
  • QC Cuppers: Conduct cuppings, score coffees, verify against targets

QA Process Overview

StepDescription
1Green Coffee Intake & Physical Grading (see Receiving SOP)
2Moisture, Density & Defect Evaluation
3Profile Roasting (typically L12 or G90)
4Sensory Evaluation via Cupping
5Production Roast Review
6Documentation & Traceability in Cropster

Procedure

5.1 Green Coffee Quality Evaluation

Refer to SOP: FCR-GC-RCV001 - Receiving and Grading Green Coffee.

  • Sample size: 100g to 300g depending on quality
  • Analyze for defects, moisture (9–13%), color, uniformity, and odor
  • Record results in Cropster Sample Management or QA spreadsheet

5.2 Roast Profiling for New Coffees

  • Use L12 or G90 for test roasts (typically 4 x 13lb batches)
  • Modify one variable per batch (e.g., charge temp, airflow)
  • Cup blind with minimum two cuppers
  • Approval Scores:
    • Single Origin: ≥86
    • Blends: ≥84
    • Decaf: ≥85
  • Record data and scores in Cropster under the corresponding Sample Lot

5.3 Cupping Protocol

Refer to SOP: FCR-QA-CUP001 - Cupping Protocol & Quality Evaluation.

  • Use SCA standard ratio and timing
  • Score Fragrance, Aroma, Acidity, Sweetness, Body, Balance, Aftertaste, Overall
  • Maintain calibration through weekly sessions
  • Enter all data into Cropster Cup or upload scanned forms

5.4 Roast Consistency & QC Cupping

  • Daily: Review production roast curves (G90, G120, L12)
  • Weekly: QC cupping of high-volume SKUs and new lots
  • Monthly: Random audits of all active SKUs
  • Use cupping and curve data to recommend adjustments to roasting team

5.5 Documentation & Traceability

  • Use Cropster for all QA activities:
    • Lot Number > Roast Profile > Cupping Score > Roasted On Date
  • Link evaluation and roast data back to importer lot ID
  • Retain documentation for CFIA audit readiness (SFCR Part 5)

Compliance Notes

This SOP supports:

  • CFIA Preventive Controls (Part 4): Roasting consistency, hygienic evaluation
  • CFIA Traceability Requirements (Part 5): Lot tracking and product recall readiness
  • SCA Standards: For green grading and cupping evaluation

Training & Review

  • QA staff are trained at onboarding and annually thereafter
  • SOPs reviewed annually or after equipment/procedure changes
