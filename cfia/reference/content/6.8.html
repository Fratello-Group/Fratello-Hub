Visitor Policy

The Fratello Group recognizes that contamination of food or equipment may occur at any time, by visiting personnel. To mitigate the likelihood of this occurring, all visitors and contractors entering the production, storage or other areas must comply with the following guidelines to ensure proper hygienic practices and our production of safe food.

  • Visitors must read this policy and sign in and out at each visit
  • Visitors must be escorted by a designated employee
  • Visitors must not enter the production area if you have any communicable disease that can be transmitted through food. Symptoms of such diseases may include: diarrhea, vomiting, open skin sores, boils, fever, or jaundice
  • Wear appropriate work gear: hair covering, beard covering (as applicable)
  • Remove any smocks (where applicable) when going to washrooms or outside premises
  • No nail polish, or false nails. No clothing with studs, sequins or jewels
  • Do not wear any jewelry, except for a plain wedding band medic alert bracelet. Watches are not allowed. Studs on exposed body parts are not allowed
  • Do not consume any kind of food, drinks, gum, tobacco or lozenge
  • Do not bring any food with allergens into the production area
  • Visitors touring the facility are only required to sanitize hands properly before entering the warehouse or surrounding production areas.
  • Visitors entering the production area (demarcated by the red tape line) must follow the hand washing procedures
  • Do not touch products or equipment unless given permission, then ensure you are wearing clean disposable gloves
  • Cover all open or bleeding wounds with a colored bandage and disposable glove
  • Do not bring any personal medication into the production areas unless used for emergency (life-saving medication)
  • Do not bring any material or equipment made of glass. Eyeglasses are allowed
  • Be free of any infectious disease enumerated by the Public Health Agency of Canada
  • Work in a manner that prevents contamination of food product, i.e. do not work directly over open product.

By signing the Visitor Log, you are agreeing to the above guidelines and confirming that you are free from any communicable disease. Any visitor or contractor failing to observe the above practices may be asked to leave the establishment.
