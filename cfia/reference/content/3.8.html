Purpose

To ensure that raw green beans are loaded into the roaster and roasted correctly according to QA sampling specification.

Responsibility

Head Roaster, Quality Assurance and Coffee Roasters

Frequency

Daily (when in operation)

Important Notes

  • The Roest roaster is dedicated for the use of roasting small sample (100g) batch sizes.
  • Samples are used for Coffee profiling and QA trials only.
  • Empty the chaff from the chaff collector daily when in use.

Starting and Preheating the Roaster

  • Plug and power on the machine by turning the 'POWER' at the back side of the machine.
  • Close the drum by moving the drop handle to the downward position.
  • Tap the Profile Menu and go to 'Profiles' to select the profile. The roaster will start preheating to reach the target temperature of the chosen profile.
  • Set the timer and leave the roaster to heat up. This typically takes about 20 minutes.
  • Power on the laptop attached to the Sample Area, go to the 'Roest Web Portal'.
  • Select 'Live'.

Loading and Roasting Activities

  • Check the sample number of coffee and prepare the blue coffee tray. If the sample number is not written on the bag, inform the Head Roaster.
  • Using the scoop and scale, weigh out exactly 100g of green beans into the blue coffee tray (labeled 'GREEN ONLY').
  • After pre-heating, gently pour the green beans into the hopper.
  • Pull the charge handle to start roasting and ensure all the beans enter the roasting chamber.
  • Periodically, using the tryer and drum window, take a small portion from the roast beans and visually inspect the roast beans for color and roasting reaction, also ensuring to smell the sample until roasting is completed.
  • The automatic mode is set by default on the roaster. Follow the automatic mode; if necessary, change to manual mode.
    • Note: If necessary, enter manual mode by adjusting the knob on the top of the roaster or through the screen.
  • Roasted bean will be dropped by automation. If in manual mode, put the drum handle up to pull out the beans. Leave beans to cool for 5 minutes.

Caution: DO NOT TOUCH the roasted beans until 2 minutes after offloading as it will still be very hot.

  • Enter the bean information on the log page. The roasting profile will be saved automatically.
  • Ensure the blue coffee tray is clean and make the sample number label, putting it under the tray.
  • Transfer roasted coffee to the tray.
  • Once roasting activities are completed, put the cooling tray back into position, and open the oven by drum handle.
  • Once all the temperatures drop to under 50°C, turn off the power and unplug.
  • Remove the cooling tray, pull out the screen, and clean it.
  • Open the bottom of the panel and remove the cover.
  • Empty the chaff collector. Place all the parts back into their position.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

Deviation and corrective actions to be taken if any of the above is not as described: Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
