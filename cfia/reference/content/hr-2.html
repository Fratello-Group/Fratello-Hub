Purpose

The Fratello Group is committed to a healthy work environment free of harassment and violence for all employees. This company-wide policy is intended to prevent harassment of any type — including sexual harassment, workplace violence and bullying — and to deal quickly and effectively with any incident.

Scope

Applies to all Fratello Group companies.

Definitions

Harassment

Unwelcome written, emailed, voice-mailed, verbal or physical conduct because of race, religious beliefs, colour, gender, physical or mental disability, age, ancestry, place of origin, marital status, source of income, family status or sexual orientation. Examples that will not be tolerated: verbal or physical abuse, threats, derogatory remarks, jokes, innuendo or taunts on those grounds; display of pornographic, racist or offensive images; practical jokes causing embarrassment; unwelcome invitations or requests; and conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile or offensive work environment.

Sexual harassment

Unwanted sexual advances or requests for sexual favours where submission is made a condition of employment or affects employment — including pinching, patting, leering, suggestive or obscene comments or gestures, sexist jokes, pornographic materials, unwelcome flirtations or propositions, unwanted touching, verbal abuse or threats, and sexual assault. The behaviour need not be intentional to be considered sexual harassment.

Workplace violence

Physical assault or aggression, and unsolicited conduct, comment, gesture or contact causing offence, humiliation or physical/psychological harm. Types: stranger violence (e.g. robbery), client/customer violence, co-worker violence, and violence related to domestic issues.

Workplace bullying

A repeated pattern of negative behaviour aimed at a specific person or group, usually causing psychological rather than physical harm; often subtle and verbal, and can come from managers, supervisors, co-workers or customers.

Where it can occur

At the workplace; at employment-related social functions; during work assignments outside the workplace; at work-related conferences/training; during work-related travel; over the telephone for work-related conversations; or elsewhere if the person is there because of work-related responsibilities or relationships.

Reporting procedure

  1. Tell the harasser their behaviour is unwelcome and ask them to stop (in person or by email — keep the email).
  2. Keep a record of incidents (date, time, location, witnesses, what happened, your response).
  3. If it continues, report to your manager, the HR representative, or the senior leader in your area. For workplace violence, complete the Incident Reporting and Investigation Form.

You also have the right to contact the Alberta Human Rights Commission, and to report violence or assault to the police.

Dealing with a complaint

  • Complaints are kept strictly confidential; an investigation begins immediately and all necessary steps are taken to resolve the problem (conciliation where appropriate).
  • Both the complainant and the alleged harasser are interviewed, as are relevant witnesses; all information is kept in confidence.
  • If the complaint is substantiated, the harasser is disciplined appropriately (up to suspension or dismissal) and the incident is documented in their file. No documentation is placed on the complainant's file.
  • No reprisal for making a complaint or providing evidence.

Support

All Fratello Group employees qualify for the Employee & Family Assistance Program, available 24/7 at 1-800-387-4765 and workhealthlife.com; additional support is available from the Distress Centre Calgary. This policy is reviewed periodically (v2, March 2025).
