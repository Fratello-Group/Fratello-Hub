Purpose

To establish Fratello Coffee Roasters' commitment to regulatory compliance for all food imported into, exported from, or traded within Canada, in accordance with the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR), Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) guidance, and applicable foreign market requirements.

Scope

This procedure applies to all:

  • Imported products and ingredients, including green coffee, packaging, and food-contact materials
  • Exported finished products (e.g., roasted coffee) to international markets
  • Interprovincial shipments of food traded within Canada

Regulatory Reference

  • SFCR Part 2 – Trade (Sections 6–13)
  • SFCR Part 4 – Preventive Controls (Sections 47–92)
  • SFCR Part 5 – Traceability
  • CFIA Guidance for Importers and Exporters of Food

Linked SOPs and Documentation

The following documents make up Fratello's operational procedures and controls for this section of the Preventive Control Plan:

SOP Code Document Title Scope
SOP-PCP-1.1a Incoming Input Controls Verification of raw materials, packaging, chemical inputs (e.g., green coffee, bags, degassers)
SOP-PCP-1.3 Import Controls Supplier approval, COAs, mycotoxin testing, CFIA licensing and importer obligations
SOP-PCP-1.4 Export Controls Documentation for market eligibility, CFIA certificates, traceability and labelling for foreign markets
SOP-PCP-1.5 Interprovincial Trade Compliance Requirements for Canadian trade across provincial lines, label verification, and domestic traceability

Execution

These SOPs are reviewed:

  • Annually as part of the Preventive Control Plan review cycle
  • Immediately if CFIA, FDA, EU, or customer export requirements change
  • Quarterly for alignment with supplier and export partner changes

All supporting records (e.g., COAs, Import/Export Certificates, Supplier Approvals) are retained for 2 years minimum.
