Purpose

To ensure all new or modified equipment is assessed and approved before use in food production, with respect to:

  • Sanitary design standards
  • Cleaning and maintenance compatibility
  • Food contact surface safety
  • Preventive Control Plan (PCP) integration

This procedure ensures compliance with Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR) Part 4 – Preventive Controls.

Scope

Applies to all new or modified equipment used at Fratello Coffee Roasters in:

  • Roasting
  • Grinding
  • Packaging
  • Green coffee storage
  • Facility support systems (e.g., air compressors, conveyors)

Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
Director of Operations Approves new equipment purchases; ensures funding and specs are aligned
QA Coordinator Conducts risk assessments, sanitation compatibility checks, and recordkeeping
Maintenance Supervisor Assesses parts availability, maintenance schedule, and installation requirements
Department Lead Confirms operational compatibility and integrates into workflow

Procedure – New Equipment Approval

4.1 Initial Assessment

  • Submit proposed equipment to QA and Operations using the Equipment Evaluation Form
  • Include:
    • Manufacturer specs
    • Sanitary design certifications (e.g., NSF, CSA, FDA contact-safe)
    • Material compatibility with cleaning agents
    • Food contact surface diagrams (if applicable)

4.2 Approval Process

  • QA conducts a Hazard Identification & Compatibility Review
  • Maintenance evaluates for:
    • Service access
    • Spare parts availability
    • Lubrication points (must be food-grade if near product)
  • Sanitation team confirms:
    • Ease of cleaning
    • Compatibility with existing cleaning procedures and agents
  • Final sign-off from Director of Operations

4.3 Installation Requirements

  • Ensure electrical, ventilation, and safety lockout systems are compliant
  • Update facility schematic (if applicable)
  • Conduct initial test run and cleaning validation prior to operational use

Integration with PCP

Once approved:

  • Add to Preventive Maintenance Schedule (SOP 7.1)
  • Add to Cleaning Program (SOP 6.1)
  • Assign calibration and verification protocols (if applicable, see SOP 7.2)
  • Include in Pre-Operational Inspection Checklist (SOP 6.3)
