Purpose

To ensure that finished coffee products are transported in a clean, secure manner that prevents contamination or damage. This includes verifying the condition of shipping vehicles and enforcing hygienic practices during loading. Preventive control measures are implemented to meet CFIA's SFCR Sections 72–74.

Scope

Applies to all shipping personnel, contract carriers, and walk-in customers involved in loading finished goods from the Fratello facility.

Responsibility

  • Primary: Shipper/Receiver
  • Backup: Trained Designate

Frequency

  • Per Shipment: Prior to every load being shipped.

Procedure

Step Key Actions Food Safety Considerations
1 Customer or driver must remain on the dock during pickup. Limits unauthorized access and cross-contamination.
2 Inspect vehicle interior before loading: verify no off-odours, visible pests, dirt, debris, chemical residues, or incompatible cargo. Aligns with SFCR s.72 to prevent contamination from previous loads.
3 Record inspection results on the Shipping Record and sign/date the entry. Required documentation for traceability and verification.
4 For personal vehicles (small order pickups), record customer name and items collected in the Shipping Record. Maintains full traceability of all product movement.
5 Complete Transport Log: include condition of trailer, date, product, quantity, carrier name, and reference documents (invoice, BOL). Ensures traceability as per SFCR Part 5.
6 Load product to prevent physical damage or contamination. Secure loads to prevent shifting during transit. Protects food from damage and maintains integrity during transport.
7 Do not ship if vehicle is unacceptable. Ask driver to correct deficiencies (e.g., sweep, sanitize, remove incompatible items) before reinspection. Preventive action to mitigate hazards as required by SFCR.

Deviations and Corrective Actions

Issue Corrective Action
Vehicle unclean, odorous, or damaged Do not load. Ask driver to clean, repair, or return with a compliant vehicle.
Evidence of pests or contamination Deny access to facility. Require pest-free vehicle and conduct internal investigation if risk present.
Incompatible items in transport (e.g., chemicals, unpackaged goods) Refuse to load. Instruct removal of such items. Reinspect.
Incorrect or missing paperwork Place shipment on hold until documentation is resolved.

All corrective actions must be recorded in the Shipping Record and/or Corrective Action Log.
