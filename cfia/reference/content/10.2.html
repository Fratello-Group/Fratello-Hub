Scope

This procedure outlines the process for initiating and managing a product recall in response to a potential health or safety risk associated with any of our products. A product recall is initiated to protect consumers and uphold our commitment to product safety and regulatory compliance. Notification of a potential risk typically originates from a customer; however, Health Canada may also directly inform us if a customer reports the issue to them.

Procedure

The following procedure will be adhered to upon becoming aware of a potential health risk:

1. Initial Assessment and Scope Determination

  • Upon discovery of a potential health or safety risk, an immediate assessment must be conducted to determine the scope of the issue. This includes identifying the specific product(s) affected, the nature of the risk, and the potential severity of harm.
  • Examples of safety risks include, but are not limited to:
    • Foreign material contamination (e.g., physical objects in product)
    • Bacterial or other microbial contamination
    • Incorrect labeling or packaging that could lead to misuse
    • Defects in product design or manufacturing

2. Product Segregation and Identification

  • Any product identified as potentially hazardous must be immediately segregated to prevent further distribution or use.
  • Affected products must be placed in secure, sealed containers or designated areas.
  • Each container or area must be clearly labeled with a "Do Not Sell, Do Not Destroy" label (See Appendix A) to prevent accidental sale or disposal.
  • Detailed records of the segregated products, including lot numbers, quantities, and locations, must be meticulously maintained.

3. Recall Initiation and Health Canada Notification

  • A product recall will be initiated in accordance with Health Canada guidelines and all applicable regulations.
  • The incident must be reported to Health Canada promptly, using the following link: http://www.healthycanadians.gc.ca/apps/radar/CPS-SPC-0001.08.html?t=report_
  • All communications with Health Canada must be documented, including dates, times, and individuals involved.
  • We will fully cooperate with Health Canada and provide all necessary information and assistance throughout the recall process.

4. Recall Notice Development

  • A comprehensive recall notice must be created. This notice will contain the following information (See Appendix B):
    • Affected Products: Clear and detailed identification of the recalled products, including brand names, product names, size and product code (Best Before Date or Production Date).
    • Nature of the Hazard: A clear and concise explanation of the specific health or safety risk posed by the product.
    • Recall Instructions: Clear, step-by-step instructions for customers on what to do if they have purchased the recalled product. This may include:
      • Ceasing use of the product
      • Method of returning the product to the place of purchase
      • Contacting us for further instructions (e.g., for refunds, exchanges, or repairs)
    • Contact Information: Complete contact information for customer inquiries, including a telephone number and email address.

5. Customer Notification

  • We will identify all customers who may have purchased the affected products. This will be done by reviewing our ERP system, which contains all relevant documentation.
    • Sales records
    • Order histories
    • Distribution records
    • Customer databases
  • A detailed break and process is laid out in Appendix D for traceability in Odoo ERP.
  • Customers will be notified using the most effective and timely methods available, which may include:
    • Direct contact (e.g., telephone, email, registered mail)
    • Public announcements (e.g., press releases, website postings, social media)
    • Retailer notifications
  • The recall notice will be provided to all identified customers.
  • Customers will be instructed on how to return the product for exchange or refund, in accordance with the recall notice.

6. Product Recovery and Consolidation

  • All recalled products must be collected and consolidated as quickly and efficiently as possible.
  • The consolidation must be completed within 30 days of the initial recall notification, unless otherwise directed by Health Canada.
  • The quantity and location of all affected products recovered must be accurately documented and reported to Health Canada within 10 calendar days of the recall notification.
  • Records must be maintained of all steps taken to recover and consolidate the recalled products.

7. Product Disposition

  • Health Canada will provide guidance and authorization on the proper disposal or destruction of all recalled product.
  • All disposal activities must be conducted in accordance with applicable regulations and environmental standards.
  • Detailed records of the disposal process, including the date, location, method of disposal, and quantity of product disposed of, must be maintained.

8. Recall Effectiveness Checks

  • We will conduct recall effectiveness checks to verify that all reasonable attempts have been made to notify customers and recover the affected products.
  • Effectiveness checks may include:
    • Monitoring customer response rates
    • Following up with non-responding customers
    • Verifying the return of recalled products from distributors and retailers
  • The results of the effectiveness checks will be documented and reported to Health Canada.

9. Recall Closure

  • A final report will be submitted to Health Canada summarizing the recall activities, the amount of product recovered, and the results of effectiveness checks.
  • The recall will be closed in consultation with Health Canada, upon their satisfaction that all necessary actions have been completed.

Appendix D: Recall Procedure Based on ERP Traceability

1. Recall Protocol Initiation

  • Trigger: The identification of a potential health or safety risk (e.g., contamination, defect) will serve as the triggering event.
  • Action: The company's official recall protocol must be activated. This includes notification of the Recall Team Leader, recall team members, and customer service representatives.
  • Goal: To initiate communication with all identifiable customers who have purchased potentially affected products within 30 minutes of receiving notification concerning a potential health or safety risk, prioritizing customer well-being and rapid risk mitigation.

2. Issue Source Identification (using Odoo ERP)

  • Objective: The primary objective is to ascertain the origin of the problem, thereby defining the scope of the recall.
  • Action Steps:
    • 2.1 Identify Compromised Product: If a health or safety risk is brought to the company's attention, the affected product, and its manufacturing date must be determined.
    • 2.2 Access Bill of Materials (BOM): The Bill of Materials (BOM) for the identified finished product(s) will be accessed within the Odoo ERP system. The BOM can be used to quickly determine inputs used and the other potential implicated products. Information is retrieved by following: Inventory -> Product Variants -> Select Necessary Product -> using the More dropdown to retrieve desired BOM Information. If a contamination is identified by a supplier, the traceability of the system can be used to quickly identify all products that make use of the identified input, by selecting "Used In", as opposed to "Bill of Materials".
    • 2.3 Trace Raw Materials:
      • Using the BOM, the specific raw materials utilized (e.g., lot number of ingredients, components) will be identified.
      • We can determine the inputs, from the WIP product (Roasted bean) and its roasting number, to the green coffee used to produce the WIP, and the green coffee lot number.
      • From within a BOM, the user can select the "Roasted Bean", which will allow it to be traced to the individual inputs used during the roasting process.
      • All relevant raw material information, including supplier details, lot numbers, and dates of receipt, must be recorded.
    • 2.4 Trace Supplier (If Applicable): If the issue originates with a raw material, the Odoo ERP system will be employed to trace the raw material lot number back to the supplier. Pertinent supplier details shall be recorded. A traceability report can be run on any lot to see not only its use in product, but also date of receipt and vendor.

3. Identification of Potentially Affected Products and Customers (using Odoo ERP)

  • Objective: This stage aims to determine the extent of the potential impact and identify all affected customers.
  • Action Steps:
    • 3.1 Access Inventory Module: The Inventory Module within the Odoo ERP system will be accessed.
    • 3.2 Filter Delivery Orders:
      • The Delivery Orders section will be accessed.
      • Delivery Orders will be filtered by:
        • Date range: (e.g., "the preceding 30 days"), or a specific period predicated on the production dates of affected raw materials or finished goods.
        • Product: The specific finished product(s) identified in Step 2.1 will be located and selected.
    • 3.3 Retrieve Customer Information: Customer information will be extracted from the filtered Delivery Orders. This information can be extracted in a bulk export and includes:
      • Customer Name
      • Shipping Address
      • Contact Phone Number
      • Email Address
      A preset recall template has been created for quick retrieval of data and includes necessary fields.
    • 3.4 Export Customer Data: The customer data will be exported into a suitable format (e.g., CSV, Excel) to facilitate rapid communication. The exported data will be comprehensive. The extracted email addresses may be utilized to facilitate prompt and efficient communication via electronic mail, thereby enabling immediate contact with all customers who purchased the implicated product(s).
    • 3.5 Identify all affected inventory:
      • The warehouse manager will isolate all implicated products still in the warehouse and on the shelf. Inventory on the shelf will be compared and verified with Odoo inventory records.
      • Inventory will be marked with the "Do Not Sell, Do Not Destroy" tag, from Appendix A.
      • Inventory in Odoo will be transferred to a virtual "Quarantine" location, reflective of the treatment of the physical product.

4. Communication and Action Facilitation (using Odoo ERP)

  • Objective: The data from the Odoo ERP system will be used to inform stakeholders and initiate the physical recall process.
  • Action Steps:
    • 4.1 Communicate with Stakeholders:
      • The exported customer data from Step 3.4 will be utilized to communicate the recall to affected customers. Notification of the recall will be disseminated via electronic mail and phone if necessary.
      • Clear instructions for product return, replacement, or disposal will be provided.
      • Communication with regulatory agencies will be conducted as required.
    • 4.2 Inventory Handling and Tracking: The Odoo ERP system possesses return tracking functionality and it will be used to:
      • Record returned products.
      • Track return status.
      • Manage reverse logistics (e.g., shipping, disposal, restocking).
