Purpose

To protect food, packaging, and production areas from contamination caused by poor personal hygiene or GMP violations. This policy ensures compliance with SFCR Part 4, specifically sections covering personnel hygiene, training, food handling practices, and contamination prevention.

Scope

Applies to:

  • All Fratello employees (production, warehouse, admin)
  • Visitors, contractors, and customers entering production or storage zones

Responsibility

  • All Personnel: Must follow hygiene and GMP procedures
  • Supervisors: Monitor compliance and enforce corrective actions
  • Director of Operations: Manages training and updates

Frequency

  • Daily, ongoing during shifts
  • At key transition points (entry, breaks, washroom use)
  • As needed based on contamination risks or incidents

Hygiene and GMP Policy

1. Hand Washing & Sanitizing

When to Wash Hands

  • Entering/re-entering production or warehouse
  • Start of shift
  • After breaks, eating, smoking, sneezing, coughing, or using phone
  • After touching face, hair, doors, hoses, wood, chemicals, garbage
  • After handling tools or touching non-food-contact surfaces

Handwashing Procedure:

  • Use warm water
  • Apply soap, lather 20+ seconds
  • Scrub between fingers, nail beds, wrists
  • Rinse thoroughly
  • Dry with single-use paper towel
  • Turn off tap with towel
  • Dispose towel in bin

Handwashing Stations Are Located In:

  • Production Area
  • Warehouse
  • Service/Mop Room
  • Lunchroom/washroom areas

Sanitizer Stations Are Located:

  • Hallways
  • Production main entrance
  • Throughout warehouse and packaging areas

Note: Admin staff and visitors entering via public areas may use hand sanitizer only, unless entering red-line production zones — in which case, full handwashing is required.

2. Personal Hygiene Standards

  • Arrive clean and in clean clothing
  • No strong perfumes/colognes
  • Hair must be tied back; no hats with sequins/studs
  • Nails short, clean – no polish or false nails
  • No false eyelashes

3. Jewelry & Accessories

  • Only plain wedding bands allowed in production
  • No visible piercings, watches, or rings with stones
  • Medic alerts must be declared to the Director of Operations

4. Food, Drink & Medication

  • No food/drinks in production/storage areas
  • Allowed only in lunchroom, café, or designated spaces
  • Medications must be stored in lockers unless life-saving (e.g., epinephrine)

5. Illness & Communicable Diseases

Employees must report if they exhibit:

  • Vomiting, diarrhea, fever, sore throat
  • Discharge from eyes/nose/ears
  • Yellowing of skin/eyes (jaundice)

Diseases of concern include: Hepatitis, Listeria, Staph, Salmonella, Typhoid. Employees must not work in food areas until medically cleared.

6. Cuts, Wounds & Blood Cleanup

  • Must be covered with blue bandage + nitrile gloves
  • Uncovered wounds = no entry into production
  • Blood/bodily fluid = stop work, contact supervisor, discard affected product, clean/sanitize with gloves, and document

7. Cross-Contamination Prevention

  • Stay in designated areas
  • Wash hands frequently
  • Do not move between clean and dirty zones without sanitation
  • Use designated, clean tools and follow Sanitation SOP
  • Do not touch garbage, doors, raw materials, and then return to clean tasks without handwashing

Corrective Actions

DeviationCorrective Action
Non-compliance with hygiene rulesRetrain employee, document incident
Contaminated product due to hygiene lapseDiscard affected product, refer to Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product SOP
Ill employee workingRemove from production area, refer to medical clearance
Broken hand hygiene station or empty dispensersRefill/repair immediately, log in Maintenance Work Request Record
