Purpose

The Fratello Group is committed to a safe, productive work environment free of drug and alcohol impairment and abuse. The objective of this policy is to ensure that employees, contractors and subcontractors are fit for duty and to minimize the health and safety risk in the workplace due to alcohol and drug use.

Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors and subcontractors at any workplace. Contravention is very serious and is considered a breach of contract. Fratello has a Zero Tolerance Policy for anyone who arrives at work not fit for duty or who is under the influence of alcohol or drugs at the workplace.

Key definitions

  • Alcohol: any consumable substance with alcoholic content above 0.5%.
  • Drugs: illicit drugs, medications or mood-altering substances that can adversely affect cognition or fitness for duty.
  • Fit for duty: the ability to mentally and physically perform job duties safely and reliably.
  • Reasonable suspicion: circumstances indicating possible impairment — e.g. odour of alcohol/drugs, slurred speech, glassy eyes, unsteadiness, flushed face, disorientation, drowsiness.
  • Workplace: all land, property, structures, vehicles and equipment owned, leased, operated or controlled by Fratello, or used by the employee during the workday.

Responsibilities

  • Read, understand and fully comply with this policy; report and remain fit for duty throughout the workday.
  • Notify a supervisor immediately of suspected alcohol/drug use, drug paraphernalia, or a reasonable suspicion that someone is not fit for duty.
  • Refuse work requests while under the influence; report incidents and near-misses.
  • Consult a physician/pharmacist about whether prescribed medication affects job performance, and report any risk, limitation or restriction.
  • Use medications responsibly and only as prescribed; seek treatment for any dependency.

Requirements

  • Fitness for duty: report and remain fit for duty; if asked to work while not fit, refuse and ask that the work be reassigned.
  • Alcohol & drugs: do not use, possess, distribute, offer or sell alcohol, drugs, paraphernalia or mood-altering substances at the workplace or during the workday; do not test positive at or above the policy's concentrations.
  • Exceptions: medications are allowed only if used exactly as prescribed/intended, after checking with a doctor/pharmacist about workplace effects, and any restrictions are disclosed to a supervisor.

Breach, accommodation & testing

  • A violation is a breach of the employment relationship and may be grounds for discipline up to and including immediate termination. Fratello may place an individual on leave pending investigation.
  • Voluntary disclosure: employees who disclose a possible dependency before a violation occurs and seek assistance will not be disciplined for the disclosure; Fratello may refer them to an Employee & Family Assistance Program and may require a reinstatement monitoring agreement (return-to-duty and periodic unannounced testing, ongoing treatment conditions).
  • Testing: Fratello may test for fitness for duty on reasonable suspicion (and other circumstances set out in the full policy).
