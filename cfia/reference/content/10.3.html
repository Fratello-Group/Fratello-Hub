1. Purpose

To define the standard procedure for conducting and documenting an annual mock recall in compliance with the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR). This SOP ensures Fratello Coffee Roasters can demonstrate effective traceability and recall readiness through a structured simulation and recordkeeping process.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all food products produced and distributed by Fratello Coffee Roasters. It is intended for QA, Operations, Sales, and Executive Team members involved in recall management.

3. Responsibilities

  • Recall Coordinator: Oversees execution and documentation of the mock recall.
  • QA Manager: Leads traceability activities and ensures data integrity.
  • Sales/Customer Service: Verifies customer distribution data and simulated contact.
  • Warehouse Manager: Identifies held stock and ensures product control.
  • All Recall Team Members: Participate in execution and post-drill debriefing.

4. Frequency

A mock recall must be conducted at least once every 12 months and retained for a minimum of 2 years.

5. Procedure

5.1 Initiate Mock Recall

  • Select a scenario (e.g., allergen, contamination, foreign material).
  • Define affected product, lot code, production date, and quantity.
  • Log scenario details in Appendix A.

5.2 Assemble Recall Team

  • Notify Recall Coordinator and team.
  • Assign roles per Recall Procedure (SOP 10.1).
  • Begin logging all actions with times and responsibilities.

5.3 Identify and Trace Product

  • QA Manager retrieves production and traceability records.
  • Create distribution list (see Appendix B) showing:
    • Clients, dates, quantities shipped
    • Contact info and (simulated) response
  • Identify on-hand inventory and mark as "On Hold".

5.4 Simulate Client and CFIA Communication

  • Prepare draft Notice of Recall (see Appendix C).
  • Simulate calls/emails to clients (document who, how, when).
  • Prepare CFIA information packet (see Appendix E).
  • Do not actually contact CFIA or clients during the mock recall.

5.5 Simulate Media Communication (Optional)

  • Draft a mock press release using CFIA format (see Appendix D).

5.6 Complete Mock Recall Report

  • Use the Mock Recall Report Template (integrated into this SOP or stored as a standalone form).
  • Complete all required fields:
    • Scenario Summary
    • Product & Traceability Details
    • One-Step-Back and One-Step-Forward Records
    • Action Timeline and Team Roles
    • Communication Logs
    • Evaluation of Results
    • Corrective and Preventive Actions

6. Documentation Requirements

Maintain the following:

  • Mock Recall Report (Template Completed)
  • Appendix A: Scenario Summary
  • Appendix B: Traceability & Distribution List
  • Appendix C: Draft Notice of Recall
  • Appendix D: Press Release (if used)
  • Appendix E: CFIA Reporting Sheet
  • Appendix F: Recall Team Contact List (Updated)
  • Appendix G: Completed Mock Recall Report Template
  • Corrective Actions Log (as needed)

All documentation must be retained in physical or digital format for a minimum of 2 years and made available upon CFIA request.
