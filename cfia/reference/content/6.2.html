1. Purpose

To prevent contamination of food products through effective sanitation practices, hygiene controls, pest prevention, and containment of biological hazards within the Fratello production environment, in accordance with SFCR Part 4 – Preventive Controls.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to:

  • All food production and storage areas
  • Equipment, utensils, packaging, and tools
  • Cleaning chemicals and sanitation tools
  • Staff, visitors, and contractors who enter production zones

3. Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
Maintenance Supervisor Oversee sanitation program, approve chemical use, and verify training of sanitation personnel.
Cleaning Personnel Execute cleaning tasks as assigned in the Master Sanitation Schedule.
QA/Production Staff Conduct Pre-Operational Inspections to verify sanitation.
All Staff Follow hygiene protocols and report contamination risks.

4. Procedure

4.1 Cleaning and Sanitation Protocols

  • All sanitation follows the Master Sanitation Schedule, which includes:
    • Frequency (e.g., daily, weekly, monthly, deep cleans)
    • Equipment-specific instructions
    • Tools, chemicals, and PPE to be used
  • Use Procedure A (Dry Cleaning) for most coffee equipment (e.g., vacuums, compressed air).
  • Use Procedure B (Manual Cleaning) for wet-suitable tools/machines.
  • Use Procedure C (Sink Washing) for small parts, utensils, and tools.

4.2 Verification of Cleaning

  • Pre-Operational Inspections are conducted daily before production:
    • Check for residue, contamination, and sanitation chemical presence.
    • Confirm that all food contact surfaces are clean and undamaged.
  • Deviations must be documented on the Pre-Operational Inspection Record and corrected before start-up.

4.3 Biosecurity Measures

  • Hygiene practices are enforced via the GMP & Hygiene Policy:
    • No jewelry, perfume, or personal items in production areas.
    • Proper handwashing required at all entry points and after contamination events.
    • Visitors and contractors must sanitize hands and be supervised.
    • Sick employees or those with open wounds are not allowed in production.

4.4 Biocontainment & Cross-Contamination Prevention

  • Tools, utensils, and containers are:
    • Cleaned and stored in designated areas
    • Color-coded or labeled to prevent misuse
  • Gloves are worn when required and changed if dirty or damaged.
  • No food, drink, or medication is permitted in production zones.
  • Chemicals are dispensed safely and stored in locked areas.

4.5 Pest Control

  • Pest prevention is managed by Peregrine (monthly service).
  • Devices are checked and logged in the Service Report and Pest Control Binder.
  • If pest activity is detected:
    • Investigate root cause
    • Clean and sanitize affected areas
    • Discard any exposed product
    • Log corrective actions on the Deviation Record

5. Corrective Actions

Issue Corrective Action
Equipment found dirty during pre-op Re-clean and re-inspect. Log deviation.
Pests or pest evidence found Investigate, sanitize, discard product, update Pest Log.
Chemical misuse or unlabeled containers Dispose, retrain staff, relabel correctly.
Cross-contamination observed Isolate affected product, retrain, log deviation.

6. Records and Documents

  • Master Sanitation Schedule
  • Pre-Operational Inspection Record
  • Daily Cleaning Log
  • Pest Control Binder & Service Reports
  • Corrective Action Records
  • Chemical List and Safety Data Sheets (SDS)
  • Visitor/Contractor Sign-In Sheets
