1. Purpose

To ensure that all incoming inputs—green coffee beans, ingredients, packaging materials, cleaning chemicals, and miscellaneous supplies—are received from approved sources, inspected upon arrival, and handled in a manner that prevents contamination and ensures traceability, in accordance with SFCR Sections 47(2), 72–74.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to all materials entering the facility, including:

  • Raw ingredients (e.g. green beans)
  • Packaging materials (e.g. bags, boxes, labels)
  • Chemicals (e.g. cleaning and sanitation agents)
  • Miscellaneous items (e.g. gloves, filters)
  • Returned goods (under exception)

4. Procedure

4.1 Pre-Receiving Checks

  • Confirm that the supplier is on the Approved Supplier List.
  • Obtain and review the PO/Invoice and Bill of Lading.
  • Confirm delivery timing (e.g., separate chemical and ingredient deliveries when possible).

4.2 Carrier Inspection

  • Inspect the transport vehicle for:
    • Cleanliness (no visible dirt, mold, debris)
    • Absence of odours or pest infestation
    • Proper segregation from non-food items or chemicals
    • No signs of water damage or leaks
  • Record findings on the Transport Log.

4.3 Material Inspection

  • For Green Beans:
    • Verify LOT numbers, origin, and visual cleanliness of sacks.
    • Document on the inventory board and silo chamber magnets.
  • For Packaging and Other Inputs:
    • Inspect for damage, debris, pest evidence, or exposure to moisture.
    • Ensure items are closed, labeled, and intact.

4.4 Receiving and Documentation

  • Record all incoming items on the Transport Log.
  • Include date, supplier name, product, quantity, BOL number.
  • Apply pallet tags and record LOT numbers (if applicable).
  • If non-conforming, place on Hold and notify QA/Management.

4.5 Storage

  • Move accepted materials directly to designated storage areas.
  • Ensure chemicals are stored separately in locked rooms per Sanitation Procedures.
  • Use FIFO practices for raw ingredients and packaging.

4.6 Returned Products (Exceptional Cases)

  • Must be labeled and quarantined in designated Hold Area.
  • Document in Hold Log.
  • Evaluate condition; disposition must be approved by QA or Management.

5. Deviation and Corrective Action
