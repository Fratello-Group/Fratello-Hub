Purpose

To ensure all production equipment is maintained in a way that prevents food contamination, supports equipment reliability, and ensures compliance with CFIA requirements under SFCR Part 4 (Preventive Controls).

Scope

This SOP applies to all critical process equipment used in Fratello Coffee Roasters' production areas, including roasters, grinders, packaging lines, silos, and support utilities (e.g., air compressors, vacuum systems).

Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
Maintenance Supervisor Executes and documents preventive/corrective maintenance
QA Roaster / Designate Verifies cleanliness and post-maintenance sanitation
All Operators Inspect and report irregularities during daily startup
Director of Operations Approves major repairs and system overhauls

Maintenance GMPs

  • Follow PPE and hygiene SOPs before entering production zones.
  • Clean all tools before use in food zones.
  • Use toolboxes or pouches — tools in pockets are prohibited.
  • Never leave tools or parts on food-contact surfaces.
  • Always clean/sanitize food-contact areas post-maintenance.

If maintenance is done during production:

  • Cover or remove nearby product and packaging.
  • Ensure clean-up and post-repair QA verification are completed.

Utility Knife Policy

  • Only one-piece fixed blades are allowed.
  • No "snap-off" or perforated utility blades.
  • If a blade breaks or is missing, immediately inform management.

Maintenance Programs

6.1 Preventive Maintenance (PM)

  • Based on:
    • Manufacturer's recommendations
    • Site-specific usage data (e.g., roaster operating hours)
    • Historical repair records
    • Food safety risk
  • Refer to:
    • SOP 7.1: Preventive Maintenance & Verification Procedure
    • SOP 1.6 & 1.7: Roasting Equipment PM Manuals
    • Equipment-specific records in Cropster and Maintenance Logs

6.2 Corrective / Unscheduled Maintenance

  • All repairs logged in:
    • Equipment Repair and Maintenance Record
  • Any missed PM must be logged in the Deviation Record and corrected as soon as identified.

6.3 Maintenance Work Requests

  • Logged via the Maintenance Work Request Form
  • Prioritized by QA and Maintenance Lead based on contamination risk and operational urgency.

Repair Materials and Restrictions

Allowed Prohibited
Black zip-ties (temporary fix) Duct tape, cardboard, string
Stainless parts, food-safe lubricants Makeshift/dirty parts
Food-grade mineral oil (e.g., for bearing lubrication) Wood-handled tools (unless sealed)

Temporary repairs:

  • Must be documented in Repair Log
  • Must not pose contamination risk
  • Monitored monthly via Interior Premises Inspection Record

Equipment Condition Inspections

Inspection Type Form Used
Daily – Cleanliness and readiness Pre-Operational Inspection Record
Weekly/Monthly PM PM Records (Roasting Manual, Section 5)
Interior Environment & Equipment Monthly Interior Premises Inspection Record

Roaster-Specific PM (G90, G120, L12)

Detailed procedures in:

  • SOP 1.6 – Roasting Manuals and Preventive Maintenance
  • SOP 1.7 – Fratello Preventative Maintenance

Covers:

  • Greasing zerks
  • Chaff removal (every 3–5 roasts)
  • Cooling tray cleaning
  • Burner cleaning
  • Exhaust duct and chimney maintenance (3rd-party)

Parts Storage Requirements

  • Critical parts for roasters are:
    • Stored in labeled bins
    • Bearings/gears in zip-seal bags
    • Rubber and silicone items in cool, dark storage
    • Grease/oils sealed, stored indoors

Deviations and Corrective Action

  • If PMs are missed, they are:
    • Logged during Internal Audit (SOP 1.3)
    • Followed by Corrective Action & Retraining
    • Recorded in the Deviation Procedure

If contamination is suspected:

  • Hold or discard product
  • Initiate Recall Procedure if risk warrants (see SOP 10.1)
