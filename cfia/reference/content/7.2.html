1.0 Purpose

To ensure all critical measuring and monitoring devices at Fratello Coffee Roasters are properly calibrated and verified for accuracy to maintain product quality, traceability, and compliance with the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR) – Part 4.

2.0 Scope

This SOP applies to all equipment and instruments used to monitor critical parameters, including but not limited to:

  • Scales (green intake, roaster load, packaging)
  • Timers
  • Thermometers / Temperature Probes
  • Moisture Analyzers
  • Labeling Printers / Date Coders

3.0 Responsibilities

Role Responsibilities
QA Coordinator Maintains calibration schedule and logs; investigates out-of-tolerance readings
Roaster & Packaging Leads Perform routine checks and notify QA of any irregularities
Maintenance Technician Conducts technical calibrations and corrective action follow-ups
Director of Operations Reviews deviations and approves escalation or equipment removal from use

4.0 Calibration Schedule

The Fratello Scale Calibration Schedule.xlsx outlines the frequency and method for each calibration activity. Devices are categorized as:

Type of Equipment Calibration Frequency
Digital floor and bench scales Monthly (internal check), Annually (certified 3rd-party)
Moisture meter (e.g., for green beans) Weekly check with reference samples
Thermometers (used for roasting QA) Monthly ice-water calibration (0°C)
CoPilot printer date verification Daily time/date validation by Packaging Operator
Internal timers (roasters, coolers) Quarterly, validated against digital stopwatch

5.0 Procedures

5.1 Scale Calibration

  • Use certified test weights (CSA Class F2 or higher)
  • Record pass/fail on the Scale Calibration Log
  • If out of tolerance, remove scale from use, tag it, and initiate corrective action

5.2 Moisture Meter Calibration

  • Check with sealed reference samples (e.g., 10.0% ±0.3%)
  • Log result in Moisture Verification Log
  • Recalibrate or replace batteries if reading drifts consistently

5.3 Thermometer Calibration

  • Use ice-water slurry method
  • Reading must be within ±1°C of 0°C
  • Failures require documentation and thermometer removal until resolved

5.4 CoPilot Printer (Date Code Verification)

  • Cross-check printed date/time daily at startup
  • Any error must be documented on the Printer Verification Checklist
  • Contact QA for software recalibration or hardware reset if persistent

7.0 Corrective Action

If calibration fails:

  • Tag equipment as “Do Not Use”
  • Record deviation on the Deviation Form
  • Notify Maintenance and QA
  • Verify impact on products produced since last known good reading
  • Initiate product hold or recall steps if food safety could be affected

9.0 Regulatory Alignment

  • SFCR s.50(2) – Measuring equipment must be accurate and verified
  • SFCR s.89 – Calibration and verification records must be maintained
  • CFIA PCP Guidance – Calibration of monitoring equipment is a required control under Preventive Controls
