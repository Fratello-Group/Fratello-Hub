Purpose

To outline the training procedures required for Fratello Coffee Roasters production staff to effectively identify and report food safety hazards during all production stages. This SOP ensures all employees are equipped to support hazard recognition and risk mitigation in compliance with SFCR Part 4 and section 89(1)(2)(3)(4)(5)(6).

Scope

This SOP applies to all production team members involved in green coffee loading, roasting, cooling, destoning, bin transfer, and packaging (including tea).

Training Objectives

  • Educate staff on types of hazards (biological, chemical, and physical)
  • Train staff on critical control points and preventive control measures
  • Enable team to identify, document, and escalate any hazard occurrence
  • Reinforce Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) and sanitation protocols

Training Content

Introduction to Food Safety Hazards

  • Definitions of biological, chemical, and physical hazards
  • Examples specific to Fratello's operations

Process Walkthrough with Hazards

  • Green coffee loading (screen/magnet/dust collector hazards)
  • Roasting (heat control, fire hazard)
  • Cooling and destoning (foreign object removal)
  • Packaging (magnet systems, labeling accuracy)
  • Tea packaging (cross-contamination, allergen control)

Use of Hazard Identification Forms (11.2)

  • How to record hazards, likelihood, severity, and controls
  • What triggers a CCP review

Escalation Protocols

  • Immediate reporting to supervisor
  • Documenting in daily check logs or deviation reports

Annual Re-Training

  • Scheduled reviews of hazard assessment and updated controls

Training Frequency

  • Upon hiring and onboarding
  • Annually for all production employees
  • After any change in process, product, or control measure

Training Delivery Methods

  • In-person instruction with QA Manager
  • Hands-on floor walkthrough
  • Use of visual flow diagrams and hazard tables (11.2)
  • Quiz and verbal Q&A to confirm understanding

Training Records

  • Documented in 8.3 Personnel Training Schedule & Tracking Program
  • Employee sign-off on understanding and competency
