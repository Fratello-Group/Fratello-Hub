Purpose

To verify that all food-contact and relevant non-food-contact equipment is in clean and sound condition before production begins. This prevents contamination and ensures all cleaning, sanitation, and maintenance activities have been properly completed, in accordance with CFIA's SFCR Part 4.

Scope

Applies to:

  • All production equipment used during manufacturing
  • Handwashing stations
  • Food-contact tools and utensils
  • Sanitation and inspection records tied to operational readiness

Responsibility

Role Responsibility
Maintenance Supervisor or Designate Conducts pre-op inspections daily and records findings
Sanitation Crew Reviews findings, corrects any issues, and signs off
All Production Employees May not start operations until Pre-Op is completed and cleared

Frequency

  • Daily, prior to production start, for any equipment in use that day

Procedure

1. Pre-Operational Inspection

  • Conduct visual inspection with a flashlight for each piece of equipment in use
  • Document on the Pre-Operational Inspection Record (mark "Not in Use" where applicable)

Inspect for the following:

Item Details
Chemical residues Soapy buildup, sanitizer residue (see updated Sanitation SOP)
Product residue Coffee particles, debris, oils or dust
Excess grease or leaking Check under and around equipment
Damage Rust, broken welds, loose parts, or rough food contact surfaces
Glass/brittle plastic Refer to Glass & Brittle Plastic SOP for hazard response
Knife/cutting blade condition Blades must be intact and accounted for
Handwashing stations Check for: warm water, soap, paper towels, signage, and garbage bins

2. Daily Cleaner Sign-Off

Before cleaning begins each day, cleaners must review the Pre-Operational Inspection Record from the prior day to:

  • Identify recurring problem areas (continuous improvement)
  • Complete any outstanding cleaning (e.g. non-contact surfaces deferred from previous shift)

Corrective Actions

Deviation Action Required
Dirty food-contact equipment Must be re-cleaned and/or re-sanitized before production begins; document in Pre-Op Record
Dirty non-food contact surface May be deferred if no food safety risk; document in Pre-Op Record and clean post-production
Broken or damaged food-contact equipment Must be repaired or replaced before production starts. Document and transfer to Maintenance Work Request Record
Knife/blade damage Collect and account for all broken pieces, dispose in sharps bin, check Daily Cleaning Record, and evaluate for product contamination (use Hold Procedure if needed)
Product contamination risk Follow Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product SOP and document on Corrective Action Record
Repeat issues Review cleaning protocols, observe staff methods, contact Cintas for chemical adjustment or support, and retrain as needed

Integrated SOPs & Resources

Document Purpose
Sanitation SOP Ensures pre-op is linked to cleaning effectiveness
Master Sanitation Schedule (MSS) Reference for cleaning frequency and equipment details
Glass & Brittle Plastic SOP If breakage is observed above product zones
Chemical Management Program (Cintas) For chemical residue management
Corrective Action Record Documents re-cleaning, repairs, retraining, or holds
Maintenance Work Request Record For deferred or unresolved repairs
Hold Product Procedure Required if contamination cannot be ruled out
Pre-Operational Inspection Record Completed daily by the inspector and signed by cleaner

Verification

  • Daily: Inspection record reviewed by Maintenance Supervisor or designate
  • Monthly: Random Pre-Op record audit during internal verification review
  • Annually: Evaluated as part of Fratello's Internal Audit Program and reviewed for continuous improvement
