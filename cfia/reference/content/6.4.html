Purpose

To prevent physical contamination of coffee, packaging, and ingredients by glass, brittle plastic, or hard plastic fragments, which are serious physical hazards under SFCR. This procedure outlines the control, inspection, and corrective actions required in case of breakage.

Scope

Applies to all production, storage, receiving, and shipping areas where glass or brittle plastics are present, including lights, gauges, sight glasses, and required instruments.

Responsibility

  • Warehouse Manager / Maintenance Supervisor: Ensure monthly inspections and lead breakage response
  • All Employees: Immediately report breakages and comply with containment protocol

Frequency

  • Monthly: Formal inspection and documentation
  • As Needed: Immediate action in case of breakage

Procedure

1. Prevention and Control of Glass & Brittle Plastics

Area Control Measures
General No glass items permitted in production (except safety glasses or prescription eyewear).
Inventory Maintain a list of all essential glass/brittle plastic items in the Glass & Brittle Plastic Inspection Record.
Packaging & Ingredients Glass-packaged ingredients must be transferred to non-breakable containers before entering production.
Lighting All fixtures must be shatterproof or fitted with protective covers.
Cleaning Lighting maintenance must be done during non-production or with all product and packaging removed or covered.
Inspections Monthly checks for integrity, damage, and inventory accuracy using the Premises Inspection Procedure. Update the inventory list as needed.

2. Breakage Response & Cleanup Procedure

If breakage occurs:

Step Key Actions
1 Immediately stop production. Notify Warehouse Manager or Maintenance Supervisor.
2 Determine and isolate affected area. Place all exposed ingredients, packaging, or product on hold (use Hold Tags per Hold Procedure).
3 Carefully remove large pieces by hand (with gloves), then sweep or vacuum the area. Dispose of material in a sealed trash container.
4 Clean affected equipment/surfaces with cleaning solution or water. Use a flashlight to verify no fragments remain.
5 Inspect and clean/discard all tools used in cleanup (e.g., brooms, gloves, vacuum nozzles). Inspect shoes.
6 Resume production only after sign-off by Warehouse Manager or trained designate.
7 Complete the Corrective Action Record detailing the incident, response, and verification of cleanup.

Training

  • Employees are trained during onboarding and annually on:
    • Reporting breakages
    • Contamination control
    • Use of PPE and inspection tools during cleanup
