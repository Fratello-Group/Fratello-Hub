Purpose

To ensure that food, packaging, ingredients, and chemicals are transported, received, and stored in a manner that prevents contamination, supports traceability, and meets CFIA's Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR), specifically sections 47, 72–74.

Scope

This program applies to all conveyances (company-owned, contract carriers, and customer vehicles), inbound and outbound goods, and storage of food, packaging, and chemicals at the Fratello facility.

B.1 Food Conveyances (SFCR ss. 72–73)

Conveyance Requirements

  • Must be clean, free from damage, off-odours, pests, incompatible items (e.g., chemicals, unpackaged food, animals).
  • Must maintain ambient conditions to preserve coffee quality.
  • Must be inspected before loading/unloading.
  • Interior lights must be shielded, and seals/gaskets intact.

Refer to SOPs:

  • Fratello Receiving Procedure
  • Fratello Shipping Procedure
  • Fratello Combined Shipping & Receiving Procedure

Carrier Assurance

  • Third-party carriers must provide annual letters of assurance verifying cleanliness and suitability for food transport.

On-Site Monitoring

Who What
Shipper/Receiver or designate Inspect carriers (interior, condition, odours, pests, debris). Record on Transport Log.
Warehouse Coordinator Ensure carrier cleaning is recorded. Maintain approved carrier list. Review non-compliances.

B.2 Purchasing, Receiving & Storage (SFCR s.47 & s.74)

B.2.1 Purchasing & Receiving

Requirements:

  • All materials must come from approved suppliers.
  • All incoming goods are reviewed for:
    • Cleanliness, no damage, no pests.
    • Proper documentation (PO, invoice, lot #s).
    • Appropriate packaging and labeling.

Returned Product:

  • Generally not accepted; if received, must be held in Hold Area and managed per the Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product Procedure.

Refer to SOPs:

  • Fratello Receiving Procedure
  • Supplier Approval Program
  • Hold Product Procedure

Monitoring

Who What
Receiver/Designate Verify PO, inspect shipment, assign pallet tags, and document all entries on the Receiving/Shipping Log.
Warehouse Coordinator Review completeness of supplier paperwork and oversee corrective actions.

B.2.2 Storage (SFCR s.74)

Storage Requirements:

  • Ingredients, packaging, and products are stored:
    • Off the ground
    • Separated by risk (chemicals away from food/packaging)
    • According to FIFO rotation
  • Chemicals:
    • Must be labeled, stored in designated, locked chemical room
    • Reviewed monthly and expired items disposed

Finished Goods:

  • Ambient storage
  • Labeled per Recall & Traceability Program
  • Tracked in Cropster and tagged with lot codes

Monitoring

Who What
Shipper/Receiver Follow Transportation and Storage Procedures for inventory rotation and segregation.
Warehouse Coordinator Inspect storage areas monthly using the Premises Inspection Record. Oversee chemical handling training.

Corrective Actions & Deviations

  • All deviations are documented on the Corrective Action Record.
  • Product may be rejected, returned, or placed on hold depending on severity.
  • Training or retraining conducted if improper handling is found.

Verification Activities

  • On-site verification follows the Verification Procedure.
  • Periodic record audits ensure SOPs and CFIA requirements are met.

Records Referenced

  • Hold Log
