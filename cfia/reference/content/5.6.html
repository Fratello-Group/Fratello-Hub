1. Purpose

To ensure that all raw materials, in-process items, and finished products are traceable from origin to customer, enabling rapid and effective product recall or investigation in case of a food safety concern, as required by the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR), Part 5 & Section 89.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

  • Green coffee beans
  • Roasted and ground coffee
  • Packaging materials
  • Finished packaged coffee
  • Returned or non-conforming product

3. Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
Receiving Personnel Record incoming LOT numbers, suppliers, and product data in Transport Log.
Roasters / Production Staff Ensure LOTs are recorded in Cropster and physical labels match production logs.
QA / Traceability Coordinator Ensure full forward and backward traceability; conduct mock recalls and verify record completeness.
Warehouse Team Maintain Hold Log and ensure proper tagging of quarantined product.

4. Procedure

4.1 Incoming Traceability ("One Step Back")

  • All raw materials and packaging are received per SOP-PCP-1.1a.
  • At receiving:
    • Supplier, product name, LOT number, BOL, quantity, and condition are logged in the Transport Log.
    • Green beans are tagged and recorded on the Silo Inventory Board, chamber magnets, and entered into Cropster.
    • Packaging and other materials are assigned traceable identifiers and stored appropriately.

4.2 Internal Traceability

  • During roasting:
    • Roasters select LOTs and link them to Cropster Roast Profiles and equipment (e.g., G90, G120, L12, Roest sample).
    • QA records roast values and assigns Cropster-generated LOT numbers (PR codes) to finished batches.
  • During grinding:
    • Ground coffee is recorded in the Grinding Log with reference to original roasted batch LOT number.
    • Ground totes are tagged and held in WIP area for traceability and packaging.
  • In Odoo (Manufacturing Module):
    • Assembly builds link input LOTs (green/roasted beans) to output LOTs (finished product) for full trace-forward/back capability.
    • All roasted coffee is linked to its Cropster PR code via the 'Lot/Serial Number' field.

4.3 Finished Product Traceability ("One Step Forward")

  • At packaging and shipping:
    • Packaged product is tagged with Cropster LOT number and roast date.
    • Shipping records capture customer, product name, quantity, and LOT number for each shipment.
    • All records are stored in QuickBooks, Cropster, and Shipping Logs.

4.4 Non-Conforming and Returned Product

  • Returned or damaged items:
    • Are moved to a designated Hold area.
    • Logged in the Hold Log and tagged with reason for hold and LOT number.
    • Evaluated by QA prior to any disposition decision.

4.5 Recordkeeping & Recall Readiness

  • Records include:
    • Receiving logs
    • Silo inventory boards
    • Cropster roast and grinding logs
    • Odoo assembly builds
    • Shipping/invoice records
    • Hold and corrective action logs
  • Traceability records are retrievable within 24 hours in case of CFIA request or product recall scenario.

5. Corrective Actions

Issue Action
Missing LOT number Hold product, investigate and trace back. Document deviation.
Incorrect LOT tagging Correct label, update records, and retrain staff.
Unable to link product forward/backward Investigate record gaps, conduct mock recall, retrain staff.

6. Relevant Records and Systems

  • Transport & Receiving Logs
  • Cropster Roasting & Grinding Logs
  • Odoo Assembly & Build Sheets
  • Daily Roast Tracking Sheet
  • Inventory Board & Silo Chamber Magnets
  • Hold Log
  • Shipping and Invoicing Records
  • Corrective Action Records

7. CFIA Compliance Notes

SFCR Requirement Fratello Compliance
Part 5 – Traceability Meets "one step forward / one step back" traceability for all food and packaging.
s. 89 – Retention of records All records retrievable within 24 hours; backed up in digital systems (Cropster, Odoo).
Product Recall Preparedness Ready to support recall investigations and lot isolations efficiently.
