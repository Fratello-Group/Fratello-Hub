Purpose

To ensure that all vehicles used for transporting ingredients, packaging, and finished products are inspected prior to loading or unloading to prevent contamination, damage, or deterioration, as required under the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR s.72–73).

Scope

Applies to all incoming and outgoing shipments delivered by:

  • Third-party carriers
  • Customer vehicles
  • Fratello-operated transport

Includes vehicles used to deliver or pick up food, food-contact packaging, or any materials entering food production/storage areas.

Responsibility

  • Shipper/Receiver: Completes inspection and documents on Form 2.1.01
  • Warehouse Manager: Verifies form accuracy and responds to non-compliance or deviations
  • Director of Operations: Conducts periodic verification reviews

Frequency

  • Per Shipment: Before loading or unloading any vehicle.

Procedure

Step Key Actions Food Safety Considerations
1 Complete Form 2.1.01 – Conveyance Vehicle Inspection before any goods are received or shipped. Ensures standardized documentation for every shipment.
2 Inspect vehicle exterior: clean, free from damage, weather-tight. Prevents environmental contamination and physical hazards.
3 Inspect interior compartment for cleanliness, off-odours, debris, pests, and signs of damage (e.g. rust, pooled water, sharp edges). Required under SFCR s.73 for maintaining a clean, safe transport environment.
4 Check for non-compatible items (e.g. chemicals, unpackaged food, animal transport history). Prevents cross-contamination of food or packaging.
5 Review cargo stacking and condition (if pre-loaded): no crushed boxes, open containers, or unsafe stacking. Prevents physical damage and ensures product integrity.
6 If any deviations are found, document in comment section and report to Supervisor immediately. Early identification and corrective action mitigate food safety risk.
7 Ensure all details are completed: shipment date, driver name/signature, truck ID, shipper/receiver name/signature. Traceability and accountability.

Deviation Management

If the vehicle does not pass inspection:

  • Do not proceed with loading/unloading.
  • Notify the Warehouse Manager.
  • Record findings in the Corrective Action Record and note on Form 2.1.01.
  • If contamination is suspected, implement the Hold Product Procedure.
