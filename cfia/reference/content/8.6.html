1.0 Purpose

To ensure all employee injuries are promptly reported, assessed, and documented, with a focus on maintaining both personal safety and food safety integrity. This procedure also ensures regulatory compliance with SFCR regarding contamination control and employee health management.

2.0 Scope

This SOP applies to all employees, contractors, and visitors at Fratello Coffee Roasters who experience an injury or incident within the facility or on the premises, particularly in or near food handling or storage areas.

3.0 Responsibilities

RoleResponsibility
Injured IndividualImmediately report incident to supervisor; avoid further contact with food/product areas
SupervisorInitiate incident report, secure area, notify QA and Operations Manager
QA CoordinatorAssess food safety implications, initiate corrective actions, document impact on product
Director of OperationsAuthorize further investigation and preventive measures if needed

4.0 Definitions

  • Injury – Any cut, puncture, burn, strain, or other bodily harm sustained at work
  • Major Injury – Requires external medical treatment or time off
  • Minor Injury – Treatable onsite, does not impact work or require external care
  • Food Safety Impact – Any chance that blood, bodily fluid, or material from the injury may have come into contact with product, packaging, or food contact surfaces

5.0 Procedure

5.1 Immediate Response

  • Provide first aid or medical support as needed
  • Stop all production in impacted area if applicable
  • Apply appropriate PPE and isolate any contaminated product

5.2 Incident Reporting

  • Complete Injury Incident Report (Form 8.6a) within 2 hours
  • Submit to QA and Director of Operations
  • Include:
    • Time, date, location
    • Type and cause of injury
    • Food/product contact details (if applicable)
    • Witnesses and corrective actions taken

5.3 Food Safety Impact Assessment

  • QA evaluates whether any food contact or contamination occurred
  • Initiate Product Hold if necessary (see SOP 9.4)
  • Conduct cleaning and sanitizing of affected areas as per SOP 6.1

5.4 Corrective & Preventive Actions

  • If injury was equipment-related: inspect and repair equipment
  • If GMP or training issue: provide refresher or corrective training
  • Preventive action logged in QA system for tracking

6.0 Records and Forms

  • 8.6a Injury Incident Report (fillable form)
  • Deviation Reports (if applicable)
  • Cleaning/Sanitation Logs (if applicable)
  • Corrective Action Reports
