Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to define the methods to maintain critical equipment to ensure such equipment does not impact product quality or delivery schedules.

Definitions

  • Corrective Maintenance — Can be defined as a maintenance task performed to identify, isolate, and rectify a fault so that the failed equipment, machine, or asset can be restored to an operational condition within the tolerances or limits established for in-service operations.
  • Critical Parts — Is a part, the failure of which could have a catastrophic effect upon the rotocraft, and for which critical characteristics have been identified which must be controlled to ensure the required level of integrity.
  • Key Process Equipment — Is a piece of equipment that is a critical path for the product or service being offered or provided. The equipment would be considered essential and absolutely necessary in order to deliver the product being produced or the service being offered.
  • Preventive Maintenance — Is maintenance that is regularly performed on a piece of equipment to lessen the likelihood of it failing. It is performed while the equipment is still working so that it does not break down unexpectedly.

Scope

Preventive maintenance only applies to "key process equipment"; these are defined as those devices or tooling that are critical for maintaining continuing process capability (i.e., uninterrupted manufacturing flow), and those machines and systems that the breakdown of which could adversely impact product quality.

Emphasis shall be put on preventive maintenance (P/M), designed to ensure equipment operates without unexpected down time or error. Correcting a fault in a machine after it breaks is considered repair, and not maintenance. The purpose of a robust P/M program is to eliminate the need for unscheduled repairs and down time.

Responsibilities

The Production Manager is responsible for identifying key process equipment, machines, and systems to be included in the preventive maintenance program. The Production Manager will ensure preventive maintenance records are maintained for each unique piece of key process equipment. This record will contain, at a minimum, the following information:

  • Type of device
  • Manufacturer
  • Model number
  • Serial number / company asset number
  • Location
  • P/M tasks required
  • Frequency of each P/M task (monthly, weekly, annually, before use, etc.)

Maintenance requirements shall be developed for all critical equipment in our production facility and shall be based on:

  • Manufacturers recommended practice,
  • industry experience,
  • applicable regulatory requirements, and
  • relevant industry standards

Maintenance work may be performed by employees or approved third party maintenance service providers, as needed. If third party providers are used, the provider's maintenance records may be maintained in lieu of any company internal records.

In order to identify equipment problems at an early stage and to prevent breakdowns, process equipment operators are instructed to monitor tool wear, process performance, vibrations, etc., and report any abnormal functioning to their supervisor or the Production Manager directly.

Procedure

Equipment shall be operated and maintained with due care and attention to ensure:

  • The safety of personnel,
  • Protection of the facility, and
  • To maximize equipment availability, reliability and service life.

Sound operating practice shall be employed by operators of all Fratello equipment. Maintenance practices shall be primarily focused on monitoring equipment operating parameters and wear condition to schedule effective equipment repair and or replacement activities to prevent unscheduled equipment breakdown or unavailability.

Preventive maintenance (P/M) tasks shall be based on manufacturer's guidelines, but may be overridden or altered to suit the company's specific needs, based on equipment usage, criticality to quality, etc.

The resulting records may take the form of logs, procedure(s), databases, spreadsheets or other methods as deemed appropriate by the Production Manager.

Records of completed P/M tasks must be maintained. P/M records must show:

  • the completion of the required P/M step,
  • the operator responsible for completing the step,
  • the date of completion, and
  • any notes or problems encountered.

For P/M tasks that are done daily, hourly, "before use" or at a more frequent basis, the need for a record is not required. Records must be maintained for any task performed at a frequency of weekly or greater by using the Inspection Log.

Equipment specific (make and model) maintenance standards shall establish:

  • Maintenance Inspection basis, i.e. time (calendar) use (e.g. hours, tonnage produced, etc.)
  • Maintenance Inspection schedule, i.e. time/use periods to first and subsequent inspection
  • Maintenance Inspection routine, i.e. detailed inspection steps
  • Critical operating parameter and wear measurements
  • Limits for operating parameter and wear, based on OEM and/or Fratello standards
  • Measuring equipment requirements for operating parameter and wear measurement, e.g. heat gun, calipers etc.
  • Measuring and recording responsibilities, i.e. mechanic, electrician, technician, etc.
  • Lubrication type and monitoring requirements, i.e. type and acceptable lube manufacturers, and oil sample analysis requirements
  • Cooling type and monitoring requirements, i.e. type and acceptable cooling additive/inhibitors, and sample analysis requirements

When new equipment is introduced to the operation, the manufacturers maintenance guidelines shall be used as the interim maintenance standard.

Equipment specific maintenance inspection schedules shall be compiled into a master maintenance inspection schedule.

Maintenance records shall be retained for a period of at least three years.

Equipment maintained on usage basis shall have effective means of tracing usage, e.g. hour meters, ton-mile log book, etc.

Warehouse Storage of Critical Parts

Given the unique nature of the roasters and inability to purchase and obtain certain critical parts in a timely manner, the Production Manager will ensure that parts identified as critical be maintained in storage and be readily available when needed. The storage location must be:

  • Clean, organized with parts clearly marked by a unique identifier such as an accounting code.
  • All bearings and gears must be protected using zip-lock bags.
  • All rubber goods should be stored in a cool, dark place.
  • Grease and other lubricants should be moved to inside storage, with all tops sealed and secured.

Roaster Specific Procedures

Prior to starting any P/M activities, the health and safety of Fratello employees is of the utmost concern. For all work on roasters, employees are required to have nitrile gloves to minimize exposure to lubricants. It is also required that employees wait at least 10 minutes after shutting the roasters down to allow for cooling time.

G90 Roaster

Tools Required:

  • Grease Gun with High Temp Food Safe Grease
  • Bristle Brush
  • Vacuum
  • Broom and Dust Pan
  • Hoe

This procedure must be completed and documented once each week of roasting time.

  • Ensure roaster is turned off.
  • Grease Both Zerks shown in the photo below. Do not remove the bearing cover. Wipe excess grease from zerk with cloth.
  • Grease the 2 bearings on the main shaft. Wipe excess grease with cloth.
  • Grease Both Motors. Wipe excess grease from zerks.
  • Using the Bristle Brush and the vacuum, Scrub the Cooling Tray to clear the perforations of coffee bean Build Up. A solution of Purocaff and Hot water may be used but must be thoroughly rinsed before use.
  • Remove and Wash the Air Intake and the upper (1) and lower (2) Afterburner Filters. Place on Rack at compressor Exhaust to Dry. Allow ½ hour to dry before replacing.
  • Using the Bristle Brush and the vacuum, Scrub the Cooling Tray to clear the perforations of coffee bean Build Up. A solution of Purocaff and Hot water may be used but must be thoroughly rinsed before use.
  • Open access Panels below cooling tray and hub to remove debris.

G120 Roaster

Tools Required:

  • ½" Wrench
  • Grease Gun with High Temp Food Safe Grease
  • Bristle Brush
  • Vacuum
  • Broom and Dust Pan
  • Hoe

This procedure must be completed and documented every eight (8) hours of roasting time.

  • Using the ½" wrench, remove the 4 bolts on the front Bearing Cap as shown in picture above.
  • Inspect Roller Bearings for wear. Look for loose/missing rollers.
  • Pump a bead of High Temp Grease onto the Roller Bearings and press the grease firmly into the Roller Bearings with your finger tip.
  • Inspect the Bearing Cap for Metal Contamination. Clean Cap of old grease and Replace onto Roaster. Snug the ½" bolts. Don't Over Tighten!
  • Blow Down and Lube the Fan Motor Located at the Rear of the Roaster. Wipe zerk with cloth.
  • Using the Bristle Brush and the vacuum, Scrub the Cooling Tray to clear the perforations of coffee bean Build Up. A solution of Purocaff and Hot water may be used but must be thoroughly rinsed before use.
  • Open the Access Panels (3 of them) under the cooling table and remove debris. Be sure to secure the panels after debris removal to ensure proper table function.
  • Open the Lower Panel on the Hub of the cooling table and remove debris.

L12 Roaster

Tools Required:

  • Grease Gun with High Temp Food Safe Grease
  • Bristle Brush
  • Vacuum
  • Broom and Dust Pan

This procedure must be completed and documented every eight (8) hours of roasting time.

  • Using a Standard Screwdriver, remove the 3 screws from the Bearing Cap.
  • Inspect Roller Bearing for wear. Clean and Press High Temp Grease into Roller Bearing. Replace cap and screws. Don't Over Tighten.
  • Grease and blow down Drive Motor located in the back panel.

Appendices

G90 Operating the Roaster

Before you start to roast:

  • Empty chaff collector. Pictured below.
  • Inspect the cooling pan. If the screen is clogged use compressed air and/or wire brush to clean it.
  • Make visual inspection of all system components. Check for cracks in gas and electrical lines, all buttons and switches are intact etc.

Warm-up procedure

To start the Probat G90, use the touch screen control panel.

  • Push the "Run Process Motors" button.
  • Push the "Main Burner Start" button.
  • Push the "Afterburner Start" button.
  • Wait for 5 minute purge cycle to complete. This will automatically start once the burners are running.
  • Close the oven door and let the drum warm up for 15 minutes.

The Probat G90 is ready to operate.

Roasting

To roast a batch on the Probat G90:

  • Load green coffee into holding silo located above the roaster.
  • Once the desired loading temperature is reached, push the "Batch Start" button.
  • Set the valve to the appropriate setting for the batch size and roast profile.
  • Set FBT and ET2 to desired temperatures, this is 240 to insure G90 roasting software does not try to regulate temperature before. This will ensure we have manual control.
  • Once within 1 minute of completion of the roast, push the "Stir On" and the "blower Start" buttons.
  • Press the "Open Oven" button once the batch reaches FBT.

NOTE: Empty the chaff collector at least every fifteenth roast.

Cooling the roaster

  • Leave the machine running after the last load has been dropped.
  • Leave the drum doors and the main gate open.
  • Push "Enable Cool down" Button, Note: If the afterburner has not been functioning properly the enable cool down button will not turn off the main burner, in this case press Main burner 1 button in bottom right hand corner.

Important things to watch for!

  • Using probe to monitor progress of roasting is essential even though there is a group of temperature sensors controlling the whole process. On rare occasions small fragments of drum's inner build-up or other artifacts may get loose and fall into the thermocouple funnel obstructing or stopping passage of beans causing falsely low bean temperature readout and possibly burned roast, or in case of French roast even a fire.
  • Chaff collector needs to emptied at least every fifteenth roast. Failing to perform this operation can dangerously increase the risk of fire or explosion in the chaff collector and exhaust duct causing its meltdown.

Maintenance

Chaff collector

  • Empty the chaff collector every fifteenth roast. Accumulated chaff presents a serious fire hazard.
  • Periodically open the inspection door on top of exhaust duct (directly above the drum) and check the condition of thermocouple sensor. If necessary, speak with head roaster if there are any issues. Sand paper or knife may be used to remove any buildups present.

Roaster

  • Lubricate all bearings and gears at least one a week (preferably on Friday at the end of the day or first thing in the morning on Monday).
  • Exhaust ducts and chimney. This is done by a 3rd party.
  • Disassemble and clean the exhaust ducts as well as the chimney twice a year. Use of pneumatic hammer might be necessary. This is done by a 3rd party.
  • Afterburner. No maintenance required. If afterburner doesn't want to ignite, contact head roaster.

Cooling pan

  • Clean the cooling pan every morning using compressed air and wire brush if necessary.
  • Every month open the inspection doors and remove accumulated debris and broken beans in the space below cooling screen.

Roasting

To roast a batch on the Probat G120:

  • Once Roaster reaches 210 degrees you may start roasting.
  • Load green coffee into holding silo located above the roaster. Ensure G90 Control panel is set to Silo 1 for loading into the G120.
  • Once the desired loading temperature is reached pull lever on roasting hopper to release coffee into roasting drum.
  • Turn on proper burners for Batch size and Profile (Usually this is Aux burner, Secondary Burner and Main Burner) Profiles are found in Cropster.
  • Once within 1 minute of completion of the roast, turn the "Stir arm starts" and the "Cooling fan" switch on.
  • Manually open the oven door once the batch reaches FBT.

NOTE: Empty the chaff collector at least every third roast.

Cooling the roaster

  • Leave the machine running after the last load has been dropped.
  • Leave the drum doors and the main gate open.
  • Turn off main burner, DO NOT turn off the drum until temperature is below 50 degrees.

Important things to watch for!

  • Using probe to monitor progress of roasting is essential even though there is a group of temperature sensors controlling the whole process. On rare occasions small fragments of drum's inner build-up or other artifacts may get loose and fall into the thermocouple funnel obstructing or stopping passage of beans causing falsely low bean temperature readout and possibly burned roast, or in case of French roast even a fire.
  • Chaff collector needs to emptied at least every Third roast. Failing to perform this operation can dangerously increase the risk of fire or explosion in the chaff collector and exhaust duct causing its meltdown.

Please refer to Roasting Fire Response Plan.

Chaff collector.

  • Empty the chaff collector every 3rd roast. Accumulated chaff presents a serious fire hazard.
  • Periodically open the inspection door on top of exhaust duct (directly above the drum) and check the condition of thermocouple sensor. If necessary, speak with head roaster if there are any issues. Sand paper or knife may be used to remove any buildups present.

Roaster.

  • Exhaust ducts and chimney. This is done by a 3rd party.
  • Disassemble and clean the exhaust ducts as well as the chimney twice a year. Use of pneumatic hammer might be necessary. This is done by a 3rd party.

Afterburner. No maintenance required. If afterburner doesn't want to ignite, contact head roaster.

Cooling pan.

  • Clean the cooling pan every morning using compressed air and wire brush if necessary.
  • Every month open the inspection doors and remove accumulated debris and broken beans in the space below cooling screen.
