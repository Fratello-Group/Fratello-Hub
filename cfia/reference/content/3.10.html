Purpose

To ensure consistent, accurate, and standardized evaluation of all green and roasted coffees through cupping, aligned with Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) standards, and integrated with Cropster for traceability and quality assurance.

Scope

This procedure applies to all green sample assessments, production batch evaluations, and daily quality assurance cuppings at Fratello Coffee Roasters.

Responsibilities

  • Quality Assurance (QA): Oversees all cupping operations and documentation.
  • Head Roaster: Ensures samples are roasted and presented per protocol.
  • Cupping Team: Participates in calibrated sensory analysis.

Frequency

  • Daily: Production roast cuppings (previous day's roasts)
  • Weekly: QA calibration and training cuppings
  • As Needed: New sample evaluation, issue investigation, profile discovery

Cupping Setup (Per SCA Standards)

  • Water: 92.2–94.4°C; Clean, odor-free, SCA compliant TDS (150 mg/L)
  • Ratio: 8.25g coffee / 150mL water (1.63g per 1oz if adjusting)
  • Vessels: Identical tempered glass or ceramic (7–9 fl oz)
  • Grind: 70-75% pass through 20 mesh sieve
  • Roast: Between 8-12 mins, cupped 8-24 hrs post-roast
  • Roast Level Target: Agtron Gourmet 63.0 ± 1.0 (Colortrack 62.0 ± 1.0)
  • Room: 110+ sq ft; 36" perimeter space; neutral odor

Cropster Integration

6.1 Sample Management

  • Log all green samples upon arrival in Cropster (Importer, Lot #, Origin, Process, Moisture)
  • Assign Sample IDs and track through roasting and cupping
  • Use Sample Management > "Take Sample" and "Transfer to Inventory" for accepted coffees

6.2 Roasting Integration

  • Roast samples on Roest or production machines (G90, G120, L12)
  • Link roast curve to Sample or Production Lot in Roasting Intelligence (RI)
  • Mark cupping batches as "For QC" to tag and group them

6.3 Lot Tracking

  • Lot # is assigned at receipt and carried through:
    • Cropster Sample → Green Inventory → Roasting → Roasted Batch → Cupping
  • Ensure roasted samples reference correct Lot # in Cropster for traceability

6.4 Quality Module

  • Log all cupping scores using Cropster Cup or manual form upload
  • Record:
    • Fragrance/Aroma, Flavor, Acidity, Body, Balance, Uniformity, Clean Cup, Sweetness, Overall
    • Defects and notes
    • Final score
  • Use Sensorial History for trend tracking per lot and roast profile

Cupping Procedure

  • Define cupping type (e.g., Discovery, Production QA, Profile Tuning)
  • Prepare coffees per SCA ratio, grind, and vessel standards
  • Set cupping table with labels and reference numbers (no identifiers)
  • Evaluate dry fragrance
  • Pour water; allow 4 mins steeping
  • Break crust at 4 mins, smell aroma
  • Skim foam, begin tasting at 6-10 mins
  • Score on Cropster or SCA form; log into Cropster QC

Calibration & Training

  • Weekly calibration session led by QA
  • Tongue mapping for new cuppers (optional)
  • Ongoing feedback and review of results

Corrective Actions

  • Coffees <80 points or with repeated defects: trigger review
  • Out-of-spec production roasts: flagged for re-roast or halt of lot
  • Issues logged in Cropster and communicated to production team

Recordkeeping & Review

  • All cupping results archived in Cropster
  • Maintain physical backup of forms for 12 months
  • Review cupping data monthly for trends and consistency

Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
