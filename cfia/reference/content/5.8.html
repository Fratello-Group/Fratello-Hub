Purpose

To ensure that roasted coffee and ground beans are packed correctly according to customer specifications and to ensure that traceability of inventory of finished products are maintained.

Responsibility

Packaging employees or trained alternate

Frequency

Daily (when in operation)

Important Information

  • Pre-labelling activities should be performed prior to packing operations. This could sometimes happen at the end of a production or work shift prior to the next day.

Procedure

Sealing and Boxing Machines

  • At the start of the shift and prior to packing operations, plug in and turn on the sealing machine.
  • Once on, check that the settings on the machine is correct. The machine is pre-set and the two sets of temperature required for sealing the bags are indicated on the machine.
Bag type Speed Setting Temperature Setting
Fratello, Analog, White 480 230
Clear 700 215
  • Turn on the box making machine, which also powers on the conveyor belt and flip on the switch for the printer.
  • Leave the machine on and allow to heat up for at least 30 minutes. When the machine is heated up, packing operations can begin.

Pre-labelling Activities

  • The Warehouse Manager prepares the schedule for Daily Production Record based on orders received.
  • Packaging employees will review the production record and select the appropriate packaging materials (bags and boxes) needed to fulfill the required orders.
  • Place the product label stickers on the product bags. These labels contain the product name and description as well as the date code.
  • For customers that require UPC code on the product pack, print and attach to the product pack. Depending on the customer requirements/specifications, place the UPC labels on the back or bottom of the product pack.

Note: A wide range of customer packing requirements have been pasted in the packaging department for reference purposes. This will be reviewed and updated periodically as per customer specifications.

Transfer

  • Based on the product to be packed, select the appropriate cart from the Work In Progress (W.I.P) storage area. Ensure the product details indicated on the Daily Coffee Tag matches product to be packed.
  • Place cart in position and connect the suction hose.

Packing

  1. Select the corresponding program on the machine based on products to be packed.
  2. There are two selections based on the pack sizes. The larger bucket is used for the 2lb and 5lbs bags while the smaller bucket is used for the 10oz, 12oz and 16oz and 1lb bags.
  3. There is an interchangeable air line that goes into each bucket which is located on a hook at the side of the machine (Note: there are two bowls to set these buckets in place). Ensure that the airline is plugged into the correct bucket as required.
  4. Once the air line is connected, adjust the product outlet gate according to the product being packed. These are clearly marked on the machine.
  5. To fill bags from the larger bucket (2lb and 5lb bags):
    • i) From the main control panel, press the 'RECALL PROGRAM' button
    • ii) Press the 'ZERO'/'TARE' button and then
    • iii) Press '2' button for 2lb OR press the '5' button for 5lb
    • iv) Press the 'RUN/STOP' button

15b. To fill bags from the smaller bucket (12oz bags):

  • i) From the main control panel, press the 'RECALL PROGRAM' button
  • ii) Press the 'ZERO'/'TARE' button and then
  • iii) Press '1' and '2' buttons
  • iv) Press the 'RUN/STOP' button

15c. To fill bags from the smaller bucket (1lb bags):

  • i) From the main control panel, press the 'RECALL PROGRAM' button
  • ii) Press the 'ZERO'/'TARE' button and then
  • iii) Press '1' and '6' buttons
  • iv) Press the 'RUN/STOP' button

Note: After the required program selection has been made, a tare may be required to ensure the number is accurate. Press the 'ZERO'/'TARE' button for this.

  1. Hold the bag to the product outlet chute and use one foot to press the foot pedal attached to the machine.
  2. The pre-weighed product will be automatically dispensed from the bucket into the product bag. This is then manually passed through the sealing machine (see the steps below).
  3. For weight accuracy, a manual check of the product pack is done after filling every 15 bags. If the weight is found to be inconsistent or inaccurate, empty the bucket and reset or restart the machine and follow through Steps 11 – 17.
  4. After packing operations have been completed, all left over product must be emptied out from the bucket into bags. To do this, press the 'RUN/STOP' button and '5' button. If this is less than the bag size being packed, the bag is stored for the next run (of the same product type).

Sealing

  1. Using the crank, adjust the height of the conveyor on the sealing machine to the corresponding marked line indicated on the machine for product bags to be sealed.
  2. Fold the top of the product bag evenly and slide into the groove.
  3. Product bag is then automatically conveyed through the sealer and sealed. Sealed bags exit the other end of the machine and is then manually transferred for boxing (see steps below).

Boxing

  1. Using the Daily Production record, select the appropriate boxes for the sealed product bags.
  2. To set the machine:
    • i) Select the mark on the graduated metal
    • ii) Using the crank, adjust the machine to the box size/width selected
    • iii) Looking directly into the machine, adjust the length to correspond in position with the side of the box
    • iv) Turn the lever counter clockwise to loosen and adjust the size of the box (length) using the indicator and ruler
    • v) Turn the lever clockwise to tighten into position.
  3. Once all the settings above have been completed, the boxes are ready to be formed.
    • i) Following the marks indicated, put the box into the machine and adjust if necessary
    • ii) If all settings are correct, the box will be formed by holding the box down in the machine
  4. After the box is formed, the sealed product bags are properly arranged into the box with the required quantity as per customer specifications.
  5. Filled boxes are then ready to be passed through the tape and printing machine (see steps below).

Taping

  1. To set the taping machine:
    • i) Adjust the arm of the machine to the size and width of the box to be taped. The crank on the side of the machine is used to adjust the width.
    • ii) The top arm is a manual lift up, push this down to the height of the box to be taped.

Note: Tapes used for boxes are either clear or Analog branded tape depending on the customer specifications. For Analog branded tapes, the tape will be manually installed on the boxes and the top arm of the machine is lifted out of the way so that the boxes are not clear taped.

  1. Once all settings have been completed, the boxes put through the machine will be taped.
  2. Taped boxes automatically go through the conveyor belt for printing.

Printing

  1. Turn on the machine.
  2. Select the 'file' picture button (colour yellow).
  3. Select the Customer Profile. This contains information on product types and boxing requirements.
  4. Select the product type to be boxed and then press the Start button to run the printer.

Important Note: To ensure printing is legible, the space between the printer head and the boxer (distance) needs to be about an inch and a half. Speed of the machine is set between 30 and 35.

Palletize/Storage

  1. Customer labelled boxes are transferred onto pallets.
  2. All other Fratello packed products (which are not boxed) are placed in clean blue cases per product type, labelled and dated.

Build Assembly

  1. Once packing operations have been completed, the build assembly activities will be carried out on the computer in the packaging area. To do this:
    • i) Open QuickBooks
    • ii) Insert username and password provided
    • iii) On the Build Assembly page:
      • Type in the Item Code in the 'Assembly Item' column.
      • From the list generated in the drop-down box, select the pack size of the finished products.
      • In the column 'Quantity to build', type in the number of bags/boxes packed.
      • A number is displayed on the 'Build Ref No' column, write this number in the 'Assembly Number' column on the Daily Production record.
      • Select the 'Build and New' column.
      • This updates the inventory and QuickBooks can be closed.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

To be taken if any of the above is not as described:

  • In the event that product (ingredients, packaging materials, finished products) safety is compromised – see Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product SOP.
  • Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
