Purpose

To outline the steps Fratello Coffee Roasters will take when a product recall or withdrawal is necessary due to a food safety concern, regulatory non-compliance, or consumer risk. This procedure aligns with CFIA recall expectations and internal traceability and communication protocols.

Scope

This procedure applies to all food products, packaging, and ingredients manufactured, packaged, or distributed by Fratello Coffee Roasters.

Classes of Recalls (as per CFIA)

  • Class I: High risk of serious or life-threatening health consequences (e.g., confirmed Listeria or undeclared allergens).
  • Class II: Temporary or non-life-threatening consequences (e.g., Norovirus risk).
  • Class III: No likely health risk (e.g., mislabelling of weight or non-organic as organic).

Recall Initiation & Notification

  • Open recall file and begin collecting all records related to the incident.
  • Notify CFIA immediately with:
    • Nature of the issue
    • Product name, size, lot code, label image
    • Distribution area and quantity
    • Dates of distribution
    • Complaints or illnesses reported
    • Contact information of recall coordinator and after-hours contact
  • Notify all customers (from ERP system) through the Director of Operations or QA.
  • Use prepared Recall Notice and Media Statement templates (see Appendix A & B).

Product Identification & Traceability

  • Identify affected products using:
    • Receiving Log
    • Invoices, POs, BOLs
    • Production Spreadsheet
    • Shipping Log
    • Packaging Receiving Logs
  • All lot-coded items must be traceable back to raw materials and packaging.
  • Use roast date or sequential numbering as lot codes.

Product Control & Disposition

  • Products within Fratello's control are placed on HOLD (see Hold Product SOP).
  • Returned or recalled product may either:
    • Be collected at customer site and sent to landfill/disposal facility
    • Be returned to Fratello's HOLD area for verification
  • Disposition actions are documented on the Recall Worksheet.

Communication Protocol

  • All recall communications are directed by the Recall Team Leader or designate.
  • Customers hold product until picked up or directed for destruction.
  • All communication is documented using the Customer Communication Log.
  • CFIA must be updated throughout the process.

Mock Recall and Traceability Testing

  • Conduct one mock recall annually. PLEASE NOTE – All Mock Recalls MUST include actual communication to be sent out to 3-5 clients who will be notified of our mock recall process. Each client will have 24 hours to respond. If Fratello Coffee Roasters does not hear back from them in 8 hours, we are to follow through with a direct phone call.
  • Includes forward and backward trace from raw material to finished product and finally to client/consumer.
  • Must achieve 99.5–105% mass balance within 4 hours.
  • If not effective:
    • Repeat within 60 days
    • Update procedures and conduct retraining

Recall Effectiveness & Closure

  • Recall is terminated only by the Recall Team and/or CFIA.
  • Effectiveness is measured by:
    • % product recovered
    • Time to complete recall
    • Confirmation that all customers were notified
  • Results and improvements documented in the Recall Record.
  • HACCP Plan and prerequisite programs reviewed post-recall.

Labeling & Product Coding

  • All products labelled with:
    • Common name
    • Net quantity
    • Fratello contact info (or "Prepared for")
    • Date marking (MMDDYYYY or YYMMDD)
    • Storage instructions (if needed)
    • Lot code (roast date, sequential number, or best before date)
  • Labels are only printed by trained staff and supervisors.
  • Label changes are restricted to supervisors.
