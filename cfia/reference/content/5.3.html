Purpose

To ensure that all materials entering the facility—including ingredients, packaging, and non-food items—are inspected for cleanliness, suitability, and integrity to prevent contamination. This supports compliance with SFCR sections 47, 72–74.

Scope

Applies to all incoming deliveries of raw materials, packaging, chemicals, and miscellaneous goods. Includes deliveries by suppliers, contract carriers, and walk-in deliveries.

Responsibility

  • Primary: Designated trained shipping/receiving personnel
  • Backup: Supervisor or designate

Frequency

  • Per Delivery: Every time goods are received.

Procedure

Step Key Actions Food Safety Considerations
1 Ensure truck driver stays in designated receiving area. Limits unauthorized access to food production zones.
2 Inspect trailer and pallets: look for dirt, debris, odours, signs of pests, water damage, or incompatible cargo (e.g., chemicals, animals, car parts). Prevents introduction of physical, biological, or chemical hazards per SFCR s.72.
3 If any issues are found, do not unload. Either reject the shipment or place on hold. Record decision and actions taken. Ensures compromised or potentially unsafe products do not enter the food stream.
4 Review all paperwork (PO, invoice, BOL). Verify supplier is approved and materials match documentation. Required to confirm supplier control under the Preventive Control Plan.
5 Record information on Transport Log: date, supplier, carrier, product, quantity, BOL, trailer condition, and lot numbers. Supports traceability and preventive control recordkeeping (SFCR Part 5).
6 Inspect product packaging for cleanliness, intact seals, labeling, and absence of damage or contaminants (e.g., staples, odours, water, pests). Prevents introduction of food hazards from poor packaging.
7 Tag incoming food products with pallet tags. Supports lot traceability and inventory rotation (FIFO).
8 Store items in designated, segregated areas per storage SOP. Segregation prevents cross-contamination and allergen risks.
9 Receive chemicals and non-foods at different times from ingredients when possible. Verify they are on the approved list. Reduces risk of cross-contact between food and hazardous substances.
10 Inspect and store miscellaneous goods appropriately (e.g., office supplies, hairnets, gloves). Maintains general sanitation and traceability control.

Returned Products

  • May be stored in the designated Hold area until evaluated.
  • Must be logged in the Hold Record and evaluated per Transportation and Storage SOP.

Deviations and Corrective Actions

Issue Corrective Action
Unsanitary or incompatible vehicle Do not receive shipment. Contact supplier. Record in Transport Log.
Damaged, infested, or wet goods Place materials on hold or reject. Escalate to management for disposition.
Unapproved supplier or undocumented goods Reject delivery or place on hold. Report deviation and review Approved Supplier List.
Chemical or ingredient delivery at same time Segregate immediately. Avoid cross-contamination.

All corrective actions must be recorded in the appropriate logs.

Verification Activities

  • Conducted as per the facility's Verification Procedure.
  • Includes periodic review of Transport Logs and inspection practices.

Records and Documentation

  • Transport Log
  • Hold Log
  • Corrective Action Record
  • Bill of Lading / Invoice
