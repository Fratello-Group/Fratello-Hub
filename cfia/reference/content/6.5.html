Purpose

To monitor and control pest activity within and around Fratello's facility, preventing potential contamination of food products, ingredients, and packaging materials. This procedure meets the CFIA's SFCR requirements under Part 4, s.81 (Biological Hazards & Pest Prevention).

Scope

Applies to:

  • All indoor and outdoor areas of the roasting facility, including the receiving/shipping dock, production, storage, and maintenance zones
  • Contracted pest control inspections and Fratello's internal follow-up measures

Responsibility

Role Responsibilities
Contracted Pest Provider: Peregrine General Pest Control Inc. Monthly inspections, bait rotation, service reports, recommendations
Warehouse Manager / Maintenance Supervisor Follow-up on technician findings, implement corrective actions, maintain records
All Employees Report pest sightings or trap disturbances immediately

Frequency

  • Monthly: Full facility inspection and trap monitoring by Peregrine General Pest Control Inc.
  • Ongoing: Staff visual monitoring and reporting
  • Spring/Summer: Seasonal reassessment of fly light use

Procedure

1. Pest Monitoring

Action Details
Inspections Conducted monthly by Peregrine General Pest Control Inc.; recorded in Service Reports stored in the Pest Control Binder
Trap Checks Check all interior rodent traps and exterior bait stations using the Pest Device Map
Trap Placement Devices must be: flush to the wall, entrance holes unobstructed, labeled, clean, functional
Bait Rotation Any bait added or changed is logged in the Pesticide Usage Log
Exterior Devices Stations are weighted, tamper-resistant, and placed around loading dock, front, and back of building
Seasonal Devices Fly lights may be added in warmer months if pest risk increases

2. Pest Device Map

The Pest Control Floor Map outlines locations of:

  • Tin Cat traps
  • Evo bait stations
  • RTU bait stations

A printed copy is included in the Pest Control Binder and reviewed during internal inspections.

3. Deviation & Corrective Actions

If pest activity or trap anomalies are found:

Trigger Corrective Action
Mouse caught indoors Conduct inspection for facility gaps, door seals, or sanitation issues
Multiple captures in same trap Investigate for patterns, increase trap frequency, check structural integrity
Droppings or damage to packaging Segregate and discard affected product, complete Corrective Action Record
Recommendations from Peregrine General Pest Control Inc. Actioned by Warehouse Manager with documentation of response
Trap damaged or blocked Replace trap, update records, retrain employees if needed
Evidence of pest contamination in product Dispose of product, record on Deviations & Corrective Action Log, and notify QA

4. Training

  • Staff are trained on pest awareness during orientation and refreshed annually
  • Training includes how to report pest sightings and trap disturbances
  • All pest control training records are maintained in the Training Record

Verification Activities

  • Monthly: Review of Peregrine General Pest Control Inc. reports and pest activity logs by Warehouse Manager
  • Quarterly: Internal audit of pest control practices and trap conditions
  • Annually: Full review of pest control effectiveness and seasonal strategy updates
