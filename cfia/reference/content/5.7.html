Purpose

To ensure that green beans are loaded correctly into the silos when needed and to maintain the traceability and inventory of products loaded into the Silos.

Responsibility

QA Roasters or designated alternate

Frequency

As needed

Important Information

  • The LARGE Silos is identified as A1 – 4 and B 1 – 4, has 4 separate compartments each and has a maximum capacity of 40 sacs of green beans in each compartment.
  • The SMALL Silos is identified as A 5 – 12 and B 5 – 12, has 8 separate compartments each and has a maximum capacity of 20 sacs of green beans in each compartment.
  • To ensure that the inventory is balanced, empty silos need to be communicated to the accounting department. Please speak with them to ensure our inventory records are balanced prior to loading activities.

Procedure

Prior to loading

  • Check that the chamber of the silos to be loaded is completely empty by opening and cleaning/dusting out the chamber of any left-over green beans.
  • Please note, an empty Rubbermaid container should be placed on the floor underneath the silo being cleaned BEFORE opening the compartment. This is done to catch all loose beans/dust that is trapped inside.
  • Once cleaned out, close the silos and put the zip tie on.
  • Please note, silos are mirrored in the A zones and B zones. Once a silo is empty, roasters will pull their green beans from the other side. At that time, the roasters will cut and remove the zip tie from the full silo.
  • Ensure that the tube coming from the blower is attached to the correct silos and that the silos tube is connected to the correct bin chamber.
    • Example: If Silos A, Chamber 4 is to be loaded with green beans, the tube is attached to the corresponding silos and bin chamber number.
  • Please note, before continuing on, have someone from the roasting team double check the set up of your tubbing and ensure the silo-handle is moved to the correct chamber.
  • Move the pallet of green beans to be loaded into position on the floor (not in loading position yet).
  • Write down the green beans name and LOT number from the green bean sacs onto the inventory board, also indicating the Silos and Chamber to be loaded.
  • The same LOT number, date and number of bags being loaded, must be written under the corresponding chamber magnet.

Loading

  • Put on protective ear equipment.
  • Visually inspect the magnets for any debris or dirt and remove any if seen before proceeding.
  • Please note, when removing metal grate to clean magnets, ensure safety gloves are worn to protect fingers from sharp edges of the grate.
  • Take the vacuum cleaner and put the hose behind the dust vacuum.
  • Using the forklift, move the pallet under the air pressure line.
  • Gently blow all the debris and dirt on the pallet with the air pressure line.
  • Using the forklift, carefully lift the pallet of green beans, lowering into position of the blue bucket elevator and lower onto the grate, while slowly tilting at a slight angle (of about 15 degrees).
  • Turn on the vacuum cleaner and the dust vacuum.
  • Adjust the red loading hopper and ensure the sliding door is fully closed and then opened a half turn.
  • Turn on the bucket elevator, and then turn on the auger and the blower in order.
  • Using a clean knife from the tool basket, carefully tear off the shrink wrap and safety straps from the sacs on the pallet.
  • On each pallet, there are usually 5 sacs of green beans per row, with two rows and a total of 10 sacs. Each sack weighs from (59kg) 130 to (70kg) 154.323 lbs, it is important to maneuver the sacs gently using the weight of the product as an advantage.
  • Work your way through the topmost bag to bottom and then front of pallet to back.
  • Please note, it is ok if a sack falls onto the metal grate. It will not harm the product nor the bucket elevator.
  • Starting from the topmost sacs and working your way down, make a clean cut on the bag of green beans using the knife (one bag at a time).
  • Once the cut is made, allow the green beans pour into the hopper, which is then automatically sucked up into the Silos.
  • Work on the first 2 sacs in front (starting at the top, moving down), then move to the back row, moving the top sac(s) from the back row, to the front row, repeating the above steps after each sac is emptied.
  • While the green beans are loading into the hopper, visually inspect the beans for any debris (e.g., burlap, pieces of plastic liner) and remove if any is seen.
  • Once all the sacs on the pallets have been loaded, fully open the sliding door on the red loading hopper to ensure that all green beans are blown into the Silos. This will eliminate any green bean mixtures the next time, and also ensure that product is fully loaded for inventory purposes.
  • Depending on the quantity of sacs required to fill the Silos, another pallet of green beans might be required (e.g., in cases of 40 sacs). If this is the case, then repeat Steps 10 to 20 until the silos is fully loaded.
  • Ensure that the Inventory board is updated with the total number of sacs loaded and the date of loading. This information is then transferred into Cropster for traceability and inventory purposes.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

To be taken if any of the above is not as described:

Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
