Purpose

To ensure that roasted beans are ground correctly according to customer specifications.

Responsibility

QA Roasters or trained alternate (packaging and maintenance personnel)

Frequency

Daily (when required)

Important Information

  • During grinding operations, carbon monoxide present in the beans is released into the air. It is important that the operator stands behind the yellow caution lines to avoid inhalation.

Procedure: BIG Grinder (700 FX Coffee Granulizer)

Product Information and Grind Settings

  • Discuss with Head/Coffee Roaster or Quality Assurance to ascertain coffee specifications for grinding of a dark roast or light roast AND the grind settings required on the grinding machine.
  • Enter the information on the COFFEE GRINDING LOG.
  • Using the 'GRIND TYPE' settings, set the top and bottom grinding rollers to the desired settings.
  • To adjust the top and bottom grinding rollers, pull the lever and then use the adjusting knob to sit on the graduated number line required (as per the grind settings).
  • Once the grinding rollers have been set, inform the Head Roaster or QA to verify the settings are correct before starting grinding operations.

Machine Set Up and Transfer of Beans

  • After grind settings have been verified on the machine, visually inspect the filling hose for any obstructions in the filler tube.
  • Ensure the vacuum hose is unplugged. This is located by the wall in the roasting area.
  • Turn on the MAIN VACCU FLO switch located on the support of the Grinder.
  • Turn on the HOUSE VACUUM switch located on the wall in the packaging area.
  • Turn on the HOOD VENTILATION switch located on the support of the Grinder.
  • Move the cart of roasted beans into position and connect the suction hose ensuring that all beans get sucked through the hose.
  • Once all the beans have been sucked into the storage hopper, turn off the MAIN VACCU FLO switch.
  • Turn off the HOUSE VACUUM switch.
  • Put the filling hose away.
  • Select a clean white tote bag and:
    • Tie the two ropes at the bottom of the tote (first layer). Secure by using the clip string.
    • Tuck the outer first layer which has now been tied into the second layer, secure by using the clip string.
  • Place the white tote bag on a tote cart and secure by tying the 4 ends on the tote cart handles.
  • Move the secured white tote bag and place underneath the auger of the grinding machine.
  • Remove the COFFEE TAG from the cart and put it into the transparent sleeve of the white tote bag.
  • Once this is in place, grinding operations are ready to begin.

Grinding Operations

  • From the control panel, put the MASTER SWITCH to the 'ON' position by turning clockwise.
  • Press down on the 'GRINDER START' button to put the grinder on.
  • From the control panel, press down on the 'FEEDER START' button to turn the feeder on.
  • Walk round to the other side of the machine, pull the cable wire and ensure it is latched onto the hook. This opens up the gate on the grinder.
  • Visually check the 'FEEDER SPEED CONTROL' knob is placed on the '30' line. This is marked with an asterisk (*) on the panel.
    • Note: The knob should never be turned beyond this '30' mark as that is the maximum setting allowed.
  • Turn the 'FEEDER GATE' knob to the 'OPEN' position by turning clockwise.
  • Grinding of the beans begins at this point.
  • Hold up the auger gate lever to let out the first set of ground coffee into the tote bag. Once there is an even flow in the outlet, release the auger lever into downward position as this will enable continuous flow of the ground beans into the tote bag.

Caution: Operator to step behind the yellow caution lines to avoid inhalation of carbon monoxide released into the air.

  • Wait for grinding to be complete. This could typically take 10 – 20 minutes depending on grind quantity.
  • Using a hammer, gently tap the sides of the machine and the sides of the auger. This ensures that any left over residue of ground coffee falls out into the tote bags.
  • Also shake the auger gate lever to let out any ground coffee residue.
  • On the control panel, turn off the feeder by putting the 'FEEDER GATE' knob to the 'CLOSE' position turning anti clockwise.
  • On the side of the machine, unhook the cable wire by removing the latch from the hook pin.
  • On the control panel, press down on the 'FEEDER STOP' button to turn the feeder off.
  • On the control panel, press down on the 'GRINDER STOP' button to turn the grinder off.
  • Then put the 'MASTER SWITCH' knob in the 'OFF' position by turning anti clockwise.
  • Turn off the HOOD VENTILATION switch located on the support of the Grinder.
  • Cover the tote bag carefully by using the flap on the tote bag.
  • Move the tote bag and place in the Ground coffee holding area for at least 24 hours before packaging.

Post Grinding Activities

  • After completion of grinding operations, brush off the machine using a hand-held brush to get rid of all chaff and debris.
  • Sweep off all dirt off the floor and empty dirt into the dedicated garbage bins.
  • Leave grinding area clean and tidy.

Procedure: SMALL Grinder

Important Information

  • Prior to grinding operations on the small grinder, ensure that the machine is properly dusted.
  • Open the manual gate and clean internal chute to get rid of any ground coffee residue in the grinder. Transfer any residue into the garbage bin.

Procedure

  • Collect product information required for grinding from the Warehouse Manager.
  • Select the appropriate roasted coffee beans required for grinding.
  • The Warehouse Manager, QA or Head Roaster will give the operator the type of grind required.
  • Based on the type of grind, select the corresponding grind settings.
  • Using the adjusting dial on the machine, select the number required for the grind by turning either clockwise or anti clockwise.
  • Once settings have been selected, put the manual gate of the machine in the close position.
  • Turn on the machine by pressing the 'START' button.
  • Carefully dump the beans into the hopper.
    • Note: If using roasted beans from already packaged bags, ensure that the bags are opened carefully by gently tearing open the bag.
  • Place a bag or blue bin at the outlet of the grinder.
    • Note: New bags are required for torn bags.
  • Slide the manual gate open to collect the ground coffee.
  • Once grinding is completed, gently tap the sides of the hopper and machine using hands and visually inspect inside the hopper to ensure that all beans are ground.
  • Once grinding is completed, close the manual gate.
  • Turn off the machine by pressing the 'STOP' button.
  • If ground coffee is in bags, transfer to packaging department for sealing.
  • For post grinding activities, follow the Post Grinding Activities steps above.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

Deviation and corrective actions to be taken if any of the above is not as described: Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
