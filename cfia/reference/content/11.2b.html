Purpose

To outline the procedures and responsibilities for the implementation, monitoring, and verification of the Production Hazard Identification & Risk Assessment (HIRA) as detailed in form 11.2 "Fratello Hazard Identification & Risk Assessment Production (HIRA) Form." This SOP ensures the identification, documentation, control, and verification of biological, chemical, and physical hazards associated with the coffee and tea production processes.

Scope

This SOP applies to all production activities at Fratello Coffee Roasters that involve green bean loading, roasting, cooling, destoning, bin storage, and packaging of roasted coffee and loose-leaf tea.

Roles & Responsibilities

Production Supervisor

  • Ensure all staff involved in production activities are trained in hazard awareness and control.
  • Conduct regular reviews of HIRA form 11.2 and update as necessary.
  • Monitor for deviations and initiate corrective actions where required.

Quality Assurance (QA) Coordinator

  • Verify implementation of HIRA procedures during routine internal audits.
  • Retain records of all completed HIRA forms and associated corrective actions.
  • Facilitate root cause analysis for hazard-related incidents.

Production Team Members

  • Follow established procedures for monitoring, inspection, and documentation.
  • Immediately report any observed or suspected hazard to a supervisor.

Procedure

1. Initiation of HIRA Documentation

  • The QA Coordinator and Production Supervisor complete HIRA form 11.2 prior to any process changes or at least annually.
  • Each production area is assessed step-by-step to determine potential biological, chemical, and physical hazards.
  • Control measures, monitoring, and verification activities are assigned for each identified hazard.

2. Daily Implementation Procedures

a. Green Bean Loading:

  • Inspect silos, magnet screens, and dust collectors prior to use.
  • Record condition and cleanliness on daily pre-operational checklist.

b. Roasting:

  • Monitor temperature parameters and air flow.
  • Check for fire safety equipment readiness as per SOP 6.6.

c. Cooling and Destoning:

  • Inspect magnets and destoner functionality.
  • Look for foreign material.

d. Packaging:

  • Inspect hopper, conveyors, and magnet strips.
  • Ensure proper allergen and sanitation controls are in place.
  • Validate scale calibration and bag weight.

3. Recordkeeping and Verification

  • Use daily HIRA log sheets to record observations and verification activities.
  • Store completed documents in QA documentation folder (digital and/or physical).
  • Supervisors review logs weekly; QA reviews monthly and during audits.

4. Corrective Actions

When a hazard is identified without a proper control measure:

  • Stop the process immediately.
  • Notify QA and supervisor.
  • Record deviation and corrective action taken.

Root cause analysis and preventive measures are implemented within 72 hours.

5. Review and Updates

  • HIRA implementation SOP and form 11.2 are reviewed annually or whenever a process modification occurs.
  • Updates are tracked in document control records.
