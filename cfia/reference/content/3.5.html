Purpose

To ensure that raw green beans are loaded into the roaster and roasted correctly according to customer and QA specifications while also maintaining traceability and inventory of roasted products.

Responsibility

Head Roaster, Quality Assurance and Coffee Roasters

Frequency

Daily (when in operation)

Important Notes

  • The G90 is used for roasting 100lbs or 50lbs batch size of coffee beans
  • Empty the chaff from the chaff collector after every 6 roast cycles

Preliminary Roasting Procedure

  • The first employee to arrive at the facility will:
    • Turn on switches which control the lights in the roasting area.
    • Turn on dust vacuum which is located on the wall behind the Silos.
    • Turn on the compressor located in the compressor room located at the rear of the facility beside the service room. The green button turns the machine on.
  • The order for roasting coffee beans is as follows:
    • Decaffeinated coffee
    • Dark roast coffee
    • Medium roast coffee
    • Light roast coffee

Note: If there is any order for portion pack ground coffee, this will be expedited after the decaffeinated roast has been completed.

Starting and Preheating the Roaster

  • Check the chaff collector and ensure it is emptied out.
  • Turn on the main power. Note: the oven door will be closed automatically in a minute.
  • Power on the laptop attached to the G-90 and launch the Cropster Roasting Intelligence Programme.
  • Check the temperature readings displayed on the screen (bean temp and env. temp).
    • Note: If the temperature readings are displayed, it means it is properly connected. If however it does not show up it means the thermometer is disconnected; then simply adjust the USB connection cable attached to the laptop.
  • From the Control Screen, select the 'MANUAL SCREEN' button.
  • Press the 'OPEN OVEN' button. There should be a gap between the drum and the body of the roaster.
  • Press the 'RUN PROCESS MOTORS' button and visually check the drum is rotating.
  • Press the 'CLOSE OVEN' button to close the oven.
  • Press the 'BLOWER START' button.
  • Press the 'MAIN BURNER START' button.
  • Press the 'AFTER BURN START' button.

Note: If while the burner is turning it has failed to turn the flame on and the alarm light is on, press the 'FLAME SAFEGUARD RESET' to restart the burner.

Note: The 'Emergency Stop' buttons located on the main control panel and the afterburner control panel should only be used in a scenario where all systems need to be immediately shut down for the sake of safety and must both be pressed simultaneously.

  • Make sure the 'COOLING TABLE' button is highlighted green and does not say 'STONER'.
  • With clean hands, check for air flow on the cooling tray.
  • From the Control Screen, visually check the % values displayed for 'COOL TABLE', 'OVEN ROTATE' and 'OVEN AIRFLOW'. These buttons should have 100% displayed on the screen.
  • The 'VALVE MAX. POSITION' and the 'DAMPER ADJUSTED' should both read 25%. If required, adjust to 25% using the 'VALVE MAXIMUM POSITION' button and the 'ADJUST DAMPER POSITION' button.

Important Note: Depending on the roast profile, there are different damping percentages required which include 25% (as described above), 50% damper and 75% damper. The 'ADJUST DAMPER POSITION' will be used to adjust the settings as required.

  • Once the temperature attains the preheat set point, it automatically maintains the temperature between 210 - 220°C. When the temperature range is stable, it is ready to roast at this point. It typically takes an hour.

Important Note: Must periodically check the temperature during pre-heating.

Loading Green Beans (from the Silos)

  • Based on the roasting schedule, identify the associated silos from which the green beans will be taken.
  • On Cropster, select the Roast Profile required.
  • Visually check the roaster hopper to make sure it is emptied.
  • Using the green bean cart, open the sac and inspect for any left-over green beans. If any is observed, ensure this is emptied out prior to weighing activities.
  • Turn on the scale of the green bean cart and 'TARE' to ensure it reads 0.
    • Note: If the scale is not turned on, check the battery underneath the cart. Make sure the battery switch is positioned on. Then check the 'low battery' light. If the light is on it means the battery needs to be charged.
  • Carefully move and place the cart into position under the Silos from where the green beans will be collected.
  • Once in position, slide back the latch of the silo compartment; this dispenses the green beans into the weighing cart.
    • Note: When weighing, it is important that the precise weight needed for the roast is weighed. If more is dispensed, then the green beans should be manually scooped out into the dedicated blue bins and labeled.
  • Once the green beans needed have been weighed, transfer the cart to the vacuum conveyor making sure the cart chute is properly aligned.
  • Check that the suction switch is turned to the G-90 mark indicated on the suction pipes.
    • Note: If the switch is turned to the G-120 mark, go to the G-90 control screen, and press the '2' button to set the flow to the G-90.
  • Put on protective earmuffs. This is important prior to turning on the vacuum conveyor.
  • Turn on the black button which activates the air flow of the vacuum.
  • To load the green beans into the roaster, use the lever on the weighing cart and pull up to open the gate. This allows the green beans to flow into the vacuum conveyor. Take care to ensure that this is released slowly to avoid the beans overflowing on the surface area. If this happens, close the cart gate to allow the green beans in the vacuum conveyor to flow through the chute.
  • Ensure the green bean cart is emptied and return cart to charging station located at the midpoint of all silos. Visually check the inside of the vacuum that the beans are not left over.
  • Turn off the vacuum using the red button on the main switch.

Loading Green Beans (Manual weighing from green beans sacs)

  • Based on the roasting schedule, identify the sacs or silos from which the green beans will be taken.
  • On Cropster, select the Roast Profile required.
  • Visually check the roaster hopper to make sure it is emptied.
  • For loading green beans from sacs, use designated blue bins, turn on the floor scale at the Green Coffee Area and 'TARE' the blue bin to 0.
  • Find the required coffee sac on the rack.
  • Scoop out and weigh the required quantity into the blue bin.
  • Manually move the bin to the vacuum conveyor.
  • Check that the suction switch is turned to the G-90 mark indicated on the suction pipes.
    • Note: If the switch is turned to the G-120 mark, go to the G-90 control screen, and press the '2' button to set the flow to the G-90.
  • Put on protective earmuffs. This is important prior to turning on the vacuum conveyor.
  • Turn on the black button which activates the air flow of the vacuum.
  • To load the green beans into the roaster, carefully tilt the bin to allow the green beans to flow into the vacuum conveyor. Take care to ensure that this is released slowly to avoid the beans overflowing on the surface area.
  • Visually check the inside of the vacuum that the beans are not left over.
  • Turn off the vacuum using the red button on the main switch.
  • Ensure the bin is emptied and return the bin to green bean area.

Roasting Activities

  • Once the green beans have been loaded and pre-heat time is completed, roasting is ready to begin.
  • Double check that the correct roast profile has been selected.
  • Carefully follow the roast profile noting to drop the beans slightly earlier:
    • Roast profile with 35% dumper (drop beans at 0.5 – 1 °C before actual roast temperature).
    • Roast profile with 50% dumper (drop beans at 1 – 2 °C before actual roast temperature).
    • Roast profile with 75% dumper (drop beans at 2 – 2.5 °C before actual roast temperature).
    This ensures that the roasting process starts at the actual temperature.
  • The 'START BATCH' button then changes to 'BATCH ENABLED'.
  • The roasting cycle begins at this point.
  • Follow the reference graph and try to ensure that the procedures follow and match the reference graph as closely as possible.
  • Periodically, using the tryer, take a small portion from the roast beans and visually inspect the roast beans for color and roasting reaction, also ensuring to smell the product until roasting is completed.
  • Before launching stir arms, check that the cooling tray is clear and free of any debris.
  • One minute from roast completion, from the Stand screen, press the 'STIR ON' button. This activates the stir arm on the cooling tray in a continuous clockwise motion.
  • When the roast is determined as finished by the coffee roaster, press the 'OPEN OVEN' button to offload the roasted beans into the cooling tray.

Important Note: After offloading the roasted beans, check for the gap in the drum and ensure it is not too wide.

  • Once the roasted beans are offloaded into the cooling tray, press the 'CLOSE OVEN' button to close the oven door, and ensure the machine settings are tuned back to default of 35%.
  • When the roasted beans are offloaded into the cooling tray, it takes at least 5-10 minutes for them to cool down. Once the roasted beans are cool, the QA inspection is ready to be performed.

Caution: DO NOT TOUCH the roasted beans until 5 minutes after offloading as it will still be very hot.

QA Inspection Activities

  • Power on the laptop attached to the QA and launch the "Colortrack – Laser Color Analyzer" Programme.
  • From the cooling tray, use a dedicated plate to scoop out the roasted beans.
  • Transfer beans into the grinder which is located on the QA inspection rack.
  • Grind a little of the roasted beans to purge left over grinds from the grinder. Discard this first grind and then proceed to grind the remaining roasted beans for inspection.
  • Grind the remaining roasted beans into the plate and level using the dedicated ruler.
  • Gently swipe the bottom of the plate and place it into the Colortrack to read the colour value.
    • Caution: Do not touch the laser equipment by hands or the plate.
  • Set the timer knob by turning clockwise to '45 seconds' and then press to start analyzing.
  • Once analyzed, enter the 'Average' information into 'Roast Value' on Cropster.

Transfer and Weighing the Roasted Beans

  • From the Stand screen, turn on the 'COOLING TABLE' button which turns on the destoner.
  • Turn the black lever from the upward position to the downward. This opens the cooling tray and roasted beans are sucked up through the destoner.
  • Ensure the cooling tray is emptied, press the 'STIR OFF' button to stop the stir arm on the cooling tray.
  • After this, pull up the lever on the cooling tray.
  • Carefully place a clean coffee cart on the scale. Tare out the scale to 0.
  • Press the 'STONER' button to switch the airflow on the cooling tray.
  • Pull open the bean hopper pully. This offloads the beans from the hopper into the coffee cart.
  • Use the rubber mallet to gently hit the sides to knock out stuck beans.
  • Use the air pressure line to blow inside the hose.
  • Weigh the product. Once products have been weighed, write down the weight and then tare the scale.
  • Pull out the cart from the scale carefully; using the cart weight on the side, double check the accurate weight.
  • For smaller loads of roasted beans or to manually transfer from the cooling tray, tare a designated blue bin on the scale and then place it under the cooling tray outlet.
  • Turn the lever down from the upward position to the downward position to let out the beans from the cooling tray.
  • Weigh the product.
  • Manually transfer beans into coffee cart.
  • Once products have been weighed, enter the weight on Cropster.
  • Enter product details on the coffee tag label.
  • Put the coffee tag on the cart.
    • Note: The roasted coffee's lot number is Cropster's ID-tag number.
  • Place bins in the Work In Progress (WIP) Storage area.

Record Completion and Build Assembly Activities

On the laptop:

  • Open Odoo.
  • Insert username and password provided.
  • Go to the Manufacturing page.
  • Click 'NEW' at the top right to create the new sheet.
  • Type in the Build Code or Product Name in the 'Product' column.
  • Type in the quantity in the 'Quantity' column; this auto-populates the information. Please ensure that you record the actual quantity in the 'To Consume' column.
  • Click the 'Save Manually' button and confirm the page.
  • Type in the quantity in the 'Quantity' column again and type in the 'Cropster ID-tag (PR-code)' in the 'Lot/Serial Number' column. This 'Lot Number' is the Roasted Bean's lot number.
  • Click the icon after the 'Lot/Serial Numbers' on the sheet. Ensure the lot number is below the 'Pick From' column, as well as the quantity. If there is an additional line, please delete it and save.
  • Repeat the previous step for each line all the way down.
  • Ensure that the check mark is in the 'Consumed' column.
  • Click the 'Save Manually' button and click 'Produce All' to build.
  • Confirm the page to finish.
  • Complete the 'Daily Roast Tracking Sheet' with the required information i.e., Item Code, roasted weight, PR. Number and Assembly number.
  • Record the 'Build Ref No' from the Build Assembly page into the 'Assembly' column on the Daily Roast Tracking Sheet.
  • Sign the 'Completed' column on the record.

Important Note: Do not sign under the 'Completed' column until all other information has been written into their respective column.

Turning off the Roaster

  • When the Daily Roasting Schedule has been completed, turn off the MAIN BURNER and AFTER BURNER on the control screen.
  • Press the 'OVEN OPEN' to open the oven.
  • Leave to cool down for 30 minutes.
  • Press the 'ENABLE COOL DOWN' button and the 'BLOWER STOP' button.
  • After this, the drum automatically turns off.
  • Visually inspect the drum is fully stopped, turn off the main power.
  • Close the Cropster programme and shut down the computer.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

Deviation and corrective actions to be taken if any of the above is not as described: Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
