Document Title: Importing and Interprovincial Trade PCP — Document Type: Preventive Control Program SOP

1. Purpose

To define the preventive control activities and verification procedures related to the importation and interprovincial trade of green and roasted coffee, packaging materials, and associated food contact materials, in compliance with the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR), Parts 2, 4 and 5.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to all incoming and outgoing products that are imported into Canada or traded between provinces, including green coffee beans, roasted coffee, and related food packaging materials handled by Fratello Coffee Roasters.

3. Responsibility

  • Director of Operations – Ensures compliance with licensing and regulatory requirements.
  • QA Roaster/Designate – Oversees incoming inspections, traceability, and deviations.
  • Receiver/Shipping Personnel – Executes inspections and recordkeeping during receiving/shipping.
  • Compliance Team – Maintains documentation and performs verification audits.

4. Regulatory References

  • SFCR Part 2 (Trade): Sections 6–13
  • SFCR Part 4 (Preventive Controls): Sections 47–89
  • SFCR Part 5 (Traceability): Sections 90–92
  • CFIA Guidance: Preventive Control Plans

6. Verification Procedures

  • Monthly internal audit of import and shipping records for compliance
  • Annual review of supplier documentation and Letters of Guarantee
  • Verification of traceability mock recall every 6 months (1 forward / 1 back)

7. Corrective Actions

All non-compliant shipments or documentation discrepancies must be:

  • Logged in the Deviation and Corrective Action Log
  • Assessed for food safety risk
  • Reported to CFIA if imported food may be unsafe or misrepresented
  • Subject to retraining of receiving personnel where required
