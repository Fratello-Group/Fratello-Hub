Purpose

To ensure that all product formulations, raw materials, packaging, and processing steps are properly defined, verified, and managed in compliance with CFIA Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR) and internal quality and safety standards.

Scope

This procedure applies to:

  • All green and roasted coffee products
  • All ingredients and packaging materials used in production
  • All processing steps including green bean receiving, loading, roasting, and packaging

Procedure

4.1 Product Formulation & Specifications (1.1b)

  • Specifications are maintained for all finished coffee products, including:
    • Bean origin
    • Roast profile
    • Moisture loss and target roast color (as per Cropster)
    • Packaging type and shelf life
  • Ingredient and packaging approval:
    • Must be from Approved Supplier List
    • Supplier must provide:
      • Technical Data Sheet (TDS)
      • Allergen Statement
      • Letter of Guarantee or Compliance to Division 23 (Food and Drug Regulations)
  • QA verifies that all raw materials and packaging:
    • Are permitted for use
    • Pose no risk of contamination
    • Are correctly labeled and traceable

4.2 Product Processing (1.1c)

  • Green Bean Handling:
    • Green beans are logged and tagged at receiving.
    • Traceability is maintained using Cropster and silo chamber logs.
    • Visual inspections for foreign materials (e.g., burlap, plastic) are performed during loading.
  • Roasting Process:
    • Follow established roasting profiles in Cropster.
    • Visually monitor for bean consistency and defects.
    • Follow SOPs for loading and cleaning roasting equipment (refer to "Green Bean Loading Procedure").
  • Processing Controls:
    • Equipment must pass daily Pre-Operational Inspections before use.
    • Equipment is cleaned per Sanitation SOP using dry or manual methods; sink washing is used for tools.
    • Chemicals used are on the Master List and stored securely.
  • Label Verification:
    • QA verifies printed labels match approved specs (origin, blend name, net weight, roast date, etc.).
    • Labels and packaging are verified against customer or regulatory requirements before release.
