Document Title: Export Control PCP — Sub-element: 1.4 – Export Control Documentation

1. Purpose

To ensure that only eligible, compliant products are exported from Fratello Coffee Roasters, and that all required documentation is maintained to meet CFIA export certification and international market access requirements.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all finished products designated for export, including roasted coffee, green beans, or contract/white label items, and any certified shipments that require CFIA export certificates or letters of guarantee.

3. Responsibilities

  • Director of Operations: Ensures Fratello is approved for export to relevant countries.
  • Export Coordinator: Manages documentation, certificate requests, eligibility review, and CFIA communication.
  • QA Roaster/Designate: Ensures traceability, lot control, and label compliance.
  • Shipping Department: Prepares shipment, ensures product segregation and seals.

5. Verification Procedures

  • Monthly verification of:
    • Export Certificate Control Log
    • Label reviews
    • Export batch production and traceability records
  • Annual internal audit of:
    • Export shipments
    • Supporting documentation
    • Market eligibility controls

6. Export Documentation Package Requirements

Each export shipment file should contain:

  • CFIA Export Certificate (copy)
  • Certificate request form
  • Production records (date, lot code, equipment cleaning log if applicable)
  • Traceability records (Cropster output or manual log)
  • Shipping records (Bill of Lading, Freight Invoice)
  • Label copy (in-market language, bilingual if required)
  • CFIA approved supplier documentation (if exporting imported goods)

7. Supporting Controls

  • Segregation Procedures: Export-designated products are clearly marked and stored in a separate pallet zone with "EXPORT ONLY" signage. Non-compliant or suspect product is physically separated and recorded in the Deviation Log or Hold Product Log.
  • Label Controls: QA must approve all labels used for exports to ensure compliance with destination country regulations. Any changes to label design, claims, or layout must be reviewed prior to production.
  • Certificate Issuance Procedure: All CFIA-issued certificates are tracked with a serial number, date, and recipient country. Copies are kept on file. Any voided, rejected, or corrected certificates are clearly marked and retained.
