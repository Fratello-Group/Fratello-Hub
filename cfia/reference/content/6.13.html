1.0 Purpose

To ensure that all chemicals used at Fratello Coffee Roasters—including cleaning agents, sanitizers, maintenance lubricants, pest control agents, and facility chemicals—are reviewed, approved, stored, and used in a manner that prevents contamination and supports CFIA compliance.

2.0 Scope

This procedure applies to:

  • All food production and handling areas
  • All employees, sanitation contractors, and maintenance staff
  • Any chemical product used on-site, whether for direct food contact surfaces or non-contact environmental applications

3.0 Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
QA Coordinator Maintains and reviews the Chemical & Sanitation List annually; ensures SDS are on file
Sanitation Lead Ensures proper chemical use, PPE, and dilution standards
Maintenance Technician Ensures only approved lubricants, sealants, or cleaning products are used on equipment
Warehouse Manager Ensures safe and segregated chemical storage

4.0 Definitions

  • SDS (Safety Data Sheet) – Document detailing chemical hazards, safe use, PPE, and first aid
  • Food-Grade Lubricant – NSF H1-certified for incidental food contact
  • D2 Cleaner – No-rinse surface sanitizer approved for food contact

5.0 Procedure

5.1 Chemical Approval Process

  • All new chemical products must be submitted to QA for review before purchase or use
  • QA must verify:
    • Product purpose and intended use
    • CFIA/NSF approval rating if applicable
    • SDS availability
    • Compatibility with existing cleaning or production SOPs

5.2 Maintaining the Chemical List

  • The master list is titled: Fratello Approved Chemicals and Sanitation List (Doc #6.12)
  • Reviewed annually by QA or when new chemicals are added
  • Must include:
    • Product Name
    • Use Category (Cleaner, Sanitizer, etc.)
    • CFIA/NSF Approval No.
    • SDS on File? (Y/N)
    • Storage Location
    • Supplier

5.3 Storage and Labelling

  • Chemicals must be:
    • Stored in original containers with intact labels
    • Kept in designated chemical storage areas (away from food and packaging)
    • Segregated by type (sanitizers vs. maintenance lubricants)
  • Spray bottles or secondary containers must be clearly labelled with product name and dilution ratio

5.4 Usage and Verification

  • Only chemicals from the approved list may be used
  • Sanitation staff must verify:
    • Correct dilution per SDS
    • PPE in use per chemical requirements
    • Application follows SOP 6.1 (Sanitation)
  • QA will periodically audit sanitation logs and observe chemical use

5.5 Discontinued or Recalled Products

  • Any discontinued, expired, or recalled chemical must:
    • Be tagged and removed from active storage
    • Logged in the Deviation Report and replaced with approved alternative
