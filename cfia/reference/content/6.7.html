Purpose

To prevent intentional contamination, tampering, theft, or sabotage of food, ingredients, packaging, or equipment at Fratello facilities by ensuring all physical and information security measures are actively managed and verified.

Scope

Applies to all personnel, contractors, visitors, drivers, and service providers accessing Fratello's production, storage, and administrative areas.

Responsibility

  • Director of Operations or trained designate: Overall program oversight, reviews, and training
  • All Employees: Daily adherence to facility access and reporting policies
  • Warehouse Manager: Oversees inbound/outbound shipment integrity and product security
  • IT Lead: Oversees digital access security and backups

Frequency

  • Security monitoring: Ongoing during operating hours
  • Facility security inspection: Monthly
  • Security self-assessment: Annually
  • Training: Orientation + Annual refresher

Procedure

Step Key Actions Food Safety/Defense Considerations
1 All employees must enter via the main entrance, which is signed and monitored. Controlled access to prevent unauthorized entry.
2 All doors (except main entrance) must be kept closed and locked when not in active use. Physical barriers to prevent tampering or theft.
3 Visitors and contractors must sign the Visitor Log and read the Visitor Policy. Meets traceability and accountability expectations.
4 Only Approved Contractors may be unaccompanied in production areas (see Approved List). Reduces supervision burden without compromising risk.
5 Drivers remain within the dock zone, which is secured by a gate. No movement into production unless escorted. Secures loading/unloading zone integrity.
6 All trucks are inspected before loading/unloading as per Shipping & Receiving SOPs. Suspect product is isolated and investigated. Required under SFCR s.53 (intentional contamination).
7 Facility is alarmed overnight and on weekends. Cameras monitor critical zones. Deters unauthorized access outside operating hours.
8 Chemical storage is locked and access-controlled. Reduces the risk of malicious or accidental misuse.
9 Mail is received outside of production zones. Suspicious packages are not opened inside food handling areas. Mitigates external food fraud or tampering risks.
10 All company computers are password protected and restricted to authorized users. Data is backed up via [insert service]. Protects food safety records and traceability logs.
11 Staff are trained to report suspicious behavior immediately and encouraged to be proactive in maintaining site vigilance. Builds a culture of awareness and food defense.

Corrective Actions

Trigger Event Corrective Action
Unauthorized person in production/storage Notify management immediately. Escort from facility. Log incident.
Suspect shipment or truck tampering Stop unloading. Isolate materials. Refer to Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product SOP.
Suspicious package/mail Do not open in production area. Report to management. Handle externally.
Facility breach (door left open, alarm failure) Investigate root cause. Retrain staff. Log corrective action.
