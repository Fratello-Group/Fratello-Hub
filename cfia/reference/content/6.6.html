1.0 Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to establish the corporate standard for the prevention of uncontrolled fires and the protection of personnel, equipment and facilities from fire damage. Prevention measures must include policies and procedures for housekeeping, storage and handling of flammables and combustibles.

2.0 Definitions

None used in this document

3.0 Scope

The requirements described in this document are applicable to all divisions of the Fratello Group.

4.0 Responsibilities

Senior Management

  • Ensure these procedures are kept up to date and conveyed to all employees.
  • Provide the necessary funding to ensure that appropriate training takes place to minimize the exposure to fires within the Fratello operations.
  • Update these procedures as required based on changes to the facility or equipment.

Operations Manager / Supervisors

  • Are responsible to communicate this procedure.
  • Conduct regularly scheduled procedure drills to verify effectiveness of this plan. Regularly scheduled drills should be performed at least on an annual basis.

Employees

  • Learn where their nearest and safest evacuation route is located where they work and meet at the designated mustering area during an evacuation.
  • Report any fire safety concerns to their direct line supervisor or Operations Manager.
  • Follow the response procedures related to the specific roaster or equipment where a fire concern may be present.

5.0 Procedure

5.1 Fire Plans

A risk assessment must be completed to determine that the work area is not a hazardous location. The Operations Manager and/or Supervisor will ensure that flammable substances stored or used at the work area:

  • Will not be in enough quantity to produce an explosive atmosphere if inadvertently released,
  • Are not stored in the immediate vicinity of the air intake of which may lead to one of the following:
    • a ventilation supply system,
    • internal combustion engine,
    • a fired heater, or
    • furnace, and/or
  • Stored in containers that are approved through the required local legislation.

Fire plans have been developed for the Fratello buildings. Where and when required, the Fratello Group will appoint a Fire Marshall for each building or store location. An alarm system must be established to warn and evacuate personnel in the event of a fire. This can include a built-in alarm system already in place in the mall or store location and/or a portable horn. Free access to escape routes and firefighting equipment must always be maintained in accordance with local, provincial, and/or federal legislation.

Within the Fratello Main Building and in the various Analog store locations, evacuation routes have been established and predetermined. For the Main Fratello location found in Southeast Calgary, a map has been created. This map has been posted and is discussed during New Hire Orientation with all new employees.

5.1.1 On Hearing the Fire Alarm

Upon hearing an alarm to evacuate, employees of Fratello Group companies and / or Analog patrons must:

  • Stop all work; ensure that nothing is left in an unsafe manner
  • Shut down all sources of ignition; smoking, welding, vehicles etc.
  • Keep phone and radio lines clean for emergency communications
  • Proceed along escape routes, closing doors behind you
  • Move directly to the designated meeting spot
  • Take attendance to ensure everyone has left the building safely.
  • Do-not re-enter the building till you are instructed that it is safe to do so
  • Do not use the elevator while exiting the building
  • Leave all personal items behind
  • Do not move vehicles
  • Do not block access roads at any time

5.1.2 On – Site

Where and when required, Fratello will provide training associated with the onsite fire equipment. Access to firefighting equipment such as fire extinguishers must always be maintained:

  • All fire extinguishers will be periodically inspected and maintained in operating condition. Defective fire extinguishers must be immediately replaced with properly functioning extinguisher of equal size.
  • All materials will be stored, handled, and piled with due regard to their fire characteristics.

5.2 Fire Extinguisher

5.2.1 Types of Fires

Fires are classified as Class A, B, C, D or Special, depending upon the types of materials involved. These classes are defined as the follows:

  • Class A: Fires in ordinary combustible materials such as wood, cloth, paper, trash, rubber and plastic
  • Class B: Fires in flammable liquid, oil, grease, tar, oil-based paint, lacquer and flammable gas
  • Class C: Fires involving energized electrical equipment or systems, resulting in the extinguishing media conducting electricity (when electrical equipment or systems are de-energized, extinguishers for Class A or B fires can be used safely).
  • Class D: Fires in combustible metals such as magnesium, titanium, zirconium, sodium, lithium, and potassium.

5.2.2 Required Location

Fire extinguishers must be required at the following locations:

  • In building and site trailers in accordance with local legislation or building/fire codes,
  • Extinguisher will be conspicuously located where they can be readily accessed in case of fire, and/or
  • Extinguishers required for general use must be located or posted nearby, not taken from locations intended for emergency service.

5.2.3 Inspection

Extinguishers will be inspected monthly to ensure they have not been actuated or tampered with and to detect damage. Extinguishers will also be subjected to an annual maintenance check and documented. Inspection tags, number for reference, and suitable for outdoor service will be installed on each extinguisher. Inspection Tags and Inspection Documents will be signed and dated after each inspection. Monthly inspections and maintenance checks are the responsibility of the site or area supervisor. Extinguishers past the inspection period must be returned to the supervisor for inspection and/or service.

5.2.4 Portable Extinguisher Inspection

  • Ensure that the extinguisher is properly mounted and located.
  • Ensure that it is visible and unobstructed.
  • Ensure that the seal is not broken.
  • Check the gauge pressure, if it is below the operating range – have it recharged.
  • Check the extinguisher for signs of physical and mechanical damage.
  • Check for excessive corrosion.
  • Check the hose and nozzle assembly for damage and signs of deterioration.
  • Check nozzle for obstruction.
  • Record inspection on the Fire Extinguisher Inspection Tag.

5.2.5 Discharged Extinguishers

Discharged fire extinguishers must be returned to the supervisor for inspection and / or service. Discharge information must be provided on the backside of the Inspection Tag, which must be completed before returning discharged fire extinguishers.

5.2.6 Recharging

Spent extinguishers must be sent back to the supplier for recharging.

5.2.7 Cleaning Extinguisher Dust

Clean the area quickly using isopropyl alchohol to remove the powder.

To neutralize the sodium bicarbonate and potassium bicarbonate, wipe with a solution of vinegar and water.

To neutralize the monoammonium phosphate, apply a solution of water and baking soda.

After this is all done clean with soap and water.

5.3 Equipment Specific Procedures for Roaster Fires

Generally, there are 3 major signs that a fire has begun inside the cast-iron drum, the afterburner, the chaff collector and/or the piping

  1. Smoke is visibly leaking out of the drum/afterburner/chaff collector/piping
  2. The roaster probes massively increase in temperature
  3. The metal piping begins to turn red

In any of these instances, the Fire Procedure should be followed immediately.

Should you have any concerns related to a roaster fire, it is critical that you notify the Head Roaster immediately, together assess the risks and severity of the situation and decide whether to take action or call in the Fire Department and evacuate. You will need to act when:

  1. If the Fire is controlled inside the Afterburner/Chaff collector
  2. If the fire is controlled inside the Drum
  3. If the fire is controlled in the piping

What to do.

If the chaff is on fire, on either roaster, turn off burner and wait for the fire to run out of fuel. Pay attention to the color of the piping to see if the fire has spread. This may require evacuation due to a high amount of smoke.

G90 Roaster

If the coffee inside the drum is on fire, turn off burner, turn off the blower, restrict the airflow fully and activate water, located on pole by afterburner, wait for the fire to go out inside the drum. This may require an evacuation due to the amount of smoke. It is critically important to not open the oven door, as this exposure to oxygen could create a sudden explosion.

G120 Roaster

If the coffee inside the drum is on fire, turn off burner, turn off the blower and activate water, this is the blue nozzle located behind the roaster. Wait for the fire to go out inside the drum. This may require an evacuation due to the amount of smoke. It is critically important to not open the oven door during a fire, as this exposure to oxygen could create a sudden explosion.

L12 Roaster

If the coffee inside the drum is on fire, turn off burner and fully restrict airflow. Wait for the fire to burn out. This may require an evacuation due to the amount of smoke. It is critically important to not open the oven door during a fire, as this exposure to oxygen could create a sudden explosion.

If the fire is in the piping, let it burn out. If the metal starts to change colour spray the outside of the piping to cool it down. DO NOT spray water into the piping.

When to Evacuate.

  1. If the fire is not going out with the above steps in a reasonable time frame or it continues to grow.
  2. If the fire ever reaches the roof.
  3. When smoke inhalation becomes a risk. (This is a very unlikely scenario as the building would most likely be evacuated before this becomes a risk)
