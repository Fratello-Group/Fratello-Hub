Purpose

To define the procedures for identifying food safety hazards and implementing preventive control measures for all incoming ingredients, packaging, and food-contact materials at Fratello Coffee Roasters. This SOP ensures compliance with the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR), specifically Sections 47(1) and 47(2), and aligns with PCP sub-elements 1.1(a), 1.1(b/c), and 1.3.

Scope

Applies to all incoming products used at Fratello, including:

  • Green coffee beans
  • Bulk loose leaf tea
  • Packaging (boxes, bags, bottles)
  • Oat milk
  • Chai concentrate
  • Chocolate sauces
  • Flavored syrups

Risk Rating Guide (Used for All Hazard Assessments)

Likelihood (L): 1 = Rare, 2 = Unlikely, 3 = Possible, 4 = Likely, 5 = Very Likely

Severity (S): 1 = Minor (No illness/injury), 2 = Low (Mild illness or minor injury), 3 = Moderate (Reversible illness or short-term impact), 4 = High (Hospitalization or major recall), 5 = Severe (Fatality or widespread outbreak)

Risk Rating = L × S

Risk Level Thresholds:

  • 1–4: Low Risk – Controlled through routine procedures
  • 5–9: Moderate Risk – Preventive control measures required
  • 10–25: High Risk – CCP or validated control required

Procedure

1. Hazard Identification (Pre-Purchase)

  • QA conducts a hazard analysis for each new ingredient or packaging item.
  • Hazards considered: Biological, Chemical, and Physical.
  • Use CFIA and Health Canada sources to inform known hazards (e.g., OTA in coffee, pesticides in tea).

2. Supplier Approval & Specifications

  • Ensure suppliers are on the approved list.
  • Request:
    • COAs (e.g. for pathogens, mycotoxins)
    • Allergen declarations
    • Letters of Guarantee (e.g. food-grade packaging compliance)
    • Product spec sheets (e.g. acidity of chai concentrate)

3. Incoming Receiving Checks

Performed by Receiving Personnel using SOPs 2.3, 2.4, 5.2, 5.3, and 5.5:

  • Truck Inspection: Clean, odor-free, pest-free (Vehicle Conveyance SOP)
  • Package Check: No damage, seals intact, correct labels/lot codes
  • Temperature: Record for perishable items (e.g. oat milk ≤ 4°C)
  • Organoleptic: Check for mold, odors, leaks
  • Documentation: Complete Receiving Record and flag deviations

4. Hazard Evaluation and Risk Rating

  • QA scores each hazard using the Risk Rating Guide.
  • Document results in Form 11.1a – HIRA Table.
  • Flag any high-risk items (e.g. chai concentrate botulism risk if pH is unverified).

5. Control Measure Implementation

  • Low Risk (1–4): Routine GMPs (e.g., visual inspection)
  • Moderate Risk (5–9): Preventive Controls (e.g., COA verification, storage SOPs)
  • High Risk (10–25): CCP or validated control required (e.g., roasting coffee for pathogen kill)

Examples:

  • Coffee roasting = CCP for biological hazard
  • Tea pesticide residues = preventive control via testing/COA
  • Glass packaging = physical hazard managed through training & handling SOP

6. CCP Management (if applicable)

For steps like coffee roasting:

  • Monitor temperature/time of each batch
  • Maintain roast logs
  • Calibrate thermocouples
  • Establish corrective actions (e.g., re-roast or dispose under-roasted coffee)

7. Documentation

Maintain the following:

  • Form 11.1a – Hazard Identification & Risk Assessment
  • Supplier specs, allergen declarations, COAs
  • Incoming material logs
  • Hold/Corrective Action logs
  • CCP logs (if applicable)

8. Training

  • Ensure all relevant staff are trained on:
    • Recognizing hazards
    • Preventive control steps
    • Allergen and cross-contact risks
  • Maintain training logs

9. Review & Verification

  • Annual review or when:
    • A new product/supplier is introduced
    • A food safety incident or CFIA finding occurs
  • QA reviews monitoring records, complaints, and lab data
  • Document review findings and revise this SOP if needed
