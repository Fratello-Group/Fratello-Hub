Purpose

To ensure that raw green beans are loaded into the roaster and roasted correctly according to customer and QA specifications while also maintaining traceability and inventory of roasted products.

Responsibility

Head Roaster, Quality Assurance and Coffee Roasters

Frequency

Daily (when in operation)

Important Notes

  • The G120 is used for roasting 200lbs batch size of coffee beans
  • Empty the chaff from the chaff collector after every 2 roast cycles

Preliminary Roasting Procedure

  • The first employee to arrive at the facility will:
    • Turn on switches which control the lights in the roasting area.
    • Turn on dust vacuum which is located on the wall behind the Silos.
    • Turn on the compressor located in the compressor room located at the rear of the facility beside the service room. The green button turns the machine on.
  • The order for roasting coffee beans is as follows:
    • Decaffeinated coffee
    • Dark roast coffee
    • Medium roast coffee
    • Light roast coffee

Note: If there is any order for portion pack ground coffee, this will be expedited after the decaffeinated roast has been completed.

Starting and Preheating the Roaster

  • Make sure the screen basket is clean and attach it underneath the destoner.
  • Power on the laptop attached to the G-120 and launch the 'Cropster Roasting Intelligence' Programme.
  • Check the temperature readings displayed on the screen (bean temp and env. temp).
    • Note: If the temperature readings are displayed, wait 2 minutes. If it shows up, it means it is properly connected. If however it does not show up it means the thermometer is disconnected; then simply adjust the USB connection cable attached to the laptop.
  • Using the BEAN ROASTER CONTROL PANEL:
    • Turn the main power switch on by turning clockwise
    • Put the 'CONTROL' knob to the 'ON' position by turning clockwise and then
    • Press the 'DRUM START' button
    • Ensure the 'DRUM RUN' light is on (this should be green)
  • Visually inspect that the drum of the roaster is rolling. There should be a gap between the drum and the body of the roaster.
    • Note a): If there is no gap, the drum seizes and stops rolling. If this happens, adjust the shaft that runs through the centre of the drum using drum wrench.
    • Note b): If the drum is still not rolling and there is a gap, open and visually inspect the electrical for abnormalities e.g., black buttons are in the downward position when the drum start is on. If there are any abnormalities observed, then inform a Supervisor to contact the maintenance company.
  • Turn on the cooling tray motor which is located at the back of the G-120 control panel.
  • Make sure the air-flow lever is pointed down so that air is flowing through the cooling tray.
  • Using clean hands, feel for air flow in the cooling tray.
    • Caution: The stir arms should be static and not moving. Do not put hands in the cooling tray if the stir arms are in motion.
  • From the control panel, put the 'MAIN BURNER' knob to the 'ON' position by turning clockwise.
  • Close the oven manually by pushing the oven lever to the downward position.
  • Check that the 'MAIN BURNER ON' light is turned on and shows an orange colour. This could take a few seconds to come on.
  • For the 'MAIN TEMP. CONTROL' use the '+ and -' buttons and make the display panel show 215. This indicates that the secondary burner is on.
    • Note: Press the '+ or -' button once, the set temperature will be raised or lowered by 1°C.
    • Hold one button, '+ or -', down and tap the other button, '+ or -', once. The set temperature will be raised or lowered by 10°C.
    • Hold one button, '+ or -', down and tap the other button, '+ or -', twice. The set temperature will be raised or lowered by 100°C.
  • From the control panel, put the 'AUX BURNER' knob to the 'ON' position by turning clockwise.
  • This preheats the oven until the pre-heat temperature of 225° - 230°C is attained.
  • Once the temperature is between 225 - 230°C, from the control panel, put the 'AUX BURNER' knob to the 'OFF' position by turning counter-clockwise and set the secondary burners to 100°C.
  • At this point, only the 'MAIN BURNER' remains on until ready to roast.
    • Note: If the temperature exceeds 230°C, you can turn off the 'MAIN BURNER' for a short while and then back on to keep the temperature between 215 - 228°C.
  • Leave the roaster to heat up. This typically takes about 55 minutes for dark and medium roasts, and an hour and a half for light roasts.

Important Note: Must periodically check the temperature during pre-heating.

  • Repeat the visual inspections on the drum by opening the oven. Check the gap and ensure that there is no gap larger than a green bean between the drum and the body of the roaster.

Loading the Green Beans

  • Based on the roasting schedule, identify the associated silos from which the green beans will be taken.
  • On Cropster, select the Roast Profile required.
  • Visually check the roaster hopper to make sure it is emptied.
  • Using the green bean cart, open the sac and inspect for any left-over green beans. If any is observed, ensure this is emptied out prior to weighing activities.
  • Turn on the scale of the green bean cart and 'TARE' to ensure it reads 0.
    • Note: If the scale is not turned on, check the battery underneath the cart. Make sure the battery switch is positioned on. Then check the 'low battery' light. If the light is on it means the battery needs to be charged.
  • Carefully move and place the cart into position under the Silos from where the green beans will be collected.
  • Once in position, slide back the latch of the silo compartment; this dispenses the green beans into the weighing cart.
    • Note: When weighing, it is important that the precise weight needed for the roast is weighed. If more is dispensed, then the green beans should be manually scooped out into the dedicated blue bins and labeled.
  • Once the green beans needed have been weighed, transfer the cart to the vacuum conveyor making sure the cart chute is properly aligned.
  • Check that the suction switch is turned to the G-120 mark indicated on the suction pipes.
    • Note: If the switch is turned to the G-90 mark, go to the G-90 control screen, and press the '1' button to set the flow to the G-120.
  • Put on protective earmuffs. This is very important prior to turning on the vacuum conveyor.
  • Turn on the black button which activates the air flow of the vacuum.
  • To load the green beans into the roaster, use the lever on the weighing cart and pull up to open the gate. This allows the green beans to flow into the vacuum conveyor. Take care to ensure that this is released slowly to avoid the beans overflowing on the surface area. If this happens, close the cart gate to allow the green beans in the vacuum conveyor to flow through the chute.
  • Ensure the green bean cart is emptied and return cart to charging station located at the midpoint of all silos. Visually check the inside of the vacuum that the beans are not left over.
  • Turn off the vacuum using the red button on the main switch.

Roasting Activities

  • Once the green beans have been loaded and pre-heat time is completed, roasting is ready to begin.
  • Double check that the correct roast profile has been selected.
  • Turn on all 3 burners from the control panel and carefully follow the roast profile noting to drop the beans slightly earlier. This should be at 2.5 - 3°C lower than the charge bean temperature. This ensures that the roasting process begins at the correct temperature.
    • Note a): If this is a first roast, the main burner should already be on. Set the secondary burner to 215 (as per the preheating step), then turn on 'AUX BURNER'.
    • Note b): For subsequent roasts, extra measures will be taken to ensure the temperature remains between 210 - 230°C range.
  • Raise the Hopper Lever upwards to dump the green beans from the hopper. The roasting cycle begins at this point.
    • Note: During the dumping process, use the rubber mallet to gently hit the sides of the roaster. This ensures that all the beans get into the roaster.
  • Follow the reference graph and try to ensure that the procedures follow and match the reference graph as closely as possible.
  • Periodically, using the tryer, take a small portion from the roast beans and visually inspect the roast beans for color and roasting reaction, also ensuring to smell the product until roasting is completed.
  • Before launching the stir arms, check that the cooling tray is clear and free of any debris.
  • One minute from roast completion, press the 'STIR ARM START' button (located on the control panel). This activates the stir arm on the cooling tray in a continuous clockwise motion.
  • When the roast is determined as completed by the coffee roaster, manually pull out the oven lever on the roaster, raising the oven door, to offload the roasted beans into the cooling tray.

Important Note: After offloading the roasted beans, check for the gap in the drum and ensure it is not too wide. This gap check must be performed after every single roast.

  • When the roasted beans are offloaded into the cooling tray, it takes at least 5-10 minutes for it to cool down. Once the roasted beans are cool, the QA inspection is ready to be performed.

Caution: DO NOT TOUCH the roasted beans until 5 minutes after offloading as it will still be very hot.

QA Inspection Activities

  • Power on the laptop attached to the QA and launch the "Colortrack – Laser Color Analyzer" Programme.
  • From the cooling tray, use a dedicated plate to scoop out the roasted beans.
  • Transfer beans into the grinder which is located on the QA inspection rack.
  • Grind a little of the roasted beans to purge left over grinds from the grinder. Discard this first grind and then proceed to grind the remaining roasted beans for inspection.
  • Grind the remaining roasted beans into the plate and level using the dedicated ruler.
  • Gently swipe the bottom of the plate and place it into the Colortrack to read the colour value.
    • Caution: Do not touch the laser equipment by hands or the plate.
  • Set the timer knob by turning clockwise to '45 seconds' and then press to start analyzing.
  • Once analyzed, enter the 'Average' information into 'Roast Value' on Cropster.

Transfer and Weighing the Roasted Beans

  • Make sure the air-flow lever is pointed up. This re-directs air flow from the cooling tray to the destoner.
  • Turn the lever on the cooling tray into a downward position. This opens the cooling tray and roasted beans are sucked up through the destoner.
    • Note: During the transfer of beans to the destoner, ensure the beans are floating well to allow for an even flow. If the beans are not floating well or stop inside the pipe, close the gate of the destoner immediately.
  • After loading, ensure the cooling tray is clear, pull the lever on the cooling tray upward.
  • Press the 'STIR ARM STOP' button to turn the stir arm off (located on the control panel).
  • Carefully place a clean coffee cart on the scale. Tare out the scale to 0.
  • Adjust the airflow lever into the downward position.
  • Pull open the bean hopper pully. This offloads the beans from the hopper into the coffee cart.
  • Use the rubber mallet to gently hit the sides to knock out stuck beans. Adjust the air flow using the lever to help knock loose beans that are stuck. Repeat it until the bean hopper is empty.
  • Weigh the product.
  • Once products have been weighed, write down the weight and then tare the scale.
  • Pull out the cart from the scale carefully; using the cart weight on the side, double check the accurate weight.
  • Enter the weight on Cropster.
  • Enter product details on the coffee tag label and put the tag on the cart.
    • Note: The roasted coffee's lot number is Cropster's ID-tag number.
  • Place cart in the Work In Progress (WIP) Storage area.

Record Completion and Build Assembly Activities

On the laptop:

  • Open Odoo.
  • Insert username and password provided.
  • Go to the Manufacturing page.
  • Click 'NEW' at the top right to create the new sheet.
  • Type in the Build Code or Product Name in the 'Product' column.
  • Type in the quantity in the 'Quantity' column; this auto-populates the information. Please ensure that you record the actual quantity in the 'To Consume' column.
  • Click the 'Save Manually' button and confirm the page.
  • Type in the quantity in the 'Quantity' column again and type in the 'Cropster ID-tag (PR-code)' in the 'Lot/Serial Number' column. This 'Lot Number' is the Roasted Bean's lot number.
  • Click the icon after the 'Lot/Serial Numbers' on the sheet. Ensure the lot number is below the 'Pick From' column, as well as the quantity. If there is an additional line, please delete it and save.
  • Repeat the previous step for each line all the way down.
  • Ensure that the check mark is in the 'Consumed' column.
  • Click the 'Save Manually' button and click 'Produce All' to build.
  • Confirm the page to finish.
  • Complete the 'Daily Roast Tracking Sheet' with the required information i.e., Item Code, roasted weight, PR. Number and Assembly number.
  • Record the 'Build Ref No' from the Build Assembly page into the 'Assembly' column on the Daily Roast Tracking Sheet.
  • Sign the 'Completed' column on the record.

Important Note: Do not sign under the 'Completed' column until all other information has been written into their respective column.

Turning off the G120 Roaster

  • When the Daily Roasting Schedule has been completed, turn off all three burners from the control panel.
  • Ensure the cooling tray is clear and using the oven lever, manually open the oven.
  • Start the timer on the laptop. Once it reaches 30 minutes, stop the afterburner on the control panel. Reset the timer.
  • Wait until the 'HIGH LIMIT CONTROL PANEL' displays 50°C or less.
  • When the timer reaches 15 minutes, press the 'DRUM STOP' button on the control panel.
  • Visually confirm that the drum in the roaster has stopped rolling.
  • Once drum has stopped, put the 'CONTROL' knob to the 'OFF' position by turning counterclockwise.
  • Put the main power switch to the 'OFF' position by turning anticlockwise.
  • The roaster is completely turned off at this point.
  • Close the Cropster programme and shut down the computer.

Deviation and Corrective Actions

Deviation and corrective actions to be taken if any of the above is not as described: Employees will be retrained on procedures as required.
