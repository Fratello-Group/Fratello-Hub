Purpose

To ensure equipment, infrastructure, and tools used in food production are maintained in a clean, functional condition that prevents contamination and supports food safety. This SOP fulfills CFIA Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR) Part 4 requirements.

Scope

Applies to all:

  • Roasting, grinding, packaging, brewing, and green coffee handling equipment
  • Food-contact and hygiene infrastructure (hand sinks, sanitizer stations, mop room)
  • Warehouse, dock, pest barriers, HVAC systems
  • External third-party maintenance providers

Responsibility

Role Responsibility
Maintenance Supervisor Oversees PM program, assigns tasks, verifies records
Designated Operators Complete scheduled tasks and visual checks
QA or Director of Operations Spot checks, audit PM logs, verify third-party records
Contractors (e.g., Orkin, Cintas) Perform scheduled services and provide documentation

PM Worksheet Reference

The Fratello PM Worksheet.xlsx contains:

  • Manual: Task instructions by area
  • Daily: Routine cleaning, sanitation checks
  • Weekly-Monthly: Systematic maintenance tasks
  • Quarterly-Annual: Deep cleaning, filter replacements, calibrations
  • Third Party: Pest control, chemical service, HVAC, fire equipment

Preventive Maintenance Procedures

1. Daily Sanitation & Visual Checks

  • Follow Sanitation SOP Procedures A–C
  • Inspect hand sinks, sanitizer stations, mop room (see Pre-Op Inspection SOP)
  • Complete entries in Daily PM tab with initials and date

2. Weekly and Monthly Maintenance

  • Clean silos, dust fans, inspect exhaust ducts and grates
  • Grinder cleaning and calibration checks (cross-ref: Calibration SOP)
  • Documented in Weekly–Monthly tab with signature and date

3. Quarterly and Annual Maintenance

  • Roaster greasing, exhaust and cyclone cleaning, QA table cleaning
  • Water filter replacement and pest-proofing infrastructure
  • Calibrations (e.g., thermometers, moisture analyzers)
  • Logged in Quarterly–Annual tab with equipment ID, date, initials

4. Third-Party Maintenance

  • Pest control (Orkin) – see Pest Control SOP
  • Chemical replenishment (Cintas) – see Sanitation SOP
  • Fire suppression, HVAC service – see Third Party tab
  • Certificates stored in shared maintenance folder and tracked monthly

Maintenance GMPs & Contamination Prevention

  • Tools must be clean, wiped before use, and carried in toolboxes or pouches (not pockets)
  • Never place tools on food contact surfaces or floors
  • Remove or cover exposed food/packaging before maintenance begins
  • Inspect area after maintenance:
    • Tools removed
    • No excess grease/oil
    • Equipment cleaned/sanitized if food contact
  • Perform maintenance outside production hours where possible

Utility Knife Policy

  • Only one-piece replacement blade knives allowed
  • No snap-off or perforated blades
  • Broken or missing parts must be reported and investigated immediately

Temporary Repairs

  • Prohibited: duct tape, cardboard, string
  • Acceptable: black zip ties (temporary use only)
  • All temporary repairs must be:
    • Tracked in Repair and Maintenance Log
    • Monitored via Interior Premises Inspections
    • Replaced with permanent fixes ASAP

Lubricants

  • Only food-grade, incidental contact-approved lubricants allowed near food zones
  • Daily lubrication with mineral oil does not require a record
  • Store lubricants away from food and clearly labeled

Corrective Actions

Issue Action
Missed task Complete ASAP, log reason in PM sheet
Equipment damaged/out of spec Tag OUT OF SERVICE, initiate Maintenance Request
Maintenance zone affects hygiene Deep clean area, escalate if contamination possible
Temporary repair overdue Escalate to Supervisor, complete permanent fix
Product contamination suspected Trigger Hold Product SOP and complete Corrective Action Record
