Purpose

This SOP establishes the process for creating, reviewing, approving, updating, distributing, and archiving all documents within Fratello Coffee Roasters' Preventive Control Plan (PCP), as required under the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR) Part 4 (Preventive Controls) and Part 5 (Traceability).

Scope

Applies to all controlled documents relevant to food safety, traceability, and operational quality, including but not limited to:

  • Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
  • Policies and checklists
  • Logs, forms, and verification records
  • Training documents
  • External reference documents (CFIA, supplier specs)

Responsibilities

Role Responsibility
QA Coordinator Maintains master document list, issues new versions, archives obsolete SOPs
SOP Author (Dept Lead) Drafts or updates applicable documents
Director of Operations Final approval of all PCP-related SOPs
All Staff Use only the most current, approved versions of documents provided to their work area

Document Creation and Approval

  • All SOPs and forms must follow Fratello's standard template format.
  • Each document is assigned a unique SOP number (e.g., SOP-PCP-3.2).
  • The author must submit the draft to QA for review.
  • Final approval is granted by the Director of Operations.
  • Approved documents are saved in:
    • Digital folder: QMS > PCP Master Binder > [Section #]
    • Physical binder: Printed and inserted in the correct section

Version Control

  • All documents include:
    • SOP Number
    • Version Number (e.g., v1.0, v2.1)
    • Effective Date
    • Approval Signatures
  • Only one version may be marked "Active" at a time.
  • Older versions are archived digitally with "Superseded" marked on the document.

Review and Revision

Type Frequency
Routine Review Annually, during the Internal Audit (per SOP 1.3)
Change Triggered Immediately after process, regulation, or equipment changes
Minor Edits (e.g., formatting) As needed, with version update noted in revision log

Distribution and Access

  • Current versions only are:
    • Posted in workstations, loading docks, cupping labs, roasting bays, and shipping areas
    • Shared in read-only format via company SharePoint or server folder
  • Obsolete versions are removed from all access points and labeled "Archived – Do Not Use."
