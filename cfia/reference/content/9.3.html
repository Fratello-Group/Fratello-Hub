1. Purpose

To ensure all customer complaints are documented, investigated, and resolved promptly and effectively, in compliance with SFCR Section 86 and as part of Fratello's Preventive Control Plan (PCP). This process also supports product safety, quality assurance, and continuous improvement.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all customer complaints received regarding:

  • Coffee or non-coffee consumables
  • Product quality (e.g., taste, aroma, freshness)
  • Food safety concerns (e.g., foreign materials, illness/injury)
  • Packaging or labeling issues

3. Responsibilities

RoleResponsibility
First Responder (e.g., Order Desk, Sales, Customer Service)Completes Sections 1–2 of the Customer Complaint Form; forwards case to appropriate designate.
Head RoasterInvestigates and resolves complaints related to coffee quality or safety.
Warehouse ManagerInvestigates non-coffee consumable complaints.
Director of OperationsReviews complaint files for completeness; approves final closure; ensures CFIA is contacted for reportable food safety issues.
ControllerPerforms monthly trend analysis of complaints.

4. Procedure

4.1 Complaint Intake

  • First Responder completes Section 1 and 2 of the Customer Complaint Form:
    • Contact info, product info (LOT, SKU), complaint description
    • Flags food safety risk, injury, illness, or foreign material (checkboxes)
  • Assign complaint to:
    • Head Roaster (coffee)
    • Warehouse Manager (non-coffee)

4.2 Investigation and Risk Assessment

  • Designate completes Section 3 of the Complaint Form:
    • Confirms whether complaint is food safety, quality, or other
    • Checks LOT history (Cropster, inventory logs)
    • Determines if hold or recall is required
    • Describes corrective and preventative actions
  • If illness, injury, or potential recall:
    • Notify the Director of Operations immediately
    • Contact CFIA per Recall SOP if required
    • Isolate affected LOT(s) using Hold Procedure; record Hold # on form

4.3 Resolution and Closure

  • Communicate findings to customer within 48 hours, where feasible
  • Final closure includes:
    • Signature of Designate
    • Product disposition and quantity
    • Complaint logged in Customer Complaint Log
  • Complaint is reviewed and signed off by Director of Operations

4.4 Corrective Action Verification

  • Effectiveness of actions is verified by:
    • Monitoring repeat issues
    • Observing employee practices
    • Reviewing quality control trends

4.5 Monthly Review & Trend Analysis

  • The Controller or Director of Operations performs monthly review of:
    • Complaint types and frequency
    • Root causes
    • Effectiveness of preventative measures
  • Findings are shared with production and QA for continuous improvement

5. Corrective Actions
