Purpose

To ensure the facility and equipment are maintained in a clean and sanitary condition to prevent contamination of food products, packaging, and ingredients. This procedure ensures compliance with CFIA SFCR Part 4, particularly sections addressing biological, chemical, and physical hazard prevention.

Scope

Applies to:

  • All food-contact and non-food contact surfaces
  • Production, packaging, warehouse, and support areas
  • Administrative offices and washrooms (via contractor)
  • Sanitation tools and storage areas

Responsibility

Frequency

  • As per the Master Sanitation Schedule (MSS)
  • Bi-weekly Cintas chemical replenishment and support
  • Daily/weekly/yearly cleaning records are maintained

Chemical Name Use

Chemicals are stored in a locked cabinet, labeled clearly, and only handled by trained staff. Any new chemical must be approved before use and added to the Chemical List.

Sanitation Procedures

Procedure A – Dry Cleaning (Routine Daily Tasks)

  • Power down equipment before cleaning.
  • Remove debris (beans, paper, plastic, etc.).
  • Brush or vacuum coffee residue.
  • Wipe with clean rag if needed.
  • Sweep floor debris into designated bins.

Procedure B – Manual/Hand Cleaning

  • Option 1 – Full Wash Method (low moisture zones):
    • Perform dry clean first.
    • Wash with water and detergent.
    • Rinse with potable water.
    • Apply Cintas no-rinse sanitizer (min. 2-minute contact).
  • Option 2 – Sensitive Surfaces (e.g., electronics):
    • Use approved cleaning wipes.
    • Leave surface wet for required contact time.
    • Inspect for cleanliness before restarting equipment.

Procedure C – Three-Compartment Sink Cleaning

Used for utensils, tools, removable parts:

  • Clean → Rinse → Sanitize (2 min contact) → Air Dry
  • Follow chemical dilution as per Cintas instructions
  • Dry on approved racks

Equipment Disassembly and Maintenance Cleaning

  • Equipment requiring disassembly is outlined in the MSS
  • After any maintenance, affected food-contact areas must be sanitized before production resumes
  • Sanitation is documented in the Maintenance Log

Tool Cleaning

  • Brushes, squeegees, and sanitation tools must be cleaned and sanitized after use using the three-step method
  • Tools are stored in designated sanitary locations

General Housekeeping Guidelines

  • Clean-as-you-go is mandatory
  • Equipment and parts must not be stored on or near product zones
  • Packaging and ingredients are stored in their designated, clean locations
  • Spills are cleaned at breaks/lunch; fallen products are wiped before use

Verification

  • Verified daily via Pre-Operational Inspection Procedure
  • Dirty or improperly sanitized equipment triggers corrective action
  • Periodic review of sanitation effectiveness is conducted by the Maintenance Supervisor

Corrective Actions

Issue Response
Improper chemical concentration Re-mix and re-sanitize affected equipment
Persistent residue post-cleaning Observe procedures, re-train if needed, consult with Cintas rep
Repeated sanitation failures Reassess cleaning methods, increase frequency, modify procedures
Contaminated product Refer to Deviation Procedure and discard affected items
