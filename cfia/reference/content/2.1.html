1.0 Purpose

To ensure that all materials and services sourced by Fratello Coffee Roasters meet food safety, quality, and regulatory requirements as outlined by the Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR). This program applies to all suppliers of:

  • Raw materials (green beans, ingredients)
  • Primary and secondary packaging materials
  • Non-food chemicals and services (e.g., pest control, sanitation)
  • Third-party contractors and service providers

2.0 Scope

This policy applies to all materials received at Fratello's facilities, whether used in production, packaging, maintenance, or sanitation. It governs:

  • Supplier selection and onboarding
  • Risk assessments
  • Ongoing monitoring and verification
  • Documentation and traceability
  • Exceptions and emergency sourcing

4.0 Supplier Approval & Onboarding

4.1 Minimum Documentation Requirements

Raw Materials & Ingredients

  • Letter of Guarantee (LOG)
  • Specifications (physical, chemical, microbiological)
  • Allergen declaration
  • Certificate of Analysis (CoA)
  • HACCP certification or third-party audit
  • GMO, Organic, Kosher certificates (if applicable)
  • Diligence agreement
  • Country of Origin
  • CCP declaration

Primary Packaging

  • LOG
  • Technical data/spec sheet
  • CFIA or Health Canada letter of no objection (for direct food contact)
  • Allergen declaration
  • 3rd-party audit (if applicable)

Secondary Packaging

  • LOG on company letterhead
  • Specification sheet
  • Allergen declaration

Chemicals & Non-Food Inputs

  • SDS
  • LOG
  • Specification sheet
  • CFIA or Health Canada approval documentation
  • Safe use instructions

Contract Services (e.g., pest control, sanitation)

  • Signed service agreement
  • Food safety/GMP compliance documentation
  • Contractor sign-in & verification at each visit

5.0 Supplier Risk Assessment

Conducted by QA prior to approval and reviewed annually. Evaluation includes:

  • Allergen risk
  • Foreign material risk
  • Microbiological contamination
  • Chemical contamination
  • Substitution or fraud potential

Risk Levels

  • High Risk: GFSI certification or Fratello audit required
  • Medium Risk: Third-party audit or extensive documentation
  • Low Risk: Supplier questionnaire + documentation

6.0 Monitoring & Verification

  • Suppliers are reviewed annually or upon a significant change.
  • CoAs must accompany each lot (where applicable).
  • Spot audits or evaluations may be triggered by product issues or changes.
  • Low-risk supplier questionnaires are renewed every 3 years.

7.0 Receiving & Traceability Controls

  • All materials received are matched against the Approved Supplier List.
  • QA or Receiving staff ensure incoming goods are:
    • Correctly labelled
    • Free of damage/contamination
    • From approved vendors only
  • Non-approved deliveries may only be accepted in emergency, must be logged in PR-B-1117, and subject to QA review.

9.0 Review and Updates

This policy and the Approved Supplier List shall be reviewed at least once annually or whenever:

  • A new supplier is added
  • A product complaint or incident arises
  • CFIA regulations are updated

10.0 CFIA / SFCR Compliance Notes

  • SFCR Part 4, s. 47: Requires preventive controls for incoming materials to prevent hazards.
  • SFCR Part 5, s. 90: Requires traceability of ingredients and packaging.
  • SFCR Part 6, s. 89: Supports oversight of service providers and recordkeeping.
