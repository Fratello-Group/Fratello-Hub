Purpose

To prevent contamination and ensure traceability, this SOP outlines inspection and handling procedures for both incoming and outgoing goods. These procedures align with SFCR Parts 4 and 5 by identifying preventive controls for shipping and receiving.

Scope

Applicable to all staff receiving and shipping raw materials, packaging, chemicals, finished goods, or miscellaneous supplies.

Responsibility

  • Receiving: Trained receiving personnel
  • Shipping: Trained shipping personnel

Frequency

  • Every time a product is received or shipped.

Receiving Procedure

Step Key Actions Food Safety Considerations
1 Confirm driver remains in designated receiving area. Prevents unauthorized access.
2 Verify paperwork: PO, BOL, Approved Supplier, Lot #. Traceability, procurement compliance.
3 Inspect vehicle: cleanliness, no pests, damage, odours, incompatible cargo. SFCR s.72 – Prevents contamination.
4 Inspect goods: packaging intact, labelled, clean, no contamination or damage. Prevents physical, chemical, and biological hazards.
5 Record findings on Receiving/Shipping Log. Include seal if applicable. Required for traceability and verification.
6 Tag incoming food products with Pallet Tags. Enables internal traceability and FIFO.
7 Store products in designated areas based on product type. Segregates allergens and chemicals.

Shipping Procedure

Step Key Actions Food Safety Considerations
1 Ensure customer or contract driver remains in shipping area. Prevents facility contamination.
2 Inspect truck for cleanliness, condition, no pests or contaminants. Required per SFCR s.72–73.
3 Verify compatibility of cargo and cleanliness of trailer. Prevents cross-contamination.
4 Inspect outgoing products: sealed, clean, labelled, correct lot. Prevents incorrect shipments and maintains traceability.
5 Load product carefully to avoid damage or crushing. Prevents physical hazard and maintains packaging integrity.
6 Record shipment on Receiving/Shipping Log, including lot # and seal. Enables recall and tracking per SFCR Part 5.

Deviations and Corrective Actions

Issue Corrective Action
Truck dirty or damaged Reject load or require cleaning; notify supplier. Record action.
Product compromised Place on Hold; refer to Damaged or Suspect Product Procedure.
Improper loading Reload and retrain staff. Document issue.
Receiving without proper paperwork Place materials on Hold until verified.
Returned goods Follow "Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product" Procedure.

Verification Activities

  • Conducted during verification procedure audits.
  • Director of Production or designate to review records monthly.

Relevant Records

  • Receiving/Shipping Log
  • Hold Log
  • Corrective Action Record
  • Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product Procedure
