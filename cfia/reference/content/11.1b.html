1.0 Purpose

To ensure that warehouse and receiving team members are trained and competent in inspecting, documenting, and escalating issues related to the delivery and transport of all incoming raw materials in accordance with CFIA Preventive Control Plan (PCP) requirements.

2.0 Scope

This SOP applies to all staff who handle the receiving of green coffee, tea, packaging, or other raw materials. It ensures compliance with SOPs 5.2, 5.3, 5.5, and 11.1, as well as SFCR sections 47 and 89.

3.0 Responsibilities

  • Warehouse/Receiving Staff: Perform inspections, document results, flag deviations.
  • QA Coordinator: Deliver training, verify records, and oversee deviation handling.
  • Supervisors: Ensure team members are trained and compliant.

4.0 Training Content

Training must include the following components:

Daily Inspection Requirements

  • Visual inspection of transport vehicle for sanitation, pest risks, debris
  • Check package condition: rips, water damage, mold, pest activity
  • Labeling match: lot #, product name, PO match
  • Physical inspection of materials (e.g., green coffee for mold, foreign matter)

Documentation

  • How to complete the "Incoming Material Receiving & Transport Log"
  • Recording any non-conformance and assigning initial corrective actions

Deviation Handling

  • Immediate flag to QA
  • Use of Hold Tag and entry into Hold Product Form (9.4)
  • Documentation in Corrective Action Log (Section 9)

Verification Requirements

  • Cross-referencing material with supplier information or COAs when applicable
  • Identifying when COAs are required based on HIRA (e.g., OTA, pesticides)

5.0 Frequency of Training

  • Upon hiring (initial onboarding)
  • Annually for all receiving staff
  • When SOP 5.2, 5.3, 5.5, 11.0, or 11.1 are updated

6.0 Records and Documentation

  • Training records must be documented in the Personnel Training Schedule (8.3)
  • Signed acknowledgment by trainees filed in the Training Binder (Section 8)
