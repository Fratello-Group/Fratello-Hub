Purpose

To document and control the handling, assessment, and disposition of any product identified as defective, contaminated, or returned. This SOP supports compliance with Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR) Part 4, ensuring affected product does not enter the food supply chain without evaluation.

Scope

Applies to all food products, packaging materials, or ingredients that are:

  • Returned by customers or transporters
  • Identified internally as damaged, non-conforming, or suspect
  • Exposed to potential contamination due to sanitation, equipment failure, pests, or physical hazards

Responsibility

RoleResponsibility
Warehouse ManagerInitiates product hold and inspection
QA or DesignateAssesses safety and quality
Director of OperationsApproves final product disposition
All EmployeesMust report suspected product or contamination events immediately

Frequency

  • As required whenever defective, returned, or suspect product is identified

Procedure

1. Product Identification and Initial Hold

  • Immediately label affected product with HOLD stickers
  • Complete the top section of Form 3.2 – Defective, Suspect, or Returned Product:
    • Date, Lot Number, Inspector Name
    • Type of Product, Packaging format
    • Hold sticker applied (Yes/No)

2. Returned Products

If returned by a customer or distributor:

  • Record transport company, vehicle identifier, bill of lading, and client name
  • Note reason for return (e.g. wrong product, temperature breach, visible damage)

3. Safety and Quality Assessment

  • QA or trained designate inspects product:
    • Physical condition (damage, pest exposure, contamination)
    • Packaging integrity
    • Traceability and temperature compliance
  • Document recommendation on Form 3.2:
    • Safety Issue – Dispose
    • Quality Issue – Rework or Donate

4. Product Disposition

Record final action in Form 3.2:

  • Disposed: amount, method, date
  • Donated: amount, organization, event
  • Reworked: new product name, lot code, percent of rework added

All product dispositions must be signed off by the Warehouse Manager or QA, and retained for audit.

5. Investigation (if required)

If contamination is suspected, trigger the following SOPs:

  • Returned, Damaged or Suspect Product Procedure
  • Hold Product Procedure
  • Deviation Procedure
  • Pest Control SOP (if applicable)
  • Glass & Brittle Plastic SOP (if applicable)

Evaluate whether additional product must be placed on hold or recalled.

Corrective Actions

DeviationAction
Product not labeled or isolatedApply HOLD sticker, move to designated hold area
Contaminated product not disposedEscalate to Director of Operations, dispose immediately
Disposition not documentedComplete Form 3.2 and file with QA
Repeat returns or product complaintsConduct trend analysis and supplier or process review

Verification

  • Records reviewed monthly by QA or designate
  • Dispositions and trends analyzed quarterly as part of internal audit
